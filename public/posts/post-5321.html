Respect and Honor Between Spouses (Romans 12:10)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Respect and Honor

Respect and honor between spouses in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Romans 12:10: "Love one another with brotherly affection. Outdo one another in showing honor." This passage shows that respect and honor are not just nice qualities but are essential expressions of love that should be practiced actively in marriage. When couples understand respect and honor between spouses, they are not just being polite but are actively showing love through respect and honor and creating an environment of dignity and mutual appreciation.

This biblical foundation shows that respect and honor between spouses is not just about being nice but is about actively outdoing one another in showing honor and treating each other with the dignity they deserve as children of God. When couples understand this respect and honor, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to show respect and honor.

Respect and Honor as Expressions of Love

Respect and honor between spouses recognizes that respect and honor are not just social niceties but are powerful expressions of love that recognize the dignity and worth of each person. Just as we are called to outdo one another in showing honor, so too are married couples called to actively show respect and honor to each other. This respect and honor is not about being formal but is about being loving and appreciative.

When couples understand respect and honor between spouses, they are not just being polite but are actively showing love through respect and honor. They are able to treat each other with dignity and respect in all situations, to speak to each other with honor and appreciation, to value each other's opinions and contributions, to show appreciation for each other's efforts and sacrifices, and to honor each other's dignity as children of God. This respect and honor becomes a powerful expression of their love.

The Daily Practice of Respect and Honor

Understanding respect and honor between spouses is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes treating each other with dignity and respect in all situations, speaking to each other with honor and appreciation, valuing each other's opinions and contributions, showing appreciation for each other's efforts and sacrifices, and honoring each other's dignity as children of God. These daily practices of respect and honor build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to treat one's spouse with dignity and respect in all situations and circumstances, being willing to speak to one's spouse with honor and appreciation even in difficult times, being willing to value one's spouse's opinions and contributions to the relationship, being willing to show appreciation for one's spouse's efforts and sacrifices, and being willing to honor one's spouse's dignity as a child of God. When couples practice respect and honor in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and dignified.

Respect and Honor and Communication

Understanding respect and honor between spouses is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate with respect and honor, they are better able to show respect and honor effectively. Good communication helps couples to express their respect and honor, to listen with dignity, and to treat each other with appreciation.

This communication for respect and honor includes being willing to communicate with respect and honor even when disagreeing, being willing to listen to one's spouse with dignity and attention, being willing to express appreciation and honor for one's spouse's contributions, being willing to speak to one's spouse with respect and kindness, and being willing to show honor and appreciation in both words and actions. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on respect and honor.

Respect and Honor and Growth

Understanding respect and honor between spouses is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice respect and honor, they are growing in virtues like dignity, appreciation, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through respect and honor includes growing in dignity as they learn to treat each other with dignity, growing in appreciation as they learn to appreciate each other more deeply, growing in love as they learn to love each other with respect and honor, growing in humility as they learn to honor each other above themselves, and growing in holiness as they learn to recognize each other's dignity as children of God. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Respectful Love

Respect and honor between spouses in Catholic marriage is a respectful love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand respect and honor between spouses, they are not just being polite but are actively showing love through respect and honor and creating an environment of dignity and mutual appreciation.

As we reflect on respect and honor between spouses, let us remember that this respect and honor is not just a human courtesy but is a divine recognition that requires God's grace. When we practice respect and honor, we are building a marriage that is based on dignity, appreciation, and the grace of God.
