Holiness Through Encouraging Vocations in the Church

2024-12-20

Introduction: Vocations as God's Call

Holiness through encouraging vocations in the Church is about recognizing that every vocation - whether to priesthood, religious life, marriage, or single life - is a call from God that He gives to specific individuals for their sanctification and for the good of the Church and the world. When we encourage vocations, we're not just helping the Church to have enough priests and religious, but participating in God's work of calling people to their specific mission and helping them to discern and respond to that call. This encouragement is not just about recruitment or filling positions, but about helping people to discover God's plan for their lives and to respond to it with generosity and love.

In today's world, many people are confused about vocations or think that they're only for very holy people or for those who have a special calling. However, the Catholic understanding is that every person has a vocation from God, and that discovering and living that vocation is the path to true happiness and holiness. When we encourage vocations, we're not just helping others, but allowing God to work through us to build up His Church and to help people find their true purpose and mission in life.

How to Encourage Vocations

Encouraging vocations involves several important practices. First, it means praying for vocations, asking God to call people to priesthood, religious life, and marriage, and to help them respond generously to that call. Second, it means being open to the possibility that God might be calling us or our children to a particular vocation, and being willing to support them in their discernment. Third, it means creating an environment in our families and parishes that supports and encourages vocations, where young people can see the beauty and joy of living according to God's plan. Fourth, it means being willing to speak about vocations when appropriate, sharing our own experience and encouraging others to consider whether God might be calling them. Fifth, it means supporting those who are discerning or living their vocations, offering them encouragement, prayer, and practical help.

Conclusion: Vocations as Mission

Encouraging vocations leads to holiness because it helps us to be more aware of God's plan for each person and to participate in His work of building up His Church. When we encourage vocations, we're not just helping the Church, but allowing God to work through us to help people discover their true purpose and mission in life. This encouragement helps us to become more grateful for our own vocation, more aware of God's plan for others, and more willing to serve Him by helping others to respond to His call.

Prayer for Vocations

'Lord Jesus, help us to encourage vocations in Your Church, recognizing that every vocation is a call from You and a path to holiness. Give us the grace to pray for vocations, to support those who are discerning, and to create an environment that encourages people to respond to Your call. May our encouragement of vocations lead us to greater holiness and help us to build up Your Church. In Your name, Amen.'
