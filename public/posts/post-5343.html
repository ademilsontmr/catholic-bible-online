The Cross as the Center of Married Life (Luke 9:23)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of the Cross in Marriage

The cross as the center of married life in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Luke 9:23: "And he said to all, 'If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me.'" This passage shows that the cross as the center of married life is not just a nice concept but is a divine calling where married couples are called to take up their cross daily and follow Christ. When couples understand the cross as the center of married life, they are not just being religious but are actively embracing the cross and making it the center of their marriage.

This biblical foundation shows that the cross as the center of married life is not just about suffering but is about actively taking up one's cross daily and following Christ in marriage. When couples understand this cross, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to embrace the cross.

The Cross as Daily Sacrifice

The cross as the center of married life recognizes that the cross is not just a symbol but is a daily reality of sacrifice and self-denial. Just as Christ calls us to take up our cross daily and follow Him, so too are married couples called to embrace the cross and make it the center of their marriage. This cross is not about being dramatic but is about being daily and sacrificial.

When couples understand the cross as the center of married life, they are not just being religious but are actively embracing the cross and making it central. They are able to take up their cross daily and follow Christ, to embrace sacrifice and self-denial in marriage, to make the cross the center of their relationship, to grow in holiness through embracing the cross, and to be examples of Christ's sacrifice to others. This cross becomes a powerful center for their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Embracing the Cross

Understanding the cross as the center of married life is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes taking up one's cross daily and following Christ, embracing sacrifice and self-denial in marriage, making the cross the center of the relationship, growing in holiness through embracing the cross, and being examples of Christ's sacrifice to others. These daily practices of embracing the cross build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to take up one's cross daily and to follow Christ in marriage, being willing to embrace sacrifice and self-denial in marriage and to put one's spouse before oneself, being willing to make the cross the center of one's relationship and to build the marriage on sacrifice, being willing to grow in holiness through embracing the cross and to become more like Christ, and being willing to be examples of Christ's sacrifice to others and to inspire them toward sacrifice. When couples practice embracing the cross in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and sacrificial.

Embracing the Cross and Communication

Understanding the cross as the center of married life is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their commitment to embracing the cross and their desire to make it central to their marriage, they are better able to embrace the cross effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their sacrifices, to support each other in embracing the cross, and to work together in making the cross central.

This communication for embracing the cross includes being willing to discuss one's commitment to embracing the cross and making it central to the marriage, being willing to discuss one's sacrifices and to support each other in embracing the cross, being willing to share one's desire to grow in holiness through embracing the cross, being willing to encourage each other in taking up one's cross daily, and being willing to discuss how to be examples of Christ's sacrifice to others. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on sacrifice and the cross.

Embracing the Cross and Growth

Understanding the cross as the center of married life is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples embrace the cross, they are growing in virtues like sacrifice, self-denial, and holiness. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through embracing the cross includes growing in sacrifice as they learn to embrace sacrifice in marriage, growing in self-denial as they learn to deny themselves for each other, growing in holiness as they learn to grow in holiness through embracing the cross, growing in following Christ as they learn to follow Christ more closely, and growing in witness as they learn to be examples of Christ's sacrifice to others. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Crucified Love

The cross as the center of married life in Catholic marriage is a crucified love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand the cross as the center of married life, they are not just being religious but are actively embracing the cross and making it the center of their marriage.

As we reflect on the cross as the center of married life, let us remember that this cross is not just a human concept but is a divine calling that requires God's grace. When we embrace the cross, we are building a marriage that is based on sacrifice, holiness, and the grace of God.
