The Origin and History of Scapulars

Catholic scapulars and their meaning have their roots in the monastic tradition of the Church. Originally, a scapular was a piece of cloth that monks wore over their shoulders as part of their religious habit. Catholic scapulars and their meaning evolved from this practical garment into a powerful sacramental devotion. When we understand Catholic scapulars and their meaning, we recognize that they are not just pieces of cloth but sacred signs that help us to grow in holiness and to receive God's grace. This historical origin shows that Catholic scapulars and their meaning are deeply rooted in the Church's spiritual tradition.
