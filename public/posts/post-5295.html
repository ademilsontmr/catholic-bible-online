The Gift of Self-Control (Titus 2:11-12)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Self-Control

The gift of self-control in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Titus 2:11-12: "For the grace of God has appeared, bringing salvation for all people, training us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright, and godly lives in the present age." This passage shows that self-control is not just a human virtue but is a gift that comes from God's grace and helps us to live godly lives. When couples practice self-control in their marriage, they are not just being disciplined but are actively cooperating with God's grace to live upright and godly lives together.

This biblical foundation shows that self-control in marriage is not just about willpower but is about allowing God's grace to train us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions. When couples practice self-control, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This self-control is not about being perfect but is about being willing to cooperate with God's grace in living godly lives.

Self-Control as a Gift of Grace

The gift of self-control in Catholic marriage recognizes that self-control is not just a human effort but is a gift that comes from God's grace. Just as God's grace trains us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions, so too does it give us the gift of self-control to live upright and godly lives. This self-control is not about being perfect but is about being willing to cooperate with God's grace.

When couples practice self-control in their marriage, they are not just being disciplined but are actively cooperating with God's grace to live upright and godly lives. They are able to control their words and actions, to resist temptations and worldly passions, to make wise decisions together, to be patient and understanding with each other, and to live in a way that honors God and each other. This self-control becomes a powerful gift that strengthens their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Self-Control

Practicing self-control is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes controlling one's words and actions, resisting temptations and worldly passions, making wise decisions together, being patient and understanding with each other, and living in a way that honors God and each other. These daily practices of self-control build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to control one's words and actions and to think before speaking or acting, being willing to resist temptations and worldly passions that could harm the relationship, being willing to make wise decisions together and to consider the consequences of one's actions, being willing to be patient and understanding with one's spouse even when it's difficult, and being willing to live in a way that honors God and one's spouse. When couples practice self-control in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and disciplined.

Self-Control and Communication

Practicing self-control is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate with self-control, they are better able to build trust and to resolve conflicts effectively. Good communication helps couples to express their thoughts and feelings in ways that are controlled and respectful.

This communication for self-control includes being willing to think before speaking and to control one's words, being willing to listen to one's spouse without interrupting or reacting impulsively, being willing to express disagreement in a controlled and respectful way, being willing to be patient and understanding in communication, and being willing to make wise decisions together through controlled and thoughtful discussion. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on self-control and respect.

Self-Control and Growth

Practicing self-control is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice self-control, they are growing in virtues like discipline, wisdom, and holiness. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through self-control includes growing in discipline as they learn to control their words and actions, growing in wisdom as they learn to make wise decisions together, growing in holiness as they learn to live godly lives, growing in patience as they learn to be patient and understanding, and growing in love as they learn to love each other with self-controlled love. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Disciplined Love

The gift of self-control in Catholic marriage is a disciplined love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples practice self-control, they are not just being disciplined but are actively cooperating with God's grace to live upright and godly lives together.

As we reflect on the gift of self-control, let us remember that this self-control is not just a human effort but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we practice self-control, we are building a marriage that is based on discipline, wisdom, and the grace of God.
