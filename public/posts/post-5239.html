Encouraging One Another Daily (Hebrews 3:13)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Daily Encouragement

The practice of encouraging one another daily in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Hebrews 3:13: "But exhort one another every day, as long as it is called 'today,' that none of you may be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin." This passage shows that daily encouragement is not just a nice thing to do but is essential for preventing the hardening of hearts and maintaining the love and unity in marriage. When couples encourage each other daily, they are not just being supportive but are actively working to strengthen their relationship and prevent the destructive effects of sin.

This biblical foundation shows that daily encouragement in marriage is not just about being positive but is about actively building up one's spouse and helping them to grow in faith and love. When couples encourage each other daily, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This encouragement is not about being perfect but is about being faithful in supporting each other's growth and well-being.

Daily Encouragement as a Spiritual Practice

Daily encouragement in Catholic marriage is a spiritual practice that helps couples to grow together in faith and love. Just as the early Christians encouraged each other daily to prevent the hardening of hearts, so too are married couples called to encourage each other daily to prevent the hardening of their relationship. This encouragement is not just about being nice but is about being a source of strength and support for one's spouse.

When couples practice daily encouragement, they are not just being supportive but are participating in God's work of building up the body of Christ. They are helping each other to grow in faith, to overcome difficulties, to persevere in love, and to remain faithful to their vocation. This encouragement becomes a powerful force for good in their marriage and in their lives.

The Daily Practice of Encouragement

Daily encouragement in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes speaking words of affirmation and appreciation, being supportive of each other's dreams and goals, being encouraging during difficult times, celebrating each other's successes, and being a source of hope and strength. These daily practices of encouragement build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of encouragement includes being willing to speak words of encouragement when one's spouse is discouraged, being willing to celebrate small victories together, being willing to support each other's efforts and endeavors, being willing to be a source of hope during difficult times, and being willing to remind each other of God's love and faithfulness. When couples practice encouragement in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Encouragement and Communication

Daily encouragement in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively with each other, they are better able to provide meaningful encouragement that addresses their spouse's specific needs and concerns. Good communication helps couples to understand what kind of encouragement is most helpful and to provide it in a way that builds up the relationship.

This communication for encouragement includes being willing to listen to one's spouse's concerns and struggles, being willing to share words of encouragement that are specific and meaningful, being willing to celebrate each other's successes and achievements, being willing to provide hope and comfort during difficult times, and being willing to remind each other of God's love and faithfulness. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building each other up in love.

Encouragement and Growth

Daily encouragement in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples encourage each other daily, they are helping each other to grow in virtues like hope, perseverance, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through encouragement includes growing in hope as they learn to encourage each other during difficult times, growing in perseverance as they learn to support each other in long-term goals, growing in love as they learn to be sources of strength for each other, growing in faith as they learn to remind each other of God's faithfulness, and growing in holiness as they learn to be instruments of God's encouragement. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Encouragement and the Mission of Marriage

Daily encouragement in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples encourage each other daily, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's encouraging love in the world.

This mission through encouragement includes being willing to encourage each other in their service to God and others, being willing to be witnesses to God's encouraging love, being willing to build a family based on encouragement and support, being willing to serve others with encouragement and hope, and being willing to be a source of strength for their children and extended family. When couples practice encouragement in these ways, they are fulfilling their mission and becoming better witnesses to God's love.

The Grace of Daily Encouragement

Daily encouragement in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to encourage each other daily and to grow in holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to encourage each other daily and to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Conclusion: A Daily Gift of Love

Daily encouragement in Catholic marriage is a gift of love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples encourage each other daily, they are not just being supportive but are participating in God's work of building up the body of Christ.

As we reflect on daily encouragement in marriage, let us remember that this encouragement is not just a human practice but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we encourage each other daily, we are building a marriage that is based on love, hope, and the grace of God.
