Holiness Through Promoting Peace and Reconciliation

2024-12-20

Introduction: Peace as Christ's Mission

Holiness through promoting peace and reconciliation is about recognizing that peace is not just the absence of conflict or a nice ideal to aspire to, but the very mission of Christ Himself and a fundamental characteristic of the kingdom of God. Jesus came to bring peace to the world, not peace as the world gives, but the deep, abiding peace that comes from reconciliation with God and with one another. When we work for peace and reconciliation, we're not just being nice or avoiding conflict, but participating in Christ's own mission and allowing Him to work through us to bring His peace to the world.

In today's world, there are many sources of conflict and division: political disagreements, cultural differences, personal conflicts, and various forms of injustice and inequality. These conflicts can seem overwhelming and impossible to resolve. However, as Christians, we're called to be peacemakers, to work for reconciliation and understanding, and to be witnesses to the truth that peace is possible through Christ's love and mercy. When we promote peace and reconciliation, we're not just helping to resolve conflicts, but allowing God to work in us and through us to bring about the transformation He desires for the world.

How to Promote Peace and Reconciliation

Promoting peace and reconciliation involves several important practices. First, it means being willing to listen to and understand different perspectives, not just seeking to win arguments or prove our point. Second, it means being willing to admit when we're wrong and to seek forgiveness and reconciliation when we've caused conflict. Third, it means working to address the root causes of conflict, whether they be injustice, misunderstanding, or simply human sin and weakness. Fourth, it means being willing to make personal sacrifices for the sake of peace, recognizing that peace often requires compromise and humility. Fifth, it means praying for peace and asking God to help us to be effective peacemakers.

Conclusion: Peace as Sanctification

Promoting peace and reconciliation leads to holiness because it helps us to become more like Christ, who came to bring peace to the world and who calls us to be peacemakers. When we work for peace, we're not just helping others, but allowing God to work in us and to make us more patient, more understanding, more humble, and more holy. This work helps us to become more aware of God's presence and more willing to allow Him to work through us to bring about His kingdom of peace and justice.

Prayer for Peace and Reconciliation

'Lord Jesus, help us to promote peace and reconciliation, recognizing that this is Your mission and Your call to us as Your disciples. Give us the grace to be peacemakers, to work for understanding and reconciliation, and to be willing to make personal sacrifices for the sake of peace. May our work for peace lead us to greater holiness and help us to be witnesses to Your love and mercy in the world. In Your name, Amen.'
