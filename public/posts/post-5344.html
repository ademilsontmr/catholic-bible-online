Growing Together in Gratitude (Colossians 3:17)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Growing in Gratitude

Growing together in gratitude in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Colossians 3:17: "And whatever you do, in word or deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him." This passage shows that growing together in gratitude is not just a nice concept but is a divine command to give thanks to God in everything. When couples understand growing together in gratitude, they are not just being thankful but are actively giving thanks to God in everything and growing together in gratitude.

This biblical foundation shows that growing together in gratitude is not just about being thankful but is about actively giving thanks to God in everything and growing together in gratitude. When couples understand this growth, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to grow in gratitude.

Gratitude as Divine Command

Growing together in gratitude recognizes that gratitude is not just a nice feeling but is a divine command to give thanks to God in everything. Just as we are commanded to do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus and give thanks to God, so too are married couples called to grow together in gratitude and to give thanks in everything. This gratitude is not about being selective but is about being comprehensive and constant.

When couples understand growing together in gratitude, they are not just being thankful but are actively giving thanks to God in everything. They are able to give thanks to God in everything they do, to grow together in gratitude and thankfulness, to express gratitude to each other and to God, to find reasons to be grateful in all circumstances, and to be examples of gratitude to others. This gratitude becomes a powerful foundation for their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Growing in Gratitude

Understanding growing together in gratitude is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes giving thanks to God in everything they do, growing together in gratitude and thankfulness, expressing gratitude to each other and to God, finding reasons to be grateful in all circumstances, and being examples of gratitude to others. These daily practices of growing in gratitude build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to give thanks to God in everything one does and to do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus, being willing to grow together in gratitude and thankfulness and to cultivate a spirit of gratitude, being willing to express gratitude to one's spouse and to God regularly, being willing to find reasons to be grateful in all circumstances and to give thanks even in difficult times, and being willing to be examples of gratitude to others and to inspire them toward thankfulness. When couples practice growing in gratitude in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and thankful.

Growing in Gratitude and Communication

Understanding growing together in gratitude is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their gratitude and their desire to grow in thankfulness, they are better able to grow in gratitude effectively. Good communication helps couples to express their gratitude, to share their thankfulness, and to encourage each other in gratitude.

This communication for growing in gratitude includes being willing to express one's gratitude to one's spouse regularly and to share one's thankfulness, being willing to discuss one's gratitude to God and to give thanks together, being willing to share reasons to be grateful and to find gratitude in all circumstances, being willing to encourage each other in growing in gratitude and thankfulness, and being willing to discuss how to be examples of gratitude to others. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on gratitude and thankfulness.

Growing in Gratitude and Growth

Understanding growing together in gratitude is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples grow together in gratitude, they are growing in virtues like thankfulness, appreciation, and joy. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through growing in gratitude includes growing in thankfulness as they learn to give thanks in everything, growing in appreciation as they learn to appreciate each other and God's gifts, growing in joy as they learn to find joy in gratitude, growing in contentment as they learn to be content with God's gifts, and growing in witness as they learn to be examples of gratitude to others. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Grateful Love

Growing together in gratitude in Catholic marriage is a grateful love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand growing together in gratitude, they are not just being thankful but are actively giving thanks to God in everything and growing together in gratitude.

As we reflect on growing together in gratitude, let us remember that this gratitude is not just a human feeling but is a divine command that requires God's grace. When we grow in gratitude, we are building a marriage that is based on thankfulness, appreciation, and the grace of God.
