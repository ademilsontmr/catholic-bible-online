The Example of Ruth and Boaz (Ruth 1:16–17)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Ruth and Boaz

The example of Ruth and Boaz in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Ruth 1:16-17: "But Ruth said, 'Do not urge me to leave you or to return from following you. For where you go I will go, and where you lodge I will lodge. Your people shall be my people, and your God my God. Where you die I will die, and there will I be buried. May the Lord do so to me and more also if anything but death parts me from you.'" This passage shows that the example of Ruth and Boaz is not just a nice story but is a powerful example of faithfulness, loyalty, and God's providence in marriage. When couples understand the example of Ruth and Boaz, they are not just being romantic but are actively learning from their example of faithfulness and God's provision.

This biblical foundation shows that the example of Ruth and Boaz is not just about romantic love but is about faithfulness, loyalty, and God's providence in bringing couples together. When couples understand this example, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to learn from their example.

Faithfulness as a Divine Example

The example of Ruth and Boaz recognizes that their story is not just a human romance but is a divine example of faithfulness and God's providence. Just as Ruth showed extraordinary faithfulness to Naomi and God provided Boaz as her husband, so too does God provide for married couples and call them to faithfulness. This example is not about being dramatic but is about being faithful and trusting in God's provision.

When couples understand the example of Ruth and Boaz, they are not just being romantic but are actively learning from their example of faithfulness. They are able to learn from Ruth's faithfulness and loyalty, to trust in God's providence for their marriage, to be faithful to each other through all circumstances, to trust in God's timing and provision, and to be examples of faithfulness to others. This example becomes a powerful lesson in faithfulness and trust.

The Daily Practice of Learning from Ruth and Boaz

Understanding the example of Ruth and Boaz is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes learning from Ruth's faithfulness and loyalty, trusting in God's providence for the marriage, being faithful to each other through all circumstances, trusting in God's timing and provision, and being examples of faithfulness to others. These daily practices of learning from Ruth and Boaz build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to learn from Ruth's faithfulness and loyalty and to be faithful to one's spouse in all circumstances, being willing to trust in God's providence for one's marriage and to believe that He will provide for one's needs, being willing to be faithful to one's spouse through all circumstances and to remain committed even in difficult times, being willing to trust in God's timing and provision and to be patient with His plan, and being willing to be examples of faithfulness to others and to inspire them toward faithfulness. When couples practice learning from Ruth and Boaz in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and faithful.

Learning from Ruth and Boaz and Communication

Understanding the example of Ruth and Boaz is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their desire to learn from Ruth and Boaz and their commitment to faithfulness, they are better able to learn from their example effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their commitment to faithfulness, to share their trust in God's provision, and to encourage each other in faithfulness.

This communication for learning from Ruth and Boaz includes being willing to discuss one's desire to learn from Ruth and Boaz's example of faithfulness, being willing to share one's trust in God's providence for the marriage, being willing to discuss one's commitment to faithfulness and loyalty, being willing to encourage each other in trusting God's timing and provision, and being willing to share one's desire to be examples of faithfulness to others. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on faithfulness and trust.

Learning from Ruth and Boaz and Growth

Understanding the example of Ruth and Boaz is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples learn from Ruth and Boaz, they are growing in virtues like faithfulness, loyalty, and trust. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through learning from Ruth and Boaz includes growing in faithfulness as they learn from Ruth's example of faithfulness, growing in loyalty as they learn to be loyal to each other, growing in trust as they learn to trust in God's providence, growing in patience as they learn to trust in God's timing, and growing in witness as they learn to be examples of faithfulness to others. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Faithful Example

The example of Ruth and Boaz in Catholic marriage is a faithful example that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand the example of Ruth and Boaz, they are not just being romantic but are actively learning from their example of faithfulness and God's provision.

As we reflect on the example of Ruth and Boaz, let us remember that this example is not just a human story but is a divine lesson that requires God's grace. When we learn from their example, we are building a marriage that is based on faithfulness, trust, and the grace of God.
