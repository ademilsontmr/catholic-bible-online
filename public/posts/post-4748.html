The Parable of the Unprofitable Servants – Luke 17:7–10

Jesus' parable of the unprofitable servants, found in Luke 17:7-10, provides profound insights into humility, obedience, and the proper attitude toward service to God. This parable reveals that we should serve God with humility, recognizing that we are unworthy servants who are simply doing what is required of us. Understanding the parable of the unprofitable servants helps you recognize the importance of humility in service and the proper attitude toward serving God. This parable serves as both a warning against pride in service and a call to humble obedience.

The Master and His Servant

The parable begins with Jesus asking, 'Suppose one of you has a servant plowing or looking after the sheep. Will he say to the servant when he comes in from the field, 'Come along now and sit down to eat'?' This question introduces the relationship between a master and his servant, representing the relationship between God and his people. Understanding the master and his servant helps you recognize that we are servants of God and should have the proper attitude toward serving him.

The Servant's Duties

The parable describes how the servant is expected to plow, look after the sheep, and prepare the master's meal. These duties represent the various responsibilities that God gives to his people. Understanding the servant's duties helps you recognize that God gives us responsibilities and expects us to fulfill them.

The Master's Expectations

The parable describes how the master expects the servant to prepare his meal and serve him before the servant eats. These expectations represent God's expectations of his people to serve him faithfully. Understanding the master's expectations helps you recognize that God expects us to serve him faithfully and put his needs before our own.

The Servant's Response

The parable describes how the servant should respond with humility, saying, 'We are unworthy servants; we have only done our duty.' This response represents the proper attitude of humility and recognition that we are simply doing what is required of us. Understanding the servant's response helps you recognize the importance of humility in service to God.

Lessons About Humility

The parable teaches that we should serve God with humility, recognizing that we are unworthy servants who are simply doing what is required of us. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of humility in service to God.

Lessons About Obedience

The parable emphasizes the importance of obedience to God's commands and fulfilling our responsibilities faithfully. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being obedient to God's commands.

Conclusion: Humble Service

The parable of the unprofitable servants reveals the importance of humility in service to God and the proper attitude toward serving him. This parable challenges us to serve God with humility and recognize that we are simply doing what is required of us.
