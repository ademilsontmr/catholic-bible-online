The Peace of Christ in Marriage (Colossians 3:15)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Christ's Peace

The concept of the peace of Christ in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Colossians 3:15: "And let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, to which indeed you were called in one body. And be thankful." This passage shows that Christ's peace is not just a nice feeling but is a ruling force that should govern the hearts of married couples. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are not just avoiding conflict but are creating an environment where love and harmony can flourish.

This biblical foundation shows that Christ's peace in marriage is not just about being calm but is about allowing Christ's peace to be the ruling principle in the relationship. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This peace is not about being passive but is about being actively ruled by Christ's peace.

Christ's Peace as a Ruling Force

Christ's peace in Catholic marriage is not just a feeling but is a ruling force that should govern the hearts and decisions of married couples. Just as Christ's peace ruled in the hearts of the early Christians, so too should it rule in the hearts of married couples today. This peace is not about avoiding all conflict but is about resolving conflicts in a way that is governed by Christ's peace.

When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are not just being calm but are allowing Christ to be the ruler of their relationship. They are willing to let go of anger and resentment, to forgive each other quickly, to work together to resolve conflicts, and to maintain harmony in their relationship. This peace becomes a powerful force for good in their marriage and in their lives.

The Daily Practice of Christ's Peace

Allowing Christ's peace to rule in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being quick to forgive, being willing to let go of anger and resentment, being willing to work together to resolve conflicts, being thankful for each other, and being willing to maintain harmony in the relationship. These daily practices of Christ's peace build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of Christ's peace includes being willing to forgive quickly when one's spouse has hurt one, being willing to let go of anger and resentment, being willing to work together to resolve conflicts, being thankful for the gift of one's spouse, and being willing to maintain harmony in the relationship. When couples practice Christ's peace in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Christ's Peace and Communication

Christ's peace in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate peacefully with each other, they are better able to resolve conflicts and to maintain harmony in their relationship. Good communication helps couples to express their thoughts and feelings in a way that is governed by Christ's peace.

This communication for Christ's peace includes being willing to speak peacefully even when disagreeing, being willing to listen to each other's concerns, being willing to work together to find solutions, being willing to forgive quickly, and being willing to maintain harmony in the relationship. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are allowing Christ's peace to rule in their relationship.

Christ's Peace and Forgiveness

Christ's peace in marriage is closely connected to forgiveness. When couples are able to forgive each other quickly and completely, they are better able to maintain Christ's peace in their relationship. This forgiveness is not about being weak but is about being strong enough to let go of anger and resentment.

This forgiveness for Christ's peace includes being willing to forgive quickly when one's spouse has hurt one, being willing to ask for forgiveness when one has hurt one's spouse, being willing to let go of anger and resentment, being willing to start fresh each day, and being willing to maintain harmony in the relationship. When couples practice forgiveness in these ways, they are not just resolving conflicts but are allowing Christ's peace to rule in their relationship.

Christ's Peace and Gratitude

Christ's peace in marriage is closely connected to gratitude. When couples are thankful for each other and for their relationship, they are better able to maintain Christ's peace in their hearts. This gratitude is not just about being polite but is about recognizing and appreciating the gift that God has given them.

This gratitude for Christ's peace includes being thankful for the gift of one's spouse, being thankful for the gift of marriage, being thankful for the blessings that God has given them, being willing to express gratitude regularly, and being willing to maintain a thankful heart. When couples practice gratitude in these ways, they are not just being polite but are allowing Christ's peace to rule in their hearts.

Christ's Peace and Growth

Christ's peace in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are growing in virtues like patience, forgiveness, and gratitude. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to Christ's peace in the world.

This growth through Christ's peace includes growing in patience as they learn to maintain peace during difficult times, growing in forgiveness as they learn to forgive quickly and completely, growing in gratitude as they learn to be thankful for each other, growing in love as they learn to love each other with Christ's peace, and growing in holiness as they learn to imitate Christ's peace. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Christ's Peace and the Mission of Marriage

Christ's peace in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to Christ's peace in the world.

This mission through Christ's peace includes being willing to serve each other with Christ's peace, being willing to serve their children with Christ's peace, being willing to serve others with Christ's peace, being willing to be witnesses to Christ's peace, and being willing to build a family based on Christ's peace. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are fulfilling their mission and becoming better witnesses to Christ's peace.

The Grace of Christ's Peace

Christ's peace in marriage is not something that couples can achieve on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts and to grow in holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts and to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Conclusion: A Ruling Peace

Christ's peace in Catholic marriage is a ruling peace that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples allow Christ's peace to rule in their hearts, they are not just avoiding conflict but are allowing Christ to be the ruler of their relationship.

As we reflect on Christ's peace in marriage, let us remember that this peace is not just a human feeling but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we allow Christ's peace to rule in our hearts, we are building a marriage that is based on peace, harmony, and the grace of God.
