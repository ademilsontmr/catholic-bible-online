Seeking the Kingdom First in Marriage (Matthew 6:33)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Seeking the Kingdom First

The practice of seeking the kingdom first in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Matthew 6:33: "But seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you." This passage shows that seeking the kingdom first is not just a nice spiritual practice but is essential for receiving God's blessings and guidance in marriage. When couples seek the kingdom first in their marriage, they are not just being religious but are actively prioritizing God's will and His kingdom above all else, trusting that He will provide for all their needs.

This biblical foundation shows that seeking the kingdom first in marriage is not just about attending church regularly but is about making God's kingdom and His righteousness the primary focus of their relationship. When couples seek the kingdom first, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This seeking is not about being perfect but is about being willing to prioritize God's will above their own desires.

Seeking the Kingdom as a Foundation

Seeking the kingdom first in Catholic marriage serves as the foundation upon which all other aspects of the relationship are built. Just as a house needs a solid foundation to stand strong, so too does a marriage need the foundation of seeking God's kingdom first. This seeking is not about neglecting the relationship but is about building the relationship on the solid foundation of God's will and His kingdom.

When couples seek the kingdom first in their marriage, they are not just being spiritual but are building their relationship on the solid foundation of God's will. They are willing to prioritize God's kingdom above their own desires, to seek God's guidance in all decisions, to trust in God's provision for their needs, to serve God together as a couple, and to build their marriage according to God's plan. This seeking becomes a powerful foundation for their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Seeking the Kingdom First

Seeking the kingdom first is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying together for God's guidance, seeking God's will in important decisions, prioritizing God's kingdom above personal desires, serving God together as a couple, and trusting in God's provision. These daily practices of seeking the kingdom first build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to pray together for God's guidance in important decisions, being willing to seek God's will in all aspects of married life, being willing to prioritize God's kingdom above personal desires and preferences, being willing to serve God together as a couple, and being willing to trust in God's provision for all their needs. When couples practice seeking the kingdom first in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Seeking the Kingdom First and Communication

Seeking the kingdom first is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate openly about their commitment to seeking God's kingdom first, they are better able to maintain this priority effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss how they can seek God's kingdom together, to share their spiritual goals and aspirations, and to support each other in their spiritual journey.

This communication for seeking the kingdom first includes being willing to discuss how to seek God's kingdom together as a couple, being willing to share spiritual goals and aspirations, being willing to pray together for God's guidance, being willing to discuss how to serve God together, and being willing to support each other in their spiritual journey. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are strengthening their commitment to seeking God's kingdom first.

Seeking the Kingdom First and Growth

Seeking the kingdom first is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples seek the kingdom first, they are growing in virtues like trust, obedience, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through seeking the kingdom first includes growing in trust as they learn to trust in God's provision, growing in obedience as they learn to follow God's will, growing in love as they learn to love God and each other more deeply, growing in faith as they learn to have faith in God's plan, and growing in holiness as they learn to seek God's righteousness. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Kingdom-Focused Love

Seeking the kingdom first in Catholic marriage is a kingdom-focused love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples seek the kingdom first, they are not just being spiritual but are building their relationship on the solid foundation of God's will and His kingdom.

As we reflect on seeking the kingdom first, let us remember that this seeking is not just a human effort but is a divine priority that requires God's grace. When we seek the kingdom first, we are building a marriage that is based on trust, obedience, and the grace of God.
