Ash Wednesday: Beginning of the Lenten Journey

Ash Wednesday marks the beginning of Lent, a forty-day period of preparation for Easter. The Church encourages fasting on this day to help us enter into the penitential spirit of Lent. The ashes we receive remind us of our mortality and our need for repentance, while fasting helps us focus on these important spiritual realities.

Good Friday: Commemorating Christ's Sacrifice

Good Friday is the day we commemorate Christ's passion and death on the cross. Fasting on this day helps us unite ourselves with Christ's suffering and sacrifice. By denying ourselves food, we are reminded of the great sacrifice that Christ made for our salvation and are called to reflect on the meaning of His death.

The Biblical Foundation of These Practices

The practice of fasting on these days has deep biblical roots. Jesus Himself fasted for forty days in the desert, showing us the importance of fasting as preparation for spiritual work. The early Church continued this tradition, recognizing that fasting helps us prepare for important spiritual events and remember significant moments in salvation history.
