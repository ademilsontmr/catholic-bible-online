Why Catholic Marriage Requires Daily Conversion (Luke 9:23)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Daily Conversion

The Catholic understanding of daily conversion in marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Luke 9:23: "And he said to all, 'If any man would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me.'" This passage shows that following Christ requires daily conversion - a daily turning away from sin and a daily turning toward God. When couples understand that marriage requires this daily conversion, they are better able to build a relationship that is truly holy and pleasing to God.

This biblical foundation shows that daily conversion in marriage is not just about avoiding sin but is about growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This conversion is not just for our own benefit but is essential for building a marriage that reflects God's love and brings glory to Him.

Daily Conversion as a Call to Holiness

Daily conversion in Catholic marriage is a call to holiness. This means that couples are called to grow in holiness every day, to become more like Christ every day, and to love each other more perfectly every day. This daily conversion is not just about avoiding sin but is about growing in virtue and becoming better spouses.

This daily conversion includes turning away from selfishness and turning toward selflessness, turning away from pride and turning toward humility, turning away from anger and turning toward patience, and turning away from indifference and turning toward love. When couples practice this daily conversion, they are growing in holiness and building a marriage that is truly holy.

The Daily Practice of Conversion

Daily conversion in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being willing to admit when one is wrong, being willing to apologize when one has hurt one's spouse, being willing to forgive when one's spouse has hurt one, and being willing to change one's behavior for the good of the relationship.

This daily practice of conversion includes being willing to listen to one's spouse's concerns, being willing to make changes in one's life for the good of the marriage, being willing to grow in virtue, and being willing to become more like Christ. When couples practice this daily conversion, they are building a relationship that is strong and holy.

Conversion and Communication

Daily conversion in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples practice daily conversion, they are better able to communicate effectively because they are more focused on understanding their spouse's perspective than on defending their own. This conversion helps couples to avoid conflicts and to resolve disagreements peacefully.

This conversion in communication includes being willing to listen without interrupting, being willing to understand one's spouse's point of view, being willing to apologize when one is wrong, and being willing to forgive when one's spouse is wrong. When couples practice conversion in communication, they are better able to understand each other and to grow together.

Conversion and Growth

Daily conversion in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice daily conversion, they are growing in virtues like patience, kindness, generosity, and love. They are also learning to see their spouse as God sees them and to love their spouse with the same unconditional love that God has for them.

This growth in virtue is essential for building a strong and holy marriage. When couples grow in virtue, they are better able to love each other, to communicate effectively, to resolve conflicts, and to fulfill their vocation. This growth also helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

Conversion and the Mission of Marriage

Daily conversion in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples practice daily conversion, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's transforming love in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often values self-interest over service to others. When couples practice daily conversion, they become a sign of hope and inspiration to others. They show the world that conversion is possible and that it brings joy and fulfillment to those who practice it.

The Grace of Conversion

Daily conversion in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to practice daily conversion and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can practice daily conversion and build a strong and holy marriage.

Conclusion: A Lifelong Journey

Daily conversion in Catholic marriage is a lifelong journey that helps couples to grow in holiness and to become more like Christ. When couples practice daily conversion, they are not just building a strong marriage but are growing in holiness and becoming better witnesses to God's love in the world.

As we reflect on daily conversion in marriage, let us remember that this conversion is not just a human effort but is a divine gift that helps us to love each other as Christ loves the Church. When we practice daily conversion, we are building marriages that are strong, holy, and filled with love.
