The Assumption as a Celebration of Mary's Victory

The Feast of the Assumption celebrates the belief that Mary, the Mother of God, was taken body and soul into heaven at the end of her earthly life, showing her special role in salvation history. Catholic tradition sees the Assumption as essential for understanding that Mary was given this special privilege because of her unique role as the Mother of God and her perfect cooperation with God's grace. The Assumption is not just about Mary going to heaven but about her victory over death and her special role in salvation.
