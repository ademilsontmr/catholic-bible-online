The Parable of the Tower Builder – Luke 14:28–30

Jesus' parable of the tower builder, found in Luke 14:28-30, provides profound insights into the importance of counting the cost of discipleship and the need to be prepared to finish what we start. This parable reveals that following Jesus requires careful consideration and commitment, and that we should not begin the journey of discipleship without being prepared to complete it. Understanding the parable of the tower builder helps you recognize the importance of counting the cost of discipleship and the need to be committed to following Jesus all the way. This parable serves as both a warning about the cost of discipleship and an encouragement to be prepared for the journey.

The Tower Builder's Planning

The parable begins with Jesus asking, 'Suppose one of you wants to build a tower. Won't you first sit down and estimate the cost to see if you have enough money to complete it?' This planning represents the importance of carefully considering the cost and commitment required before beginning the journey of discipleship. Understanding the tower builder's planning helps you recognize the importance of being realistic about what discipleship requires and being prepared for the commitment.

The Cost of Building

The parable emphasizes that building a tower requires significant resources and that the builder must have enough money to complete the project. This cost represents the various costs of discipleship, including time, energy, relationships, and sometimes material resources. Understanding the cost of building helps you recognize that discipleship is not free or easy, but requires significant investment and commitment.

The Danger of Incomplete Projects

The parable describes the consequences of starting a project without being able to complete it: 'If you lay the foundation and are not able to finish it, everyone who sees it will ridicule you, saying, 'This person began to build and wasn't able to finish.'' This ridicule represents the shame and disappointment that comes from starting the journey of discipleship but not being able to complete it. Understanding the danger of incomplete projects helps you recognize the importance of being prepared to finish what you start.

Lessons About Counting the Cost

The parable teaches that we should carefully count the cost before beginning the journey of discipleship. We should be realistic about what discipleship requires and be prepared to make the necessary sacrifices. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being honest about what discipleship will cost you.

Lessons About Commitment

The parable emphasizes the importance of being committed to completing the journey of discipleship. We should not begin the journey unless we are prepared to finish it, regardless of the challenges and difficulties that may arise. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being committed to following Jesus all the way.

Lessons About Preparation

The parable teaches that we should be prepared for the journey of discipleship before we begin it. This preparation includes understanding what discipleship requires, being willing to make the necessary sacrifices, and having the resources needed to complete the journey. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being prepared for the challenges of discipleship.

Lessons About Perseverance

The parable emphasizes the importance of perseverance in the journey of discipleship. We should be prepared to continue the journey even when it becomes difficult or challenging. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being willing to persevere through the difficulties of discipleship.

Lessons About the Consequences of Failure

The parable warns about the consequences of starting the journey of discipleship but not being able to complete it. This failure can result in shame, disappointment, and ridicule from others. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being prepared to complete the journey of discipleship.

Conclusion: Being Prepared for Discipleship

The parable of the tower builder reveals the importance of counting the cost of discipleship and being prepared to complete the journey. This parable challenges us to be realistic about what discipleship requires and to be committed to following Jesus all the way. Understanding this parable helps you recognize the importance of being prepared for the challenges of discipleship and the need to be committed to completing the journey. The parable serves as both a warning about the cost of discipleship and an encouragement to be prepared for the journey.
