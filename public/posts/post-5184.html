Why Love Is the Foundation of Catholic Marriage (1 Corinthians 13:4–7)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Definition of Love

The foundation of Catholic marriage is love, but not just any kind of love. It is the love that Saint Paul describes in 1 Corinthians 13:4–7: "Love is patient and kind; love is not jealous or boastful; it is not arrogant or rude. Love does not insist on its own way; it is not irritable or resentful; it does not rejoice at wrong, but rejoices in the right. Love bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things." This passage provides the blueprint for what love should look like in marriage.

This biblical definition of love shows that love is not just a feeling or an emotion but is a decision and a way of life. It is about choosing to be patient, kind, and self-sacrificing even when it is difficult. It is about putting the other person's needs before one's own and about being willing to bear with each other's weaknesses and faults. This kind of love is the foundation upon which a strong and lasting marriage is built.

Love as a Decision

In Catholic marriage, love is not just a feeling that comes and goes but is a decision that couples make every day. This decision is rooted in their commitment to each other and in their understanding that love is a choice that requires effort and sacrifice. When couples choose to love each other, they are choosing to be patient, kind, and forgiving even when it is difficult.

This decision to love is not just about the couple's own happiness but is about fulfilling God's plan for their marriage. God calls couples to love each other as He loves them - with patience, kindness, and self-sacrifice. This decision to love becomes the foundation upon which all other aspects of their marriage are built.

Love as Self-Sacrifice

True love in marriage involves self-sacrifice, just as Christ sacrificed Himself for the Church. This self-sacrifice is not about losing one's identity but is about finding one's true identity in the context of loving and serving another person. It is about being willing to put the other person's needs before one's own and about being willing to make sacrifices for the good of the relationship.

This self-sacrifice is expressed in many ways - in the daily tasks of married life, in caring for each other during illness, in supporting each other's dreams and goals, and in being willing to forgive each other when they fail. This self-sacrifice is not a burden but is a gift that helps couples to grow in love and holiness.

Love as Patience and Kindness

Patience and kindness are essential aspects of love in marriage. Patience means being willing to bear with each other's weaknesses and faults, to give each other time to grow and change, and to be understanding when the other person makes mistakes. Kindness means being gentle and considerate in the way we treat each other, even when we are tired or stressed.

These virtues are not always easy to practice, especially during difficult times, but they are essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When couples practice patience and kindness with each other, they create an environment of trust and safety where love can flourish. This patience and kindness become the foundation upon which their relationship grows.

Love as Forgiveness

Forgiveness is an essential aspect of love in marriage. No couple is perfect, and all couples will experience conflicts and difficulties. The ability to forgive each other and to seek reconciliation is a sign of true love and is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage.

Forgiveness in marriage is not just about saying "I'm sorry" but is about truly seeking to understand and to heal the wounds that have been caused. It is about being willing to change and to grow for the good of the relationship. This forgiveness is not a sign of weakness but is a sign of strength and maturity.

Love as Faithfulness

Faithfulness is an essential aspect of love in marriage. This faithfulness is not just about avoiding infidelity but is about being faithful in all aspects of the relationship - emotionally, spiritually, and physically. It is about being committed to each other and to the relationship, even when it is difficult.

This faithfulness is expressed in the couple's commitment to each other, in their willingness to work through problems together, and in their desire to grow together in love and holiness. This faithfulness becomes the foundation upon which trust and intimacy are built.

Love as Hope and Perseverance

Love in marriage involves hope and perseverance. Hope means believing that the relationship can grow and improve, even during difficult times. Perseverance means being willing to work through problems and to remain committed to each other, even when it is difficult.

This hope and perseverance are not based on wishful thinking but are based on the couple's faith in God and in His plan for their marriage. When couples have hope and perseverance, they are able to weather the storms of life together and to grow stronger through their difficulties.

Love as a Reflection of God's Love

The love between husband and wife is meant to be a reflection of God's love for humanity. Just as God loves us with patience, kindness, and self-sacrifice, so too are couples called to love each other in the same way. This reflection of God's love is not just for their own benefit but is meant to be a witness to the world.

When couples love each other as God loves them, they become a sign of God's love to the world. Their love becomes a witness to the power of God's love to transform human relationships. This witness is particularly important in a world that often misunderstands or rejects the true meaning of love.

Conclusion: A Sacred Foundation

Love is the sacred foundation of Catholic marriage. This love is not just a feeling but is a decision, a way of life, and a reflection of God's love for humanity. When couples build their marriage on this foundation of love, they create a relationship that is strong, lasting, and holy.

As we reflect on love as the foundation of Catholic marriage, let us remember that this love is not just for our own benefit but is meant to be a witness to God's love in the world. When we love each other as God loves us, we become signs of hope in a world that often experiences brokenness and division.
