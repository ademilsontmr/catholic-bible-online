Catholic Marriage and the Example of Mary and Joseph (Matthew 1:18–25)

2025-01-27

The Holy Family as a Model

The marriage of Mary and Joseph provides Catholic couples with a perfect model of what marriage should look like. In Matthew 1:18–25, we see how Joseph, when he learned that Mary was with child, planned to divorce her quietly, but an angel appeared to him in a dream and told him that the child was conceived by the Holy Spirit. Joseph's response was one of faith and obedience - he took Mary as his wife and cared for her and the child Jesus.

This passage shows us several important aspects of the marriage of Mary and Joseph that Catholic couples should emulate: their faith in God's plan, their obedience to God's will, their trust in each other, their commitment to their vocation, and their willingness to sacrifice for the good of their family. These virtues are essential for building a strong and holy marriage.

Faith and Trust in God's Plan

Mary and Joseph's marriage was characterized by their faith and trust in God's plan. When the angel appeared to Mary and told her that she would conceive a child by the Holy Spirit, she responded with faith: "Behold, I am the handmaid of the Lord; let it be to me according to your word." When the angel appeared to Joseph and told him to take Mary as his wife, he responded with obedience and trust.

This faith and trust in God's plan is essential for Catholic couples. When couples trust in God's plan for their marriage, they are better able to face the challenges and difficulties that come with married life. They know that God is with them and that He will provide for all their needs. This trust helps couples to remain faithful to their vows and to grow in holiness together.

Obedience to God's Will

Mary and Joseph's marriage was also characterized by their obedience to God's will. When God asked them to do something difficult or unexpected, they responded with obedience and trust. Mary accepted the role of being the Mother of God, even though it meant facing potential scandal and rejection. Joseph accepted the role of being the foster father of Jesus, even though it meant taking on responsibilities that he had not planned for.

This obedience to God's will is essential for Catholic couples. When couples are obedient to God's will, they are better able to fulfill their vocation and to grow in holiness. They know that God's plan is always for their good and that He will give them the grace they need to fulfill whatever He asks of them. This obedience helps couples to remain faithful to their vows and to serve God and others.

Mutual Respect and Trust

Mary and Joseph's marriage was characterized by mutual respect and trust. They trusted each other completely and respected each other's role in God's plan. Mary trusted Joseph to care for her and the child Jesus, and Joseph respected Mary's unique role as the Mother of God. They worked together as a team to fulfill God's plan for their family.

This mutual respect and trust is essential for Catholic couples. When couples respect and trust each other, they are better able to communicate, to make decisions together, and to support each other in their vocation. They know that they can rely on each other and that they are working together toward the same goal. This respect and trust helps couples to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Commitment to Their Vocation

Mary and Joseph were completely committed to their vocation as husband and wife and as parents. They put the needs of their family before their own needs and were willing to make sacrifices for the good of their family. They were committed to raising Jesus in the faith and to helping Him fulfill His mission in the world.

This commitment to their vocation is essential for Catholic couples. When couples are committed to their vocation, they are better able to fulfill their responsibilities and to grow in holiness. They know that their marriage is not just about their own happiness but is about serving God and helping each other to grow in holiness. This commitment helps couples to remain faithful to their vows and to fulfill their mission in the world.

Willingness to Sacrifice

Mary and Joseph were willing to make sacrifices for the good of their family. They left their home in Nazareth to travel to Bethlehem for the census, even though Mary was pregnant. They fled to Egypt to protect the child Jesus from Herod's wrath. They made many other sacrifices throughout their married life to fulfill God's plan for their family.

This willingness to sacrifice is essential for Catholic couples. When couples are willing to sacrifice for each other and for their family, they are better able to build a strong and lasting marriage. They know that love involves sacrifice and that they are called to put the needs of others before their own needs. This sacrifice helps couples to grow in holiness and to become more like Christ.

Prayer and Spiritual Life

Mary and Joseph's marriage was centered on prayer and their relationship with God. They prayed together, they participated in the religious practices of their time, and they raised Jesus in the faith. Their spiritual life was the foundation upon which their marriage was built.

This prayer and spiritual life is essential for Catholic couples. When couples pray together and participate in the sacraments together, they are better able to grow in holiness and to fulfill their vocation. They know that their marriage is not just a human relationship but is a sacrament that involves God's presence and participation. This spiritual life helps couples to remain faithful to their vows and to grow in holiness together.

Witness to the World

Mary and Joseph's marriage was a witness to the world of what marriage should look like. They showed the world that marriage is not just about personal happiness but is about serving God and helping each other to grow in holiness. They showed the world that marriage involves faith, trust, obedience, respect, commitment, sacrifice, and prayer.

This witness to the world is essential for Catholic couples. When couples live their marriage according to God's plan, they become a sign of hope in a world that often misunderstands or rejects the true meaning of marriage. They show the world that marriage is a sacred vocation that involves God's presence and participation. This witness helps to build up the kingdom of God on earth.

Conclusion: Following Their Example

The marriage of Mary and Joseph provides Catholic couples with a perfect model to follow. Their faith, trust, obedience, respect, commitment, sacrifice, and prayer are virtues that all couples should strive to emulate. When couples follow their example, they are better able to build a strong and holy marriage that serves God and helps to build up His kingdom on earth.

As we reflect on the example of Mary and Joseph, let us remember that their marriage was not perfect in the sense of being without challenges, but it was perfect in the sense of being completely surrendered to God's will. When we follow their example, we too can build marriages that are centered on God and that serve His purposes in the world.
