The Structure of the Divine Mercy Chaplet

The power of the Divine Mercy Chaplet is found in its simple but profound structure. The chaplet begins with the Sign of the Cross, followed by the Our Father, Hail Mary, and Apostles' Creed. Then we pray the main part of the chaplet: "Eternal Father, I offer you the Body and Blood, Soul and Divinity of Your Dearly Beloved Son, Our Lord, Jesus Christ, in atonement for our sins and those of the whole world." This is followed by ten repetitions of "For the sake of His sorrowful Passion, have mercy on us and on the whole world." The power of the Divine Mercy Chaplet is in offering Jesus' sacrifice to the Father and asking for mercy for ourselves and for the whole world.

This structure shows that the power of the Divine Mercy Chaplet is about participating in Christ's sacrifice and interceding for the world. We're not just asking for mercy for ourselves but for everyone. The power of the Divine Mercy Chaplet is that it makes us instruments of God's mercy, helping to bring His mercy to a world that desperately needs it. This understanding helps us to see this devotion as a mission and a responsibility.
