Saint Joseph and Discernment of God's Will

2024-01-28

Introduction: The Art of Divine Discernment

Saint Joseph's life provides a perfect example of what it means to discern God's will and to respond to divine guidance with wisdom and obedience. His ability to recognize God's voice, to understand His plan, and to act on divine guidance demonstrates the art of spiritual discernment at its finest. Joseph's example teaches us that discerning God's will is not just about making good decisions, but about developing a deep relationship with God and being attentive to His guidance in all aspects of our lives.

In today's world, where we are constantly bombarded with competing voices and conflicting advice, Joseph's example of discernment provides a powerful guide for navigating life's decisions. His life shows that true discernment is not about following our own preferences or the advice of others, but about being attentive to God's voice and being willing to follow His guidance even when it requires us to make difficult choices. Joseph's discernment demonstrates that true wisdom comes from God and that the best decisions are those that align with His will.

Joseph's discernment of God's will is particularly significant because it was exercised in the most extraordinary circumstances imaginable. From accepting his role as the guardian of the Holy Family to making life-and-death decisions about his family's safety, Joseph's discernment was constantly tested and always proved reliable. His example provides a model for all believers who seek to make good decisions and to follow God's will in their lives.

The Foundation of Joseph's Discernment

Joseph's ability to discern God's will was rooted in several important qualities that made him particularly receptive to divine guidance. First, his discernment was based on a deep relationship with God. His constant awareness of God's presence and his regular prayer life provided him with the spiritual sensitivity needed to recognize God's voice and understand His guidance.

Second, Joseph's discernment was characterized by humility and openness to God's will. He did not approach decisions with his own agenda or preferences, but was always open to God's guidance and willing to accept His plan even when it differed from his own expectations. His humility allowed him to be receptive to God's voice and to follow His guidance without resistance.

Third, Joseph's discernment was marked by trust in God's wisdom and goodness. He trusted that God's plan was always for his good and the good of others, and this trust enabled him to follow God's guidance even when it required him to make difficult decisions or to face challenging circumstances.

Recognizing God's Voice

Joseph's discernment was characterized by his ability to recognize God's voice through various means. Throughout the Gospels, we see Joseph receiving divine guidance through angelic messages, circumstances, and the quiet promptings of the Holy Spirit. His ability to recognize these different forms of divine communication demonstrates his spiritual sensitivity and his deep relationship with God.

This ability to recognize God's voice is particularly important for modern believers who often struggle to distinguish between God's guidance and their own thoughts or the advice of others. Joseph's example shows that recognizing God's voice requires spiritual sensitivity, regular prayer, and a deep relationship with God. His discernment teaches us that God speaks to us in various ways and that we need to be attentive to His voice in all aspects of our lives.

Joseph's recognition of God's voice also demonstrates his understanding that divine guidance is not always dramatic or obvious, but often comes through quiet, subtle means. His ability to recognize God's guidance through ordinary circumstances and quiet promptings shows that true discernment requires attentiveness and spiritual sensitivity.

Understanding God's Plan

Joseph's discernment was also characterized by his ability to understand God's plan and to see how his decisions fit into God's larger purpose. His acceptance of his role as the guardian of the Holy Family shows that he understood how his life was part of God's plan of salvation. His discernment demonstrates his ability to see beyond immediate circumstances and to understand the larger purpose of God's guidance.

This understanding of God's plan is particularly important for modern believers who often make decisions based only on immediate circumstances or personal preferences. Joseph's example shows that true discernment includes understanding how our decisions fit into God's larger plan and how they contribute to His purposes. His understanding teaches us that we need to see our decisions in the context of God's plan rather than just in terms of our own immediate needs or desires.

Joseph's understanding of God's plan also demonstrates his ability to trust in God's wisdom even when he didn't fully understand His plan. His willingness to accept his role in the Holy Family shows that he trusted God's wisdom and was willing to cooperate with His plan even when he didn't fully understand it.

Responding to Divine Guidance

Joseph's discernment was most clearly demonstrated through his immediate and complete response to divine guidance. When he received guidance from the angel about Mary's pregnancy, he acted immediately and completely. When he was warned about Herod's threat, he responded with immediate action. His response to divine guidance shows that true discernment includes not just recognizing God's will, but acting on it with courage and determination.

This response to divine guidance is particularly important for modern believers who often recognize God's will but hesitate to act on it. Joseph's example shows that true discernment includes the courage to act on God's guidance even when it requires us to make difficult decisions or to face challenging circumstances. His response teaches us that discernment is not just about understanding God's will, but about having the courage to follow it.

Joseph's response to divine guidance also demonstrates his understanding that following God's will often requires immediate action and complete commitment. His immediate response to the angel's guidance shows that he understood the urgency of following God's will and the importance of complete commitment to His plan.

Practical Applications for Modern Life

Saint Joseph's example of discernment has many practical applications for modern life. First, we can learn to develop our spiritual sensitivity by spending time in prayer, reading Scripture, and being attentive to God's presence in our lives. This might include setting aside time each day for prayer and reflection, being more attentive to the promptings of the Holy Spirit, or seeking guidance through spiritual reading and reflection.

Second, we can learn to approach decisions with humility and openness to God's will by being willing to put aside our own preferences and being open to God's guidance. This might include praying for guidance before making important decisions, being open to God's plan even when it differs from our own, or being willing to make changes when God provides different guidance.

Third, we can learn to respond to God's guidance with courage and determination by being willing to act on His guidance even when it requires us to make difficult decisions or to face challenging circumstances. This might include being more courageous in following God's call, being more committed to His plan, or being more willing to make sacrifices for the sake of His will.

Conclusion: Mastering the Art of Discernment

Saint Joseph's example of discernment provides us with a powerful model of what it means to recognize and respond to God's will in our daily lives. His example teaches us that true discernment is not just about making good decisions, but about developing a deep relationship with God and being attentive to His guidance in all aspects of our lives. His discernment shows us that true wisdom comes from God and that the best decisions are those that align with His will.

As we strive to follow Joseph's example in our own lives, we can commit ourselves to developing our spiritual sensitivity, approaching decisions with humility and openness to God's will, and responding to His guidance with courage and determination. We can learn to recognize God's voice, understand His plan, and act on His guidance with faith and trust.

Ultimately, Joseph's discernment points us toward the deeper truth that true wisdom and guidance come from God and that the best decisions are those that align with His will. When we understand this, we can develop the spiritual discernment needed to navigate life's decisions with wisdom and confidence.

Prayer for Discernment

'Saint Joseph, model of discernment, help me to develop the spiritual sensitivity needed to recognize God's voice and understand His plan. Teach me to approach decisions with humility and openness to God's will, and to respond to His guidance with courage and determination. May my discernment reflect your example and bring me closer to God's will. In Jesus' name, Amen.'
