Why Catholic Marriage Is Open to Life (Genesis 1:28)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation

The Catholic understanding that marriage should be open to life is firmly rooted in Scripture, particularly in Genesis 1:28: "And God blessed them, and God said to them, 'Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the earth and subdue it.'" This passage shows that from the very beginning, God intended marriage to be fruitful and to participate in His creative work. This openness to life is not just a biological reality but is part of God's plan for marriage and for the salvation of the world.

This biblical foundation shows that openness to life is not just about having children but is about participating in God's creative work and helping to build up His kingdom on earth. Each child is a unique person created in the image and likeness of God, with an eternal destiny and a specific mission in God's plan. When couples are open to life, they are cooperating with God's plan for creation and salvation.

Openness to Life as a Sign of Love

Openness to life in marriage is a sign of the couple's love for each other and for God. When couples are open to the possibility of children, they are showing that their love is not just about their own happiness but is about participating in God's creative work. This openness is a sign that their love is generous, self-sacrificing, and life-giving, just as God's love is.

This openness to life is also a sign of the couple's trust in God's providence. When couples are open to children, they are trusting that God will provide for all their needs and that He will give them the grace they need to raise their children in the faith. This trust is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage.

The Gift of Children

Children are not just a biological reality but are a precious gift from God. Each child is a unique person with an eternal destiny and a specific mission in God's plan. When couples are open to children, they are receiving this gift with gratitude and are committing themselves to helping their children fulfill their mission in life.

This gift of children is not just for the couple's own benefit but is for the good of the entire Church and society. Children help to build up the kingdom of God on earth and to continue the work of evangelization. They also help to create a more loving and compassionate society by teaching adults about the importance of love, sacrifice, and service.

The Responsibility of Parenthood

With the gift of children comes the responsibility of parenthood. Catholic parents are called to be the first and most important teachers of their children in the faith. They are responsible for helping their children to know and love God, to grow in holiness, and to fulfill their vocation in life.

This responsibility includes teaching their children to pray, to participate in the sacraments, to live according to God's commandments, and to serve others. It also includes providing for their children's physical, emotional, and spiritual needs. This responsibility is not a burden but is a privilege and a means of growing in holiness.

Openness to Life and Natural Family Planning

Being open to life does not mean that couples must have as many children as biologically possible. The Catholic Church teaches that couples can use natural family planning to space the births of their children for serious reasons, such as health concerns, financial difficulties, or the need to care for existing children. However, this planning must always be done with an openness to life and a respect for God's plan.

Natural family planning is different from artificial contraception because it respects the natural rhythms of the woman's body and does not interfere with the marital act. It also requires couples to communicate openly about their fertility and to make decisions together about their family size. This communication and cooperation helps to strengthen their marriage.

Openness to Life and Infertility

Some couples may experience infertility and be unable to have children naturally. This can be a very difficult and painful experience. However, the Catholic Church teaches that these couples are still called to be open to life and can fulfill this call in other ways, such as adoption, foster care, or serving others in their community.

These couples can also grow in holiness through their experience of infertility by learning to trust in God's plan, to support each other in their suffering, and to find meaning and purpose in their marriage even without children. Their marriage can still be a sign of God's love and a source of blessing for others.

Openness to Life and the Mission of Marriage

Openness to life is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples are open to children, they are participating in God's creative work and helping to build up His kingdom on earth. They are also becoming witnesses to God's love and generosity in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often values personal fulfillment over the gift of life. When couples are open to children, they show the world that life is a precious gift from God and that it should be welcomed and cherished. This witness helps to build a more loving and compassionate society.

The Grace of Openness to Life

Being open to life requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to be open to children and to fulfill their responsibilities as parents. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to be open to life and to fulfill their vocation as parents.

Conclusion: A Sacred Trust

Being open to life in Catholic marriage is a sacred trust that God gives to couples. When couples are open to children, they are participating in God's creative work and helping to build up His kingdom on earth. They are also becoming witnesses to God's love and generosity in the world.

As we reflect on openness to life in marriage, let us remember that this is not just about having children but is about participating in God's plan for creation and salvation. When we are open to life, we are cooperating with God's work and becoming better witnesses to His love in the world.
