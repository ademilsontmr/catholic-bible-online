Honoring One Another Above Yourselves (Romans 12:10)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Mutual Honor

The practice of honoring one another above yourselves in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Romans 12:10: "Love one another with brotherly affection. Outdo one another in showing honor." This passage shows that mutual honor in marriage is not just about being polite but is about actively seeking to honor and respect one's spouse above oneself. When couples practice this kind of mutual honor, they are not just being nice to each other but are creating an environment where love and respect can flourish.

This biblical foundation shows that mutual honor in marriage is not just about being respectful but is about being willing to put one's spouse's dignity and worth above one's own. When couples practice mutual honor, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This honor is not about being inferior but is about being willing to recognize and celebrate the dignity of one's spouse.

Mutual Honor as a Reflection of Divine Love

Mutual honor in Catholic marriage is a reflection of the divine love that God has for each of us. Just as God honors and respects each person as a unique and valuable creation, so too are married couples called to honor and respect each other as unique and valuable individuals. This mutual honor is not about being subservient but is about recognizing and celebrating the dignity of each person.

When couples practice mutual honor, they are not just being nice to each other but are imitating God's love for each of us. They are showing each other the same kind of respect and honor that God shows to each of us, and they are growing in holiness as they do so. This mutual honor becomes a powerful witness to the world of God's love and respect for each person.

The Daily Practice of Mutual Honor

Mutual honor in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes speaking respectfully to each other, listening to each other's opinions and ideas, supporting each other's dreams and goals, being grateful for each other's efforts, and showing appreciation for each other's gifts and talents. These daily practices of mutual honor build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of mutual honor includes being willing to speak kindly and respectfully to one's spouse, being willing to listen to and consider one's spouse's opinions, being willing to support one's spouse's dreams and goals, being willing to show appreciation for one's spouse's efforts and contributions, and being willing to celebrate one's spouse's gifts and talents. When couples practice mutual honor in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Mutual Honor and Communication

Mutual honor in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate respectfully and honorably with each other, they are better able to express their thoughts and feelings in a way that builds up the relationship rather than tearing it down. Good communication helps couples to show each other the respect and honor that they deserve.

This communication for mutual honor includes being willing to speak respectfully even when disagreeing, being willing to listen to and consider one's spouse's perspective, being willing to express appreciation and gratitude, being willing to apologize when one has been disrespectful, and being willing to work together to resolve conflicts in a respectful way. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on mutual honor and respect.

Mutual Honor and Service

Mutual honor in marriage includes being willing to serve each other with honor and respect. When couples are willing to serve each other, they are not just being helpful but are showing each other the honor and respect that they deserve. This service is not about being a doormat but is about being willing to put one's spouse's needs before one's own needs.

This service for mutual honor includes being willing to help each other with daily tasks, being willing to support each other's dreams and goals, being willing to make sacrifices for each other's good, being willing to be there for each other in times of need, and being willing to serve each other with joy and generosity. When couples serve each other in these ways, they are not just being helpful but are showing each other the honor and respect that they deserve.

Mutual Honor and Growth

Mutual honor in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice mutual honor, they are growing in virtues like humility, respect, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love and respect in the world.

This growth through mutual honor includes growing in humility as they learn to put others first, growing in respect as they learn to honor and respect each other, growing in love as they learn to love each other with honor and respect, growing in gratitude as they learn to appreciate each other's gifts and contributions, and growing in holiness as they learn to imitate God's love and respect for each person. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Mutual Honor and the Mission of Marriage

Mutual honor in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples practice mutual honor, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's love and respect in the world.

This mission through mutual honor includes being willing to serve each other with honor and respect, being willing to serve their children with honor and respect, being willing to serve others with honor and respect, being willing to be witnesses to God's love and respect, and being willing to build a family based on honor and respect. When couples practice mutual honor in these ways, they are fulfilling their mission and becoming better witnesses to God's love.

The Grace of Mutual Honor

Mutual honor in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to practice mutual honor and to grow in holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to practice mutual honor and to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Conclusion: A Sacred Respect

Mutual honor in Catholic marriage is a sacred respect that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples practice mutual honor, they are not just being nice to each other but are imitating God's love and respect for each person.

As we reflect on mutual honor in marriage, let us remember that this honor is not just a human practice but is a divine love that requires God's grace. When we practice mutual honor, we are building a marriage that is based on respect, dignity, and the grace of God.
