Why Catholic Weddings Are Celebrated in Church (Matthew 18:20)

2025-01-27

The Presence of Christ

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because of the belief in Christ's promise: "For where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I in the midst of them" (Matthew 18:20). When a couple exchanges their marriage vows in the church, they are not just making a commitment to each other but are entering into a sacred covenant in the presence of Christ Himself. This divine presence gives the wedding ceremony its sacred character and its spiritual significance.

The church is not just a building but is the house of God, a sacred space where the community of believers gathers to worship, pray, and celebrate the sacraments. When couples choose to be married in the church, they are acknowledging that their marriage is not just a private affair but is a sacred event that involves the entire Church community and, most importantly, Christ Himself.

The Sacramental Nature of Marriage

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because marriage is a sacrament, a sacred sign instituted by Christ that confers grace. As a sacrament, marriage is not just a human institution but is a divine mystery that reflects the relationship between Christ and the Church. The church is the appropriate place to celebrate this sacrament because it is where the Church community gathers to worship and to receive God's grace.

The sacramental nature of marriage means that it is not just a legal contract or a social ceremony but is a sacred event that involves God's presence and participation. When couples are married in the church, they are receiving the grace they need to fulfill their vocation as husband and wife. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is a continuing source of strength and help throughout their married life.

The Community of Faith

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because marriage involves the entire community of faith. The couple is not just making a commitment to each other but is also making a commitment to the Church community. The community, in turn, commits to supporting the couple in their married life and in their journey toward holiness.

This community involvement is expressed in many ways during the wedding ceremony - through the prayers of the faithful, through the presence of family and friends, through the participation of the priest or deacon, and through the blessing of the community. This community support is essential for helping couples to fulfill their vocation and to grow in holiness together.

The Liturgical Celebration

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because they are liturgical celebrations that follow the Church's ritual and tradition. The wedding liturgy is not just a ceremony but is a sacred ritual that has been developed over centuries to express the Church's understanding of marriage as a sacrament.

This liturgical celebration includes specific prayers, readings from Scripture, the exchange of vows, the blessing of rings, and the nuptial blessing. Each of these elements has theological and spiritual significance and helps to express the Church's understanding of marriage as a sacred covenant between husband and wife, witnessed by God and the Church community.

The Witness of the Church

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because the Church serves as a witness to the couple's marriage vows. When couples exchange their vows in the church, they are not just making a promise to each other but are making a sacred vow that the Church witnesses and upholds. This witness gives the marriage its sacred character and its permanence.

The Church's witness is not just symbolic but is a real participation in the couple's marriage. The Church community commits to supporting the couple in their married life, to praying for them, and to helping them to grow in holiness. This support is essential for helping couples to fulfill their vocation and to remain faithful to their vows.

The Blessing of God

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because couples seek God's blessing on their marriage. The church is the appropriate place to seek this blessing because it is where the community gathers to worship God and to receive His grace. When couples are married in the church, they are asking for God's blessing and protection on their marriage.

This blessing is not just a formality but is a real spiritual reality that helps couples to fulfill their vocation. God's blessing provides couples with the grace they need to love each other as Christ loves the Church, to remain faithful to their vows, and to grow in holiness together. This blessing is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage.

The Sacred Space

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because the church is a sacred space that has been consecrated for worship and prayer. This sacred space provides an appropriate environment for celebrating the sacrament of marriage. The church's architecture, artwork, and liturgical furnishings all help to create an atmosphere of reverence and awe that is appropriate for such a sacred event.

This sacred space also helps couples to focus on the spiritual dimension of their marriage. When couples are married in the church, they are reminded that their marriage is not just about their personal happiness but is about serving God and helping to build up His kingdom. This spiritual focus helps couples to keep their priorities straight and to focus on what is truly important.

Conclusion: A Sacred Celebration

Catholic weddings are celebrated in church because marriage is a sacred sacrament that involves God's presence and participation. The church provides the appropriate setting for celebrating this sacrament, for receiving God's blessing, and for involving the community of faith in the couple's marriage.

As we reflect on why Catholic weddings are celebrated in church, let us remember that this celebration is not just about tradition or aesthetics but is about acknowledging the sacred nature of marriage and seeking God's blessing on this important step in the couple's journey toward holiness.
