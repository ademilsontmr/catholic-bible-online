Holiness Through Perseverance in Fasting and Abstinence

2024-12-20

Introduction: Fasting as Spiritual Discipline

Holiness through perseverance in fasting and abstinence is about recognizing that these practices are not just religious obligations or ways to show piety, but powerful spiritual disciplines that help us to grow in holiness and to become more like Christ. Fasting and abstinence are not about punishing ourselves or making ourselves miserable, but about learning to control our appetites and desires, and about recognizing that we are not slaves to our physical needs and wants. When we practice these disciplines with perseverance, we're not just following rules, but allowing God to transform us through these acts of self-denial.

Many people find fasting and abstinence difficult because they're not used to denying themselves or because they don't understand the spiritual benefits of these practices. However, fasting and abstinence are not about deprivation for its own sake, but about creating space for God to work in our lives and about learning to rely on Him rather than on material comforts and pleasures. These practices help us to develop self-control, to grow in gratitude for God's gifts, and to become more aware of our dependence on Him.

The Spiritual Benefits of Fasting

Fasting and abstinence provide numerous opportunities for spiritual growth. They teach us self-control as we learn to say no to our immediate desires and to choose what's good for us in the long run. They help us to grow in gratitude as we become more aware of the many gifts God has given us and more appreciative of the simple pleasures of life. They teach us to rely on God rather than on material comforts, helping us to recognize that our true happiness comes from our relationship with Him. They also help us to identify with the poor and hungry, making us more compassionate and more willing to help those in need.

Conclusion: Fasting as Transformation

Perseverance in fasting and abstinence leads to holiness because it helps us to develop the virtues of self-control, gratitude, and compassion, and to become more aware of our dependence on God. When we practice these disciplines with love and humility, we're not just following religious rules, but allowing God to transform us through these acts of self-denial. We become more disciplined, more grateful, more compassionate, and more holy. These practices become a source of strength and grace that helps us to grow closer to God.

Prayer for Fasting and Abstinence

'Lord Jesus, help us to practice fasting and abstinence with perseverance and love, recognizing that these disciplines help us to grow in holiness and to become more like You. Give us the grace to control our appetites and desires, and to rely on You rather than on material comforts. May our fasting and abstinence lead us to greater holiness and union with You. In Your name, Amen.'
