Catholic Marriage and the Gift of Fidelity (Luke 16:10)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Fidelity

The gift of fidelity in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Luke 16:10: "He who is faithful in a very little is faithful also in much; and he who is dishonest in a very little is dishonest also in much." This passage shows that fidelity is not just about big decisions but is about being faithful in the small, everyday aspects of married life. This faithfulness in little things builds the foundation for faithfulness in the big things.

This biblical foundation shows that fidelity is not just about avoiding infidelity but is about being trustworthy, reliable, and committed to the good of one's spouse in all aspects of the relationship. When couples are faithful to each other in the small things, they build a relationship that can withstand the challenges of life.

Fidelity as a Gift from God

Fidelity in marriage is not just a human virtue but is a gift from God. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to remain faithful to each other and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

This gift of fidelity is not just for the couple's own benefit but is meant to be shared with others. When couples are faithful to each other, they become a sign of God's faithful love and a source of hope for others. This witness is particularly important in a world that often experiences brokenness and division.

The Daily Practice of Fidelity

Fidelity in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being faithful in communication, being honest about feelings and needs, being reliable in keeping promises, and being committed to the good of one's spouse. These daily practices of fidelity build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of fidelity also includes being faithful in prayer, in participation in the sacraments, and in living according to God's will. When couples are faithful to God together, they are better able to be faithful to each other. This faithfulness to God becomes the foundation for their faithfulness to each other.

Fidelity and Trust

Fidelity in marriage creates an environment of trust and security that is essential for building a strong and lasting relationship. When couples know that they can trust each other completely, they are able to be vulnerable with each other, to share their deepest thoughts and feelings, and to grow together in intimacy and love.

This trust is not just about avoiding infidelity but is about being reliable, honest, and committed to each other in all aspects of the relationship. When couples are faithful to each other, they create a safe space where love can flourish and grow. This trust becomes the foundation upon which their relationship is built.

Fidelity and Growth

Fidelity in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples are faithful to each other, they are growing in virtues like patience, perseverance, and love. They are also learning to see each other as God sees them and to love each other with the same unconditional love that God has for them.

This growth in virtue is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When couples grow in virtue, they are better able to love each other, to communicate effectively, to resolve conflicts, and to fulfill their vocation. This growth also helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

Fidelity and the Mission of Marriage

Fidelity in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples are faithful to each other, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's faithful love in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often values temporary relationships over lasting commitments. When couples remain faithful to each other, they show the world that fidelity is possible and that it brings joy and fulfillment. This witness helps to build a more stable and healthy society.

The Grace of Fidelity

Fidelity in marriage is not something that couples can do on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to remain faithful to each other and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can remain faithful to each other and build a strong and lasting marriage based on love and trust.

Conclusion: A Precious Gift

Fidelity in Catholic marriage is a precious gift that reflects God's faithful love for His people. This fidelity is not just about avoiding infidelity but is about being trustworthy, reliable, and committed to the good of one's spouse throughout their married life. When couples remain faithful to each other, they become witnesses to God's faithful love and a source of blessing for the world.

As we reflect on the gift of fidelity in marriage, let us remember that this fidelity is not just a personal commitment but is a sacred vow that God Himself witnesses and upholds. Let us also remember that with God's grace, any couple can remain faithful to each other and build a strong and lasting relationship.
