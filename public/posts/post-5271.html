Overcoming Pride in the Relationship (Proverbs 16:18)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Overcoming Pride

The practice of overcoming pride in the relationship in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Proverbs 16:18: "Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall." This passage shows that pride is not just a minor flaw but is a serious obstacle to building strong and lasting relationships. When couples work to overcome pride in their relationship, they are not just being humble but are actively removing a major obstacle to love and harmony in their marriage.

This biblical foundation shows that overcoming pride in the relationship is not just about being modest but is about actively recognizing the destructive nature of pride and being willing to work to overcome it. When couples work to overcome pride, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This overcoming is not about being perfect but is about being willing to recognize and work on the pride that can damage their relationship.

Pride as a Barrier to Love

Pride in the relationship serves as a major barrier to love and harmony in Catholic marriage. Just as pride can destroy individuals, so too can it destroy marriages by preventing couples from truly loving and serving each other. This pride is not about having healthy self-esteem but is about having an inflated sense of self-importance that prevents genuine love and service.

When couples work to overcome pride in their relationship, they are not just being humble but are actively removing a major obstacle to love and harmony. They are willing to recognize their own pride and its destructive effects, to be willing to admit when they are wrong, to be willing to apologize and seek forgiveness, to be willing to serve each other, and to be willing to put each other's needs before their own. This overcoming becomes a powerful force for good in their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Overcoming Pride

Overcoming pride in the relationship is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being willing to admit when one is wrong, being willing to apologize sincerely, being willing to serve one's spouse, being willing to listen to one's spouse's concerns, and being willing to put one's spouse's needs before one's own. These daily practices of overcoming pride build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of overcoming pride includes being willing to ask for help when needed, being willing to accept constructive criticism, being willing to celebrate one's spouse's successes, being willing to be patient with one's spouse's weaknesses, and being willing to be a source of encouragement and support. When couples practice overcoming pride in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Overcoming Pride and Communication

Overcoming pride in the relationship is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate humbly with each other, they are better able to overcome pride and to build up their relationship. Humble communication helps couples to express their thoughts and feelings in a way that is respectful and loving.

This communication for overcoming pride includes being willing to listen attentively to one's spouse's concerns, being willing to speak gently and kindly, being willing to avoid criticism and sarcasm, being willing to seek understanding rather than being right, and being willing to express disagreement in a respectful way. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are strengthening their humility and their relationship.

Overcoming Pride and Growth

Overcoming pride in the relationship is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples work to overcome pride, they are growing in virtues like humility, patience, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through overcoming pride includes growing in humility as they learn to recognize and overcome their pride, growing in patience as they learn to bear with each other's weaknesses, growing in love as they learn to love each other more selflessly, growing in selflessness as they learn to put each other first, and growing in holiness as they learn to love each other as Christ loves the Church. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Humble Love

Overcoming pride in the relationship in Catholic marriage is a humble love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples work to overcome pride, they are not just being humble but are actively removing a major obstacle to love and harmony in their marriage.

As we reflect on overcoming pride in the relationship, let us remember that this overcoming is not just a human effort but is a divine work that requires God's grace. When we overcome pride, we are building a marriage that is based on humility, love, and the grace of God.
