Friendship and Faith on Campus — "Do not be deceived: bad company ruins good morals." (1 Corinthians 15:33)

2025-01-27

Introduction: The Importance of Faithful Friendships

Friendship and faith on campus represents one of the most important aspects of student life that can either strengthen or weaken our spiritual journey. St. Paul's warning in 1 Corinthians 15:33, "Do not be deceived: bad company ruins good morals," provides the foundation for understanding how to build friendships that support our faith while navigating the challenges of campus life.

When we understand friendship and faith on campus, we recognize that the people we choose to spend time with have a significant impact on our spiritual growth and moral development. This understanding helps us to see that building faithful friendships is not just about social connection but about supporting each other in our journey of faith.

Understanding the Biblical Foundation of Friendship

Friendship and faith on campus begins with understanding the biblical foundation of friendship. The Bible teaches us that true friendship is based on shared values and mutual support in living according to God's will. Proverbs 27:17 tells us that "As iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another," emphasizing the importance of friendships that help us grow in virtue.

This biblical foundation shows that friendship and faith on campus is about building relationships that help us to grow spiritually and morally. When we understand this, we can be more intentional about the friendships we cultivate and the influence we allow others to have on our lives.

Finding Faithful Friends on Campus

Friendship and faith on campus includes learning how to find and build relationships with other students who share our faith and values. This might involve joining campus ministry groups, participating in faith-based activities, or simply being open about our faith in a way that attracts like-minded friends. The key is to be intentional about seeking out friendships that will support our spiritual growth.

This intentional approach shows that friendship and faith on campus is about actively seeking relationships that will help us to grow in our faith. When we are intentional about our friendships, we can build a support network that helps us to navigate the challenges of campus life while maintaining our spiritual values.

Navigating Friendships with Non-Believers

Friendship and faith on campus involves learning how to maintain friendships with people who don't share our faith while staying true to our values. This requires wisdom, compassion, and the ability to be a witness to our faith through our actions and words. The goal is not to isolate ourselves but to be a positive influence while protecting our own spiritual growth.

This navigation shows that friendship and faith on campus is about finding the right balance between being open to all people and protecting our spiritual well-being. When we approach these relationships with wisdom and love, we can be a positive influence while maintaining our own faith and values.

Building a Faith Community on Campus

Friendship and faith on campus includes working to build a faith community that supports all students in their spiritual journey. This might involve participating in or helping to organize campus ministry activities, Bible studies, prayer groups, or other faith-based events. The goal is to create an environment where students can grow in their faith together.

This community building shows that friendship and faith on campus is about creating an environment where faith can flourish. When we work to build a strong faith community, we can create opportunities for all students to grow spiritually and build meaningful friendships.

Conclusion: Friendships That Last

Friendship and faith on campus reveals that the friendships we build during our college years can have a lasting impact on our spiritual journey. When we choose our friends wisely and build relationships based on shared faith and values, we create a foundation for spiritual growth that can last a lifetime.

As we work to build friendships and faith on campus, let us remember that the people we choose to spend time with will have a significant impact on our spiritual growth. When we build friendships based on faith and shared values, we create a support network that helps us to grow both academically and spiritually.
