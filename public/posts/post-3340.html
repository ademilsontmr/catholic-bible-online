The Transfiguration as a Revelation of Jesus' Glory

Jesus' Transfiguration reveals His divine glory and shows that He is truly the Son of God, giving His disciples a glimpse of His heavenly splendor. Catholic tradition sees the Transfiguration as essential for understanding that Jesus is not just a human teacher but the divine Son of God who shares in the glory of the Father. The Transfiguration is not just about a miraculous event but about revealing Jesus' true nature. This understanding shows that the Transfiguration is not just about following traditions but about understanding Jesus' divinity. The Transfiguration helps believers understand Jesus' divine glory.

The Biblical Foundation

The Transfiguration is recorded in the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, and Luke, showing its importance in revealing Jesus' divine nature to His closest disciples. Catholic tradition sees this biblical foundation as essential for understanding the historical and theological significance of this event. The Bible shows how Jesus' face shone like the sun and His clothes became dazzling white, revealing His divine glory. This biblical foundation shows that the Transfiguration is not just a Church tradition but has deep roots in God's revelation. The Transfiguration helps believers understand the biblical foundation of Jesus' glory.
