Teaching Children to Love God Above All – Matthew 22:37

2025-01-27

Introduction: The Greatest Commandment

Teaching children to love God above all represents one of the most fundamental aspects of Catholic parenting. Jesus' teaching in Matthew 22:37, "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind," provides the foundation for understanding how to teach children to love God with all their heart, soul, and mind. This teaching involves recognizing that loving God above all else is the foundation of a good and holy life and that parents have a sacred responsibility to help their children develop this love.

When we understand teaching children to love God above all, we recognize that this is not just about teaching religious practices but about helping children develop a deep and personal relationship with God. This understanding helps us to see that teaching children to love God is about helping them understand that God loves them and wants to be in relationship with them.

Understanding the Biblical Foundation of Loving God

Teaching children to love God above all begins with understanding the biblical foundation of this commandment and its importance in the life of faith. The Bible repeatedly emphasizes the importance of loving God above all else, from the Old Testament's emphasis on serving God alone to Jesus' teachings about the greatest commandment. Scripture consistently shows that loving God is the foundation of all other virtues and relationships.

This biblical foundation shows that teaching children to love God above all is about recognizing that this is God's plan for human happiness and fulfillment. When we understand this, we can approach this teaching with reverence and dedication.

Modeling Love for God

Teaching children to love God above all includes modeling our own love for God in our daily lives. This involves showing our children through our actions and attitudes that God is the most important person in our lives, making time for prayer and worship, speaking about God with love and reverence, and demonstrating that our relationship with God informs all our other relationships. The key is to show our children what it means to love God.

This modeling shows that teaching children to love God above all is about being living examples of what it means to love God. When we model love for God, we provide our children with the most powerful form of instruction about loving God.

Creating Opportunities to Experience God's Love

Teaching children to love God above all involves creating opportunities for children to experience God's love and presence. This might involve praying together as a family, participating in the sacraments, reading Bible stories that show God's love, or finding ways to help children experience God's love in their daily lives. The key is to help children understand that God loves them and wants to be in relationship with them.

This experience creation shows that teaching children to love God above all is about helping children develop a personal relationship with God. When we create these opportunities, we help our children understand that loving God is about being in relationship with Him.

Conclusion: The Foundation of All Virtues

Teaching children to love God above all reveals that this is the foundation of all other virtues and relationships. When we teach our children to love God with all their heart, soul, and mind, we give them the foundation they need to live good and holy lives.

As we work to teach our children to love God above all, let us remember that this is the greatest commandment and the foundation of all other teachings. When we teach our children to love God with all their heart, soul, and mind, we honor God and help our children develop the foundation they need for a life of faith and virtue.
