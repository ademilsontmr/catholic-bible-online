Why Marriage Is Indissoluble in Catholic Teaching (Mark 10:9)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation

The Catholic teaching on the indissolubility of marriage is firmly rooted in Scripture, particularly in the words of Jesus Himself. In Mark 10:9, Jesus states clearly: "What therefore God has joined together, let not man separate." This teaching is also found in Matthew 19:6, where Jesus adds: "So they are no longer two but one flesh. What therefore God has joined together, let not man separate." These words of Christ establish the fundamental principle that marriage, once validly contracted, cannot be dissolved by human authority.

This teaching is not just a human law or a Church regulation but is a divine law that reflects the nature of God Himself. Just as God is faithful to His covenant with His people and never abandons them, so too is marriage meant to be a permanent and indissoluble bond. The indissolubility of marriage is not a burden but is a reflection of the faithfulness and permanence of God's love.

The Sacramental Nature of Marriage

The indissolubility of marriage is connected to its sacramental nature. As a sacrament, marriage is not just a human contract but is a sacred sign instituted by Christ that confers grace. The sacrament of marriage creates a bond between husband and wife that is not just physical or emotional but is spiritual and supernatural.

This sacramental bond is created by God Himself and cannot be broken by human will. Just as the sacraments of Baptism and Holy Orders leave an indelible mark on the soul, so too does the sacrament of marriage create an indissoluble bond between the spouses. This bond endures even if the couple separates or if one spouse dies, and it cannot be dissolved by any human authority.

The Reflection of Christ's Love

The indissolubility of marriage reflects the indissoluble love that Christ has for the Church. Just as Christ remains faithful to the Church despite her sins and weaknesses, so too are husband and wife called to remain faithful to each other despite their difficulties and failures. This faithfulness is not just a moral obligation but is a participation in the mystery of Christ's love.

When couples remain faithful to their marriage vows, even in difficult times, they become a sign of Christ's faithful love for the Church. Their faithfulness becomes a witness to the world of what love looks like when it is lived according to God's plan. This witness is particularly important in a world that often treats relationships as temporary and disposable.

The Good of the Spouses

The indissolubility of marriage is not just for the good of society or the Church but is primarily for the good of the spouses themselves. When couples know that their marriage cannot be broken, they are more likely to work through their problems and to grow together. This permanence gives couples the security they need to love each other freely and completely.

Without the security of permanence, couples might be tempted to give up on their relationship when difficulties arise. The indissolubility of marriage encourages couples to invest in their relationship, to work through their problems, and to grow in love and holiness together. This investment leads to deeper intimacy and greater happiness.

The Good of Children

The indissolubility of marriage is also for the good of children. Children need the security and stability that comes from knowing that their parents' relationship is permanent. When children see their parents working through difficulties and remaining committed to each other, they learn important lessons about love, commitment, and perseverance.

Children who grow up in stable, permanent marriages are more likely to develop healthy relationships themselves. They learn that love is not just about feelings but is about commitment and faithfulness. They also learn that difficulties in relationships can be overcome through love, patience, and perseverance.

The Good of Society

The indissolubility of marriage is also for the good of society as a whole. Stable, permanent marriages provide the foundation for strong families, which are the building blocks of society. When marriages are stable and permanent, families are stronger, communities are more cohesive, and society is more peaceful.

Permanent marriages also provide a model of faithfulness and commitment that society needs. In a world that often values temporary relationships and immediate gratification, permanent marriages stand as a witness to the value of commitment, faithfulness, and long-term relationships.

The Call to Growth and Conversion

The indissolubility of marriage is not just about staying together but is about growing together. When couples know that they cannot separate, they are more likely to work on their relationship and to grow in love and holiness. This growth often involves conversion - changing one's attitudes, behaviors, and priorities for the good of the relationship.

This call to growth and conversion is not a burden but is a gift. It helps couples to become better people and to grow closer to God. Through the challenges and difficulties of married life, couples learn important virtues like patience, forgiveness, self-sacrifice, and love.

The Grace to Remain Faithful

God does not ask couples to remain faithful to their marriage vows without giving them the grace they need to do so. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive specific graces that help them to remain faithful to each other and to grow in love and holiness.

These graces are available throughout the couple's married life, particularly during difficult times. Couples can access these graces through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can remain faithful to their marriage vows and grow in love and holiness.

Conclusion: A Gift, Not a Burden

The indissolubility of marriage is not a burden but is a gift that reflects God's faithful love for humanity. This permanence provides security, encourages growth, and serves as a witness to the world of what love looks like when it is lived according to God's plan.

As we reflect on the indissolubility of marriage, let us remember that this teaching is not just a rule to follow but is a truth that helps us to understand and experience the depth of God's love. Let us also remember that with God's grace, any couple can remain faithful to their marriage vows and grow in love and holiness together.
