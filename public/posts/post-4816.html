Becoming Holy Through Works of Mercy

The works of mercy are concrete ways of showing love for God and neighbor, and they are powerful means of growing in holiness. By practicing both the corporal works of mercy (feeding the hungry, giving drink to the thirsty, etc.) and the spiritual works of mercy (instructing the ignorant, counseling the doubtful, etc.), you can grow in charity and become more like Christ. Understanding how to become holy through works of mercy helps you recognize that holiness is not just about personal piety but about loving service to others. This understanding is deeply rooted in Catholic teaching, where charity is seen as the greatest of all virtues and the essence of holiness.

The Biblical Foundation: Whatever You Did for the Least

The Bible teaches us about the importance of works of mercy. Matthew 25:31-46 presents the parable of the sheep and the goats, where Jesus identifies Himself with the poor and needy and tells us that whatever we do for the least of His brothers and sisters, we do for Him. Additionally, James 2:14-17 teaches us that faith without works is dead, and 1 John 4:20 tells us that we cannot love God whom we do not see if we do not love our neighbor whom we do see. Understanding this biblical foundation helps you recognize that works of mercy are essential for authentic Christian living.

Understanding the Works of Mercy

The works of mercy are divided into two categories: corporal works of mercy, which address physical needs, and spiritual works of mercy, which address spiritual and emotional needs. The corporal works include feeding the hungry, giving drink to the thirsty, clothing the naked, sheltering the homeless, visiting the sick, visiting the imprisoned, and burying the dead. The spiritual works include instructing the ignorant, counseling the doubtful, admonishing sinners, bearing wrongs patiently, forgiving offenses willingly, comforting the afflicted, and praying for the living and the dead. Understanding the works of mercy helps you recognize the many ways you can serve others.

How Works of Mercy Make Us Holy

Works of mercy make us holy in many ways. They help us grow in charity and love for God and neighbor, they conform us more closely to Christ who came to serve, they help us overcome selfishness and self-centeredness, they provide opportunities to practice virtue, and they help us recognize Christ in the poor and needy. Works of mercy also help us develop compassion, patience, and other virtues that are essential for holiness. Understanding how works of mercy make us holy helps you recognize their importance for spiritual growth.

Practicing the Corporal Works of Mercy

Practicing the corporal works of mercy involves meeting the physical needs of others. This might include donating food to food banks, volunteering at soup kitchens, donating clothing to those in need, supporting homeless shelters, visiting the sick in hospitals or nursing homes, ministering to prisoners, and participating in funeral ministries. These works help you serve Christ in the poor and needy. Understanding how to practice the corporal works of mercy helps you find concrete ways to serve others.

Practicing the Spiritual Works of Mercy

Practicing the spiritual works of mercy involves meeting the spiritual and emotional needs of others. This might include teaching others about the faith, providing guidance and counsel to those who are struggling, gently correcting those who are in error, being patient with difficult people, forgiving those who have hurt you, comforting those who are suffering, and praying for others. These works help you serve Christ by caring for the spiritual needs of others. Understanding how to practice the spiritual works of mercy helps you serve others in their spiritual journey.

Overcoming Obstacles to Works of Mercy

There are many obstacles to practicing works of mercy, including busyness and lack of time, fear of getting involved with difficult situations, lack of resources or skills, and the tendency to focus on personal needs rather than the needs of others. Overcoming these obstacles requires prayer, planning, and a commitment to serving others. Understanding these obstacles helps you recognize what might prevent you from practicing works of mercy and develop strategies for overcoming them.

The Rewards of Works of Mercy

Practicing works of mercy brings many rewards, including deeper relationship with God, greater love for others, increased joy and satisfaction, growth in virtue and holiness, and the promise of eternal life. Jesus tells us that those who practice works of mercy will inherit the kingdom prepared for them from the foundation of the world. Understanding the rewards of works of mercy helps you recognize why these practices are worth the effort and commitment.

Conclusion: Living Mercy

Becoming holy through works of mercy is about recognizing that holiness involves loving service to others and that works of mercy are powerful means of growing in charity and becoming more like Christ. By understanding the works of mercy, recognizing how they make us holy, practicing both corporal and spiritual works of mercy, overcoming obstacles, and recognizing their rewards, you can grow in holiness through loving service to others. Understanding this approach helps you recognize that holiness is not just about personal piety but about living the love of Christ in concrete ways.
