Baptism and the Anointing with Sacred Chrism (2 Corinthians 1:21)

2025-02-02

Introduction: The Sacred Anointing

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? This question addresses one of the most important aspects of the sacrament of baptism. 2 Corinthians 1:21 provides the biblical foundation for this understanding: 'But it is God who establishes us with you in Christ and has anointed us.' This verse reveals the profound significance of anointing in the Christian life and the way it is connected to baptism.

The Catholic Church teaches that the anointing with sacred chrism is not just a ritual but is a powerful sacramental action that configures the baptized person to Christ and marks them as belonging to Him. Through this anointing, the person is consecrated to God and given a share in Christ's priestly, prophetic, and kingly mission. This anointing is a sign of the Holy Spirit's presence and work in the life of the baptized person.

The Context of 2 Corinthians 1:21

To understand the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism, we must examine the context of 2 Corinthians 1:21. Saint Paul is writing about the way God establishes and anoints us in Christ. The verse reads: 'But it is God who establishes us with you in Christ and has anointed us.'

This verse shows that anointing is a divine action that establishes us in Christ and marks us as belonging to Him. This anointing is not just symbolic but is a real spiritual reality that affects our relationship with God and our mission in the world. The anointing with sacred chrism in baptism is the visible sign of this divine anointing.

This context shows that anointing is essential for our establishment in Christ and our mission as Christians.

The Sacred Chrism

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? First, sacred chrism is a special oil that has been blessed by the bishop and is used for anointing in the sacraments. This oil is not just any oil but is a sacred substance that carries the blessing and power of the Church.

Through the blessing of the bishop, the chrism becomes a powerful sacramental that carries the grace of the Holy Spirit and serves as a visible sign of the invisible grace of anointing. This chrism is used specifically for anointing in baptism, confirmation, and holy orders, showing the connection between these sacraments.

The sacred chrism shows that the anointing is not just a human action but is a divine action that carries the power of the Holy Spirit.

The Configuration to Christ

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? Through the anointing with sacred chrism, the baptized person is configured to Christ, who is the Anointed One (the Christ). This configuration is not just symbolic but is a real spiritual reality that makes the person like Christ and enables them to participate in His mission.

Through this anointing, the person is made a member of the royal priesthood of believers and is enabled to participate in Christ's priestly, prophetic, and kingly mission. This configuration is essential for the Christian life and is established through the anointing with sacred chrism.

The configuration to Christ shows that the anointing is essential for our participation in Christ's mission.

The Gift of the Holy Spirit

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? The anointing with sacred chrism symbolizes and confers the gift of the Holy Spirit. Through this anointing, the Holy Spirit comes to dwell within the baptized person and becomes their guide and helper in the Christian life.

This gift is not just symbolic but is a real spiritual reality. Through the anointing, the person receives the Holy Spirit, who enables them to live as children of God and to participate in the mission of the Church. This gift is essential for the Christian life and is conferred through the anointing with sacred chrism.

The gift of the Holy Spirit shows that the anointing is essential for our spiritual life and mission.

The Royal Priesthood

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? Through the anointing with sacred chrism, the baptized person becomes part of the royal priesthood of believers, as described in 1 Peter 2:9. This priesthood enables the person to participate in the worship of the Church and to offer spiritual sacrifices.

This priesthood is not just symbolic but is a real spiritual reality. Through the anointing, the person is enabled to participate in the liturgy, to offer prayers and sacrifices, and to participate in the mission of the Church. This priesthood is a participation in Christ's own priesthood and is established through the anointing with sacred chrism.

The royal priesthood shows that the anointing is essential for our participation in the worship and mission of the Church.

The Prophetic Mission

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? Through the anointing with sacred chrism, the baptized person is given a share in Christ's prophetic mission. This mission involves proclaiming the truth of the Gospel and bearing witness to Christ in the world.

This mission is not just for a select few but for all baptized Christians, who are called to be witnesses to Christ and to proclaim the Gospel through their words and actions. Through the anointing, the person is given the grace and the calling to fulfill this prophetic mission.

The prophetic mission shows that the anointing is essential for our mission as witnesses to Christ.

The Kingly Mission

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? Through the anointing with sacred chrism, the baptized person is given a share in Christ's kingly mission. This mission involves ruling over creation and working for the coming of God's kingdom.

This mission involves both personal and social transformation, as the person works to become more like Christ and to transform the world according to God's will. Through the anointing, the person is given the grace and the calling to fulfill this kingly mission.

The kingly mission shows that the anointing is essential for our mission of transformation.

The Seal of the Spirit

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? The anointing with sacred chrism is a seal that marks the baptized person as belonging to Christ and as having received the Holy Spirit. This seal is not just external but is a spiritual reality that identifies the person as a child of God.

This seal is a sign of God's ownership and protection. Through the anointing, the person is marked as belonging to Christ and is placed under His protection. This seal is a sign of the person's permanent relationship with Christ and their membership in the Church.

The seal of the Spirit shows that the anointing is essential for our identity as children of God.

Conclusion: The Sacred Anointing

What is the significance of anointing with sacred chrism in baptism? Through the anointing with sacred chrism, the baptized person is configured to Christ, receives the gift of the Holy Spirit, becomes part of the royal priesthood, and is given a share in Christ's prophetic and kingly mission. This anointing is not just symbolic but is a powerful sacramental action that establishes our identity and mission as Christians.

As we reflect on 2 Corinthians 1:21 and the anointing with sacred chrism, let us be grateful for the gift of baptism and the way it anoints us as children of God. May we always remember that through baptism, we have been anointed with sacred chrism and are called to fulfill the mission that Christ has given us.
