How the Saints' Writings Can Inspire Hope in Personal Struggles

2025-02-02

What are the Saints' Writings?

The saints' writings are the spiritual works, letters, diaries, and theological texts written by men and women who have been recognized by the Catholic Church for their extraordinary holiness and virtue. These writings include works by saints such as Saint Augustine, Saint Teresa of Ávila, Saint John of the Cross, Saint Therese of Lisieux, Saint Francis de Sales, and many others. These writings are not just historical documents, but living testimonies of faith that continue to speak to our hearts today. They offer us insights into how the saints faced their own struggles, how they grew in holiness, and how they found hope and peace in God.

The saints' writings are particularly valuable because they were written by people who experienced the same human struggles and challenges that we face today. The saints were not perfect people who lived in a different world, but real men and women who faced illness, loss, doubt, temptation, and suffering. Their writings show us how they dealt with these challenges and how they found hope and meaning in their struggles. Reading their works helps us to see that holiness is possible for ordinary people and that God can work through our difficulties to bring us closer to Him.

Why Do Saints' Writings Inspire Hope?

The saints' writings inspire hope because they show us that others have faced similar struggles and have found ways to overcome them through faith and trust in God. When we read about how Saint Therese of Lisieux dealt with her spiritual dryness, or how Saint John of the Cross endured his dark night of the soul, we are reminded that we are not alone in our struggles. The saints' experiences show us that difficult times are not signs of God's absence, but opportunities for growth and deeper relationship with Him.

The saints' writings also inspire hope because they reveal the saints' deep love for God and their trust in His goodness and mercy. Even in their darkest moments, the saints never lost faith in God's love for them. Their writings show us how they found comfort in prayer, how they trusted in God's plan even when they didn't understand it, and how they found meaning in their suffering by uniting it with Christ's suffering. These examples give us hope that we too can find peace and meaning in our struggles.

How to Use Saints' Writings for Personal Struggles

Using the saints' writings to find hope in personal struggles requires an open heart and a willingness to learn from their experiences. One effective approach is to choose a saint who faced similar challenges to your own and to read their writings with prayer and reflection. For example, if you are dealing with illness, you might read the writings of Saint Therese of Lisieux, who suffered from tuberculosis. If you are struggling with doubt, you might read Saint Augustine's Confessions, which describes his own journey from doubt to faith.

Another way to use the saints' writings is to read them regularly as part of your spiritual reading, allowing their wisdom to guide you in your daily life. You might choose a particular saint's work to read slowly and prayerfully, taking time to reflect on how their insights apply to your own situation. You can also use the saints' writings as a source of prayer, using their words to express your own thoughts and feelings to God.

What to Pray For

When reading the saints' writings for hope in personal struggles, focus on several key areas. Pray for understanding of the saints' experiences, asking God to help you see how their struggles and their responses to them can guide you in your own difficulties. Pray for the grace to learn from the saints' example, asking God to help you apply their wisdom to your own life and to grow in holiness as they did.

Pray for the courage to face your struggles with faith and trust, asking God to help you follow the saints' example of trusting in Him even when circumstances are difficult. Pray for the wisdom to see God's hand at work in your struggles, asking Him to help you find meaning and purpose in your difficulties. Remember to also pray for the saints' intercession, asking them to pray for you and to help you find the hope and strength you need.

Frequency of Reading

Reading the saints' writings should be regular and consistent, ideally daily or several times a week, to ensure that you are constantly reminded of their example and wisdom. Many people find it helpful to begin each day with a short reading from a saint's work, asking God to help them apply the saint's wisdom to their day. You might read a few paragraphs or a short chapter, taking time to reflect on what you've read and how it applies to your life.

It's also helpful to read the saints' writings at specific times when you are facing particular struggles or challenges. You might turn to a specific saint's work when you are feeling discouraged, when you are facing a difficult decision, or when you need guidance in your spiritual life. The key is to make the saints' writings a regular part of your spiritual life and to allow their wisdom to guide you in your journey of faith.

Overcoming Reading Challenges

Many people face challenges in reading the saints' writings, such as finding the language difficult to understand, feeling disconnected from the historical context, or not knowing where to start. These challenges are normal and don't mean that the saints' writings are not for you. Prayer helps us overcome these challenges and to find meaning and guidance in the saints' works.

When you're facing challenges in reading the saints' writings, bring these challenges to God in prayer. Ask Him to help you understand what you're reading and to see how it applies to your life. You might also find it helpful to use modern translations or commentaries that can help you understand the historical context and the meaning of the texts. Remember that the saints want to help you and that God will guide you to the wisdom you need if you ask Him and remain open to His guidance.

The Power of Saints' Intercession

One of the most beautiful aspects of reading the saints' writings is that it helps us to develop a relationship with the saints and to ask for their intercession. When we read about a saint's life and struggles, we come to know them as friends and companions on our spiritual journey. We can ask them to pray for us and to help us in our own struggles, knowing that they understand what we're going through and that they want to help us.

The saints' intercession is particularly powerful because they are close to God and because they have a special concern for those who are struggling. When we ask the saints to pray for us, we are asking people who have experienced God's love and mercy to intercede for us with Him. This intercession can bring us comfort, hope, and the grace we need to face our difficulties with faith and trust. Trust that the saints want to help you and that their prayers can bring you the hope and strength you need in your personal struggles.
