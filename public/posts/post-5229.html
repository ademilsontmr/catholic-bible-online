Let Love Be Genuine (Romans 12:9)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Genuine Love

The call for genuine love in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Romans 12:9: "Let love be genuine. Abhor what is evil; hold fast to what is good." This passage shows that love in marriage is not just about feelings or words but is about authentic actions and genuine commitment. When couples practice genuine love, they are not just saying they love each other but are showing it through their actions and their commitment to each other's good.

This biblical foundation shows that genuine love in marriage is not just about being romantic or sentimental but is about being authentic and real. When couples practice genuine love, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This love is not about being perfect but is about being honest and authentic in their love for each other.

Genuine Love as Authentic Action

Genuine love in marriage is not just about feelings but is about authentic actions that demonstrate love. This love includes being willing to put one's spouse's needs before one's own needs, being willing to sacrifice for one's spouse's good, being willing to forgive and to seek forgiveness, and being willing to be honest and transparent with each other. When couples practice genuine love, they are not just saying they love each other but are showing it through their actions.

This authentic action of genuine love includes being willing to help one's spouse with daily tasks, being willing to listen to one's spouse's concerns, being willing to support one's spouse's dreams and goals, being willing to be patient with one's spouse's weaknesses, and being willing to be honest about one's own weaknesses. When couples practice genuine love in these ways, they are building a relationship that is based on authenticity and trust.

The Daily Practice of Genuine Love

Genuine love in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being honest with each other about feelings and needs, being willing to apologize when one has hurt one's spouse, being willing to forgive when one's spouse has hurt one, being willing to be vulnerable with each other, and being willing to share one's thoughts and feelings openly. These daily practices of genuine love build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of genuine love includes being willing to communicate openly and honestly, being willing to be transparent about one's struggles and weaknesses, being willing to share one's hopes and dreams, being willing to be honest about one's fears and concerns, and being willing to be authentic in one's expressions of love. When couples practice genuine love in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Genuine Love and Communication

Genuine love in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate openly and honestly with each other, they are better able to express their genuine love and to understand each other's needs and desires. Good communication helps couples to be authentic in their relationship and to build trust and intimacy.

This communication of genuine love includes being willing to share one's thoughts and feelings openly, being willing to listen to one's spouse's thoughts and feelings, being willing to be honest about one's needs and desires, being willing to be transparent about one's struggles and weaknesses, and being willing to communicate in a way that builds up the relationship. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building genuine love and trust.

Genuine Love and Vulnerability

Genuine love in marriage requires vulnerability. When couples are willing to be vulnerable with each other, they are able to share their deepest thoughts, feelings, and desires and to build a relationship that is based on authenticity and trust. This vulnerability is not about being weak but is about being strong enough to be honest and open with each other.

This vulnerability in genuine love includes being willing to share one's fears and concerns, being willing to share one's hopes and dreams, being willing to share one's struggles and weaknesses, being willing to share one's joys and successes, and being willing to be honest about one's needs and desires. When couples are vulnerable with each other, they are not just sharing information but are building genuine love and intimacy.

Genuine Love and Forgiveness

Genuine love in marriage includes forgiveness. When couples are able to forgive each other and to seek forgiveness when they have hurt each other, they are practicing genuine love. This forgiveness is not about being weak but is about being strong enough to love even when it is difficult.

This forgiveness in genuine love includes being willing to forgive when one's spouse has hurt one, being willing to ask for forgiveness when one has hurt one's spouse, being willing to let go of resentment and bitterness, being willing to start fresh each day, and being willing to love each other despite imperfections. When couples practice forgiveness, they are not just resolving conflicts but are building genuine love and trust.

Genuine Love and Growth

Genuine love in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice genuine love, they are growing in virtues like honesty, humility, patience, and forgiveness. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through genuine love includes growing in honesty and authenticity, growing in humility and vulnerability, growing in patience and forgiveness, growing in trust and intimacy, and growing in love and holiness. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Genuine Love and the Mission of Marriage

Genuine love in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples practice genuine love, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's genuine love in the world.

This mission through genuine love includes being willing to serve each other with genuine love, being willing to serve others with genuine love, being willing to be witnesses to God's genuine love, being willing to build a family based on genuine love, and being willing to raise children in an environment of genuine love. When couples practice genuine love in these ways, they are fulfilling their mission and becoming better witnesses to God's love.

The Grace of Genuine Love

Genuine love in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to practice genuine love and to grow in holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to practice genuine love and to build a strong and lasting marriage based on authenticity and love.

Conclusion: An Authentic Relationship

Genuine love in Catholic marriage is an authentic relationship that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting marriage. When couples practice genuine love, they are not just improving their relationship but are becoming better witnesses to God's authentic love in the world.

As we reflect on genuine love in marriage, let us remember that this love is not just a human emotion but is a divine love that requires God's grace. When we practice genuine love, we are building a marriage that is based on authenticity, trust, and the grace of God.
