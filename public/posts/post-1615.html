Introduction: Faith and Holiness

The role of faith in the journey toward holiness represents a fundamental understanding of how faith serves as the foundation and driving force in the Catholic pursuit of holiness and sanctity. Holiness, which involves growing in our likeness to Christ and becoming the people God created us to be, is not something we achieve through our own efforts alone, but a gift from God that we receive and cooperate with through faith. The Catholic understanding of the role of faith in the journey toward holiness emphasizes that faith is not just the starting point of the spiritual journey, but the ongoing foundation that sustains and guides us throughout our entire life of growing in holiness. When we understand the role of faith in the journey toward holiness, we develop a deeper appreciation for how faith and holiness work together in the spiritual life.

The Catholic approach to the role of faith in the journey toward holiness emphasizes that faith provides the foundation for all our efforts to grow in holiness, and that it enables us to recognize, receive, and cooperate with God's grace in our spiritual development. This approach recognizes that holiness is not just about following rules or achieving spiritual accomplishments, but about growing in our relationship with God and becoming more like Christ through faith and grace. When faith plays its proper role in the journey toward holiness, we are able to grow in our likeness to Christ and become the people God created us to be.

By understanding the role of faith in the journey toward holiness, individuals can develop a deeper appreciation for how faith and holiness work together in the spiritual life.
