Patronage of the Universal Church

Saint Joseph's most important patronage is his role as Patron of the Universal Church, a title that was officially proclaimed by Pope Pius IX in 1870. This patronage recognizes Joseph's special role as the protector and intercessor for the entire Catholic Church throughout the world. As the patron of the Universal Church, Joseph continues to exercise his protective care for all believers, just as he protected and cared for the Holy Family during his earthly life.

Joseph's patronage of the Universal Church is particularly significant because it recognizes his continued active role in the life of the Church. His prayers and intercession are particularly powerful because of his unique relationship with Jesus and Mary, and his understanding of the Church's needs and challenges. This universal patronage demonstrates that Joseph's protective care extends beyond his earthly family to embrace all members of the Body of Christ.

Joseph's patronage of the Universal Church also demonstrates his importance for the Church's mission and his continued relevance for contemporary believers. His patronage helps to establish Joseph as one of the most important intercessors in the Church and as a powerful protector of all believers.

Joseph's patronage of workers helps to establish the Church's social teaching on work and workers' rights.

Joseph's patronage of workers is particularly relevant for contemporary believers who face various challenges in their work life.
