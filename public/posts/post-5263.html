The Call to Unity (Ephesians 4:3)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Unity

The call to unity in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Ephesians 4:3: "Eager to maintain the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace." This passage shows that unity is not just a nice ideal but is essential for maintaining the bond of peace in marriage. When couples answer the call to unity, they are not just getting along but are actively participating in God's work of creating harmony and peace in their relationship.

This biblical foundation shows that the call to unity is not just about avoiding conflict but is about being eager to maintain the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace. When couples answer this call, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This unity is not about being identical but is about being united in purpose and love.

Unity as a Divine Gift

The call to unity in Catholic marriage recognizes that unity is a divine gift that comes from the Holy Spirit. Just as the early Christians were called to maintain the unity of the Spirit, so too are married couples called to maintain this unity in their relationship. This unity is not about being the same but is about being united in Christ and in their shared mission.

When couples answer the call to unity, they are not just working together but are actively participating in God's work of creating harmony and peace. They are willing to work together to maintain unity, to resolve conflicts in a way that preserves unity, to support each other's goals and dreams, to share a common vision for their marriage, and to be united in their faith and values. This unity becomes a powerful force for good in their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Unity

Answering the call to unity is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes working together to resolve conflicts, supporting each other's goals and dreams, sharing a common vision for the marriage, being united in faith and values, and being willing to compromise for the sake of unity. These daily practices of unity build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to work together to resolve conflicts in a way that preserves unity, being willing to support one's spouse's goals and dreams, being willing to share a common vision for the marriage and family, being willing to be united in faith and values, and being willing to compromise for the sake of unity when necessary. When couples practice unity in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Unity and Communication

Answering the call to unity is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively with each other, they are better able to maintain unity and to resolve conflicts in a way that preserves unity. Good communication helps couples to understand each other's needs and to work together to find solutions.

This communication for unity includes being willing to discuss important decisions together, being willing to share one's thoughts and feelings openly, being willing to listen to one's spouse's perspective, being willing to work together to find solutions to problems, and being willing to compromise when necessary for the sake of unity. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on unity and cooperation.

Unity and Growth

Answering the call to unity is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples answer this call, they are growing in virtues like cooperation, compromise, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through unity includes growing in cooperation as they learn to work together, growing in compromise as they learn to put unity above individual preferences, growing in love as they learn to love each other more deeply, growing in humility as they learn to put each other first, and growing in holiness as they learn to imitate Christ's unity with the Church. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A United Love

The call to unity in Catholic marriage is a united love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples answer this call, they are not just getting along but are actively participating in God's work of creating harmony and peace in their relationship.

As we reflect on the call to unity, let us remember that this unity is not just a human effort but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we answer the call to unity, we are building a marriage that is based on love, cooperation, and the grace of God.
