Trusting God in Difficult Seasons (Proverbs 3:5–6)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Trusting God

The practice of trusting God in difficult seasons in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Proverbs 3:5–6: "Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths." This passage shows that trusting God is not just a nice sentiment but is essential for navigating the difficult seasons that every marriage faces. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are not just being religious but are actively relying on God's wisdom and guidance to help them through the challenges.

This biblical foundation shows that trusting God in difficult seasons is not just about being optimistic but is about actively relying on God's wisdom and guidance. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This trust is not about being passive but is about being actively dependent on God's help.

Trusting God as an Active Choice

Trusting God in difficult seasons is not just a feeling but is an active choice that married couples must make. Just as the writer of Proverbs encourages us to trust in the Lord with all our heart, so too are married couples called to make the active choice to trust in God during difficult times. This trust is not about being naive but is about being wise enough to recognize that God's wisdom is greater than our own.

When couples choose to trust in God during difficult seasons, they are not just being religious but are making the wise choice to rely on God's wisdom and guidance. They are willing to acknowledge that their own understanding is limited and that God's wisdom is infinite. This trust becomes a powerful force for good in their marriage and in their lives.

The Daily Practice of Trusting God

Trusting God in difficult seasons is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying together about difficulties, being willing to seek God's guidance in decisions, being patient when answers don't come immediately, being thankful for God's faithfulness, and being willing to acknowledge God in all their ways. These daily practices of trusting God build a strong foundation for navigating difficult seasons.

This daily practice of trusting God includes being willing to pray together about difficulties and challenges, being willing to seek God's guidance in important decisions, being patient when God's timing is different from their own, being thankful for God's faithfulness even in difficult times, and being willing to acknowledge God's presence and guidance in all aspects of their life. When couples practice trusting God in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Trusting God and Communication

Trusting God in difficult seasons is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate openly about their difficulties and their trust in God, they are better able to support each other and to work together to navigate challenging times. Good communication helps couples to share their struggles and to encourage each other in their trust in God.

This communication for trusting God includes being willing to share one's struggles and difficulties with one's spouse, being willing to pray together about challenges, being willing to encourage each other in their trust in God, being willing to share how God is working in their lives, and being willing to work together to seek God's guidance. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building each other up in their trust in God.

Trusting God and Prayer

Prayer is essential for trusting God in difficult seasons. When couples pray together about their difficulties, they are not just asking for help but are actively acknowledging their dependence on God and seeking His guidance. This prayer is not about being superstitious but is about being spiritually wise and relying on God's grace.

This prayer for trusting God includes praying together about difficulties and challenges, praying for God's guidance in important decisions, praying for patience and wisdom during difficult times, praying for God's strength to help them through challenges, and praying for gratitude for God's faithfulness. When couples pray in these ways, they are not just asking for help but are actively participating in God's work of guiding them through difficult seasons.

Trusting God and Patience

Trusting God in difficult seasons requires patience. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are often called to wait for God's timing and to be patient with the process. This patience is not about being passive but is about being willing to trust that God's timing is perfect.

This patience in trusting God includes being willing to wait for God's timing in resolving difficulties, being patient when answers don't come immediately, being willing to trust that God is working even when they can't see the results, being patient with each other during difficult times, and being willing to trust that God's plan is good even when it's difficult to understand. When couples practice this patience, they are not just waiting but are actively trusting in God's wisdom and timing.

Trusting God and Growth

Trusting God in difficult seasons is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are growing in virtues like faith, patience, and perseverance. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's faithfulness in the world.

This growth through trusting God includes growing in faith as they learn to trust in God's wisdom and guidance, growing in patience as they learn to wait for God's timing, growing in perseverance as they learn to endure difficult times with God's help, growing in gratitude as they learn to be thankful for God's faithfulness, and growing in holiness as they learn to rely on God's grace. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just surviving difficult times but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Trusting God and the Mission of Marriage

Trusting God in difficult seasons is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's faithfulness in the world.

This mission through trusting God includes being willing to serve each other with trust in God's help, being willing to serve their children with trust in God's guidance, being willing to serve others with trust in God's strength, being willing to be witnesses to God's faithfulness, and being willing to build a family based on trust in God. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are fulfilling their mission and becoming better witnesses to God's faithfulness.

The Grace of Trusting God

Trusting God in difficult seasons is not something that couples can do on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to trust in God during difficult times and to grow in holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to trust in God during difficult seasons and to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Conclusion: A Faithful Trust

Trusting God in difficult seasons in Catholic marriage is a faithful trust that helps couples to grow in holiness and to navigate challenging times with God's help. When couples trust in God during difficult times, they are not just surviving but are growing in faith and becoming more like Christ.

As we reflect on trusting God in difficult seasons, let us remember that this trust is not just a human effort but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we trust in God during difficult times, we are building a marriage that is based on faith, trust, and the grace of God.
