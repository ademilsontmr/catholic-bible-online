Remembering Marriage as a Covenant (Malachi 2:14)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Marriage as a Covenant

Remembering marriage as a covenant in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Malachi 2:14: "But you say, 'Why does he not?' Because the Lord was witness between you and the wife of your youth, to whom you have been faithless, though she is your companion and your wife by covenant." This passage shows that marriage is not just a human contract but is a sacred covenant that God witnesses and upholds. When couples remember their marriage as a covenant, they are not just being religious but are actively recognizing the sacred nature of their commitment and God's role in upholding it.

This biblical foundation shows that remembering marriage as a covenant is not just about having a nice ceremony but is about actively recognizing the sacred nature of the marital commitment and God's role in upholding it. When couples remember their marriage as a covenant, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This remembering is not about being perfect but is about being willing to recognize and honor the sacred nature of their commitment.

Covenant as a Sacred Commitment

Remembering marriage as a covenant in Catholic marriage recognizes that marriage is a sacred commitment that God witnesses and upholds. Just as God made covenants with His people throughout salvation history, so too does He witness and uphold the covenant of marriage. This covenant is not just about human promises but is about a sacred commitment that God takes seriously.

When couples remember their marriage as a covenant, they are not just being religious but are actively recognizing the sacred nature of their commitment. They are able to take their commitment seriously, to honor their promises to each other, to seek God's help in fulfilling their covenant, to trust in God's faithfulness to their covenant, and to grow in their commitment to each other. This remembering becomes a powerful foundation for their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Remembering the Covenant

Remembering marriage as a covenant is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes taking their commitment seriously, honoring their promises to each other, seeking God's help in fulfilling their covenant, trusting in God's faithfulness, and growing in their commitment to each other. These daily practices of remembering the covenant build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to take their marital commitment seriously and to honor it in all their decisions, being willing to honor their promises to each other and to work to fulfill them, being willing to seek God's help and guidance in fulfilling their covenant, being willing to trust in God's faithfulness to their covenant, and being willing to grow in their commitment to each other and to their covenant. When couples practice remembering the covenant in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Remembering the Covenant and Communication

Remembering marriage as a covenant is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their covenant commitment and their desire to honor it, they are better able to remember their covenant effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their commitment, to share their desire to honor their covenant, and to support each other in fulfilling their covenant.

This communication for remembering the covenant includes being willing to discuss their covenant commitment and what it means to them, being willing to share their desire to honor their covenant and to work to fulfill it, being willing to discuss how they can better honor their covenant in their daily lives, being willing to support each other in fulfilling their covenant, and being willing to pray together for God's help in honoring their covenant. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on covenant commitment.

Remembering the Covenant and Growth

Remembering marriage as a covenant is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples remember their marriage as a covenant, they are growing in virtues like faithfulness, commitment, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through remembering the covenant includes growing in faithfulness as they learn to be faithful to their covenant commitment, growing in commitment as they learn to honor their promises to each other, growing in love as they learn to love each other more deeply through their covenant, growing in trust as they learn to trust in God's faithfulness to their covenant, and growing in holiness as they learn to honor their sacred commitment. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Sacred Covenant

Remembering marriage as a covenant in Catholic marriage is a sacred covenant that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples remember their marriage as a covenant, they are not just being religious but are actively recognizing the sacred nature of their commitment and God's role in upholding it.

As we reflect on remembering marriage as a covenant, let us remember that this covenant is not just a human agreement but is a sacred commitment that requires God's grace. When we remember our marriage as a covenant, we are building a marriage that is based on faithfulness, commitment, and the grace of God.
