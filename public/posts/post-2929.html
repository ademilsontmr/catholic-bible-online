What are the Psalms of Lament?

The Psalms of Lament are a collection of prayers in the Book of Psalms that express pain, suffering, grief, and complaint to God. These psalms are characterized by their honest and raw expression of human emotion, including feelings of abandonment, despair, anger, and confusion. Some well-known psalms of lament include Psalm 22, which begins with the words 'My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?' and Psalm 13, which asks 'How long, O Lord? Will you forget me forever?' These psalms are not just expressions of despair, but are prayers that ultimately lead to trust and hope in God, even in the midst of suffering.

The Catholic understanding of the Psalms of Lament is rooted in the belief that God wants us to be honest with Him about our feelings and experiences, and that He can handle our pain, anger, and confusion. The Church teaches that these psalms are inspired by God and that they provide us with a model for how to pray when we are suffering. The Psalms of Lament recognize that suffering is a real part of human life and that it's okay to bring our pain and questions to God. These psalms also show us that it's possible to maintain our faith and trust in God even when we are experiencing great difficulty and pain.

These psalms help us to be honest with God about our struggles and to trust that He can handle our pain and questions.

The Psalms of Lament also help during trials because they show us that we are not alone in our suffering. When we read these psalms, we are reminded that people throughout history have experienced similar feelings of pain and despair, and that they found ways to maintain their faith and trust in God even in the midst of their suffering. These psalms also help during trials because they ultimately lead to hope and trust in God. Even though they begin with expressions of pain and complaint, most of the Psalms of Lament end with statements of trust and confidence in God's love and care.

When praying the Psalms of Lament, focus on several key areas.
