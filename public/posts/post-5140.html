Baptism and the Profession of Faith (Romans 10:9–10)

2025-02-02

Introduction: Baptism and Faith

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? This question addresses one of the most important aspects of the sacrament of baptism. Romans 10:9-10 provides the biblical foundation for this understanding: 'Because if you confess with your lips that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. For one believes with the heart and so is justified, and one confesses with the mouth and so is saved.' These verses reveal the essential connection between faith and salvation, and how this faith is professed and lived through baptism.

The Catholic Church teaches that baptism is the sacrament of faith that requires both the profession of faith and the gift of faith. Through baptism, we profess our faith in Christ and receive the grace to live according to that faith. This profession is not just a one-time declaration but is a lifelong commitment to believe in Christ and to live as His disciples.

The Context of Romans 10:9-10

To understand the connection between baptism and the profession of faith, we must examine the context of Romans 10:9-10. Saint Paul is writing about the way salvation comes through faith in Christ. The verses read: 'Because if you confess with your lips that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. For one believes with the heart and so is justified, and one confesses with the mouth and so is saved.'

These verses show that salvation requires both belief in the heart and confession with the lips. This belief and confession are essential for salvation and are expressed through baptism. Through baptism, we both believe in Christ and confess our faith in Him, making baptism the sacrament of faith.

This context shows that baptism is intimately connected to the profession of faith and the gift of salvation.

The Gift of Faith

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? First, baptism is the sacrament through which we receive the gift of faith. This faith is not just a human quality but is a gift from God that enables us to believe in Christ and to accept His salvation.

Through baptism, we receive the theological virtue of faith, which enables us to believe in God and to accept His revelation. This faith is not just intellectual assent but is a personal trust in God and His promises. This gift of faith is essential for the Christian life and is given through baptism.

The gift of faith shows that baptism is the sacrament through which we receive the grace to believe in Christ.

The Profession of Faith

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? Through baptism, we profess our faith in Christ and in the Holy Trinity. This profession is not just a private belief but is a public declaration of our faith in Christ and our commitment to follow Him.

This profession involves confessing that Jesus is Lord and believing in our hearts that God raised Him from the dead. Through baptism, we make this profession publicly and commit ourselves to living according to this faith. This profession is essential for salvation and is expressed through baptism.

The profession of faith shows that baptism is the sacrament through which we publicly profess our faith in Christ.

The Creed

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? During the baptismal rite, the candidate (or their godparents) professes the Creed, which is the summary of the Christian faith. This profession of the Creed is not just a ritual but is a declaration of belief in the essential truths of the Christian faith.

Through the profession of the Creed, we declare our belief in God the Father, in Jesus Christ His Son, and in the Holy Spirit. We also profess our belief in the Church, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, and the resurrection of the body. This profession is essential for baptism and establishes our identity as Christians.

The Creed shows that baptism involves a comprehensive profession of the Christian faith.

The Renunciation of Sin

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? Through baptism, we not only profess our faith in Christ but also renounce sin and the devil. This renunciation is part of our profession of faith and shows our commitment to turn away from evil and to follow Christ.

This renunciation involves rejecting Satan, all his works, and all his empty promises. Through this renunciation, we declare our commitment to live according to God's will and to resist the temptations of the evil one. This renunciation is essential for the Christian life and is expressed through baptism.

The renunciation of sin shows that baptism involves a commitment to turn away from evil and to follow Christ.

The Ongoing Profession

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? While the profession of faith is made at baptism, it is not just a one-time declaration but is a commitment that must be renewed throughout our lives. Through prayer, the sacraments, and good works, we continue to profess our faith and to grow in our relationship with Christ.

This ongoing profession involves living according to our faith and bearing witness to Christ in the world. Through our words and actions, we continue to profess our faith and to show others the truth of the Gospel. This ongoing profession is essential for the Christian life and flows from our baptism.

The ongoing profession shows that baptism is the beginning of a lifelong commitment to profess and live our faith.

The Witness to Christ

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? Through baptism, we are called to be witnesses to Christ and to profess our faith not just in words but also in deeds. This witness involves living according to Christ's teachings and showing others the truth of the Gospel through our lives.

This witness is not just for a select few but for all baptized Christians, who are called to be witnesses to Christ in their own way, according to their state in life. Through our witness, we help others to come to know Christ and to profess their faith in Him.

The witness to Christ shows that baptism involves a commitment to bear witness to Christ in the world.

Conclusion: Faith and Baptism

What is the connection between baptism and the profession of faith? Through baptism, we receive the gift of faith, profess our belief in Christ, renounce sin and the devil, and commit ourselves to living according to our faith. This profession is not just a one-time declaration but is a lifelong commitment to believe in Christ and to live as His disciples.

As we reflect on Romans 10:9-10 and the connection between baptism and the profession of faith, let us be grateful for the gift of faith and the way it is expressed through baptism. May we always remember that through baptism, we have professed our faith in Christ and are called to live according to that faith.
