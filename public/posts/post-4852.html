Learning Holiness From Children's Faith

Children have a natural capacity for faith, trust, and wonder that can teach us important lessons about holiness. Their simple trust in God, their ability to wonder and marvel, and their natural inclination toward goodness can help us grow in our own spiritual life. Understanding learning holiness from children's faith helps you recognize that we can learn important spiritual lessons from the youngest among us. This understanding is deeply rooted in Jesus' teaching that we must become like children to enter the kingdom of heaven.

The Biblical Foundation: Become Like Children

The Bible teaches us about the importance of childlike faith. Matthew 18:3 records Jesus saying, 'Truly I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven.' Additionally, Mark 10:15 tells us that 'whoever does not receive the kingdom of God like a little child will never enter it.' Understanding this biblical foundation helps you recognize that childlike qualities are essential for spiritual growth and holiness.

Understanding Childlike Faith

Childlike faith involves qualities such as simple trust in God, natural wonder and awe, openness to learning, humility and dependence, natural goodness and love, and the ability to forgive easily. These qualities are not childish but childlike—they represent the best aspects of childhood that we should cultivate in our spiritual life. Understanding childlike faith helps you recognize what these qualities involve and why they are important for holiness.

How Children's Faith Teaches Us About Holiness

Children's faith teaches us about holiness in many ways. It shows us the importance of simple trust in God, it reminds us to approach God with wonder and awe, it teaches us to be open to learning and growing, it shows us the value of humility and dependence on God, it reminds us of the importance of natural goodness and love, and it teaches us to forgive easily and not hold grudges. Children's faith also shows us how to approach God with simplicity and sincerity. Understanding how children's faith teaches us about holiness helps you recognize the lessons you can learn from them.

Qualities of Children to Emulate

There are many qualities of children that we should emulate in our spiritual life. These include their natural trust and faith, their ability to wonder and marvel, their openness to new experiences, their natural goodness and love, their ability to forgive easily, their humility and dependence, and their natural joy and enthusiasm. These qualities help us grow in holiness and develop a deeper relationship with God. Understanding these qualities helps you recognize what you can learn from children.

Overcoming Obstacles to Childlike Faith

There are many obstacles to developing childlike faith, including pride and self-sufficiency, cynicism and skepticism, the tendency to overthink and complicate things, lack of wonder and awe, and the tendency to hold grudges and not forgive easily. Understanding these obstacles helps you recognize what might prevent you from developing childlike faith and develop strategies for overcoming them.

Prayer for Childlike Faith

Prayer is essential for developing childlike faith. Through prayer, you can ask for the grace to develop childlike qualities, seek God's help in overcoming obstacles to childlike faith, express your desire to trust God more simply, and receive the grace you need to develop wonder and awe. Prayer also helps you recognize that childlike faith is not just a human effort but requires God's grace and help. Understanding how to pray for childlike faith helps you recognize the importance of prayer for developing these qualities.

Learning from Children in Daily Life

Learning from children in daily life involves being attentive to the qualities they display, reflecting on how these qualities can help you grow spiritually, and trying to emulate these qualities in your own life. This might include spending time with children, observing their natural faith and trust, learning from their ability to wonder and marvel, and trying to develop similar qualities in your spiritual life. Learning from children helps you recognize the spiritual lessons they can teach you.

The Fruits of Childlike Faith

Developing childlike faith produces many fruits in your spiritual life, including greater trust in God, increased wonder and awe, deeper humility and dependence on God, greater capacity for love and forgiveness, increased joy and enthusiasm, and a more simple and sincere approach to God. Childlike faith also helps you recognize God's presence and love more easily. Understanding the fruits of childlike faith helps you recognize why these qualities are worth developing.

Conclusion: Becoming Like Children

Learning holiness from children's faith is about recognizing that we can learn important spiritual lessons from the youngest among us and that childlike qualities are essential for growing in holiness. By understanding childlike faith, recognizing how children's faith teaches us about holiness, identifying qualities to emulate, overcoming obstacles, using prayer to develop childlike faith, learning from children in daily life, and recognizing the fruits of childlike faith, you can grow in holiness by becoming more like children in your faith and approach to God. Understanding this approach helps you recognize that childlike qualities are not childish but are essential for spiritual growth and holiness.
