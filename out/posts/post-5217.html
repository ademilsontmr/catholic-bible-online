Why Catholic Marriage Needs a Foundation of Prayer (Philippians 4:6)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Prayer

The Catholic understanding that marriage needs a foundation of prayer is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Philippians 4:6: "Have no anxiety about anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God." This passage shows that prayer is not just a nice addition to marriage but is the essential foundation upon which a strong and holy marriage is built. When couples build their marriage on prayer, they are not just asking for God's help but are inviting Him to be the center of their relationship.

This biblical foundation shows that prayer in marriage is not just about asking for things but is about building a relationship with God that becomes the foundation for the couple's relationship with each other. When couples pray together, they are not just growing spiritually but are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Prayer as the Foundation of Marriage

Prayer is the foundation upon which every strong Catholic marriage is built. Without prayer, it is impossible to build a marriage that will withstand the storms of life. When couples pray together, they are not just asking for God's help but are building a relationship with Him that becomes the foundation for their relationship with each other.

This foundation of prayer includes praying together regularly, praying for each other's needs, praying for their marriage, and praying for their children. When couples build their marriage on this foundation of prayer, they are creating a relationship that is strong and lasting.

The Daily Practice of Prayer

Prayer in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying together in the morning, praying before meals, praying before bed, and praying during difficult times. These daily practices of prayer build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of prayer includes being patient with each other's prayer styles, being respectful of each other's spiritual needs, being willing to learn from each other, and being committed to growing together spiritually. When couples practice prayer together regularly, they are building a relationship that is strong and lasting.

Prayer and Communication

Prayer in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples pray together, they are communicating with each other on a deep spiritual level. This communication through prayer helps couples to understand each other better and to grow in intimacy.

This communication through prayer includes sharing their hopes and fears, their joys and sorrows, and their spiritual experiences. When couples pray together, they are not just talking to God but are sharing their inner lives with each other. This sharing helps couples to grow closer and to build a relationship that is deep and meaningful.

Prayer and Growth

Prayer in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples pray together, they are growing in virtues like humility, gratitude, and love. They are also learning to see each other as God sees them and to love each other with the same unconditional love that God has for them.

This growth through prayer includes growing in faith, growing in hope, and growing in love. When couples pray together, they are not just growing individually but are growing together as a couple. This growth together helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

Prayer and the Mission of Marriage

Prayer in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples pray together, they are not just strengthening their own relationship but are becoming a source of blessing for others. They are also becoming witnesses to God's love and power in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often experiences brokenness and division. When couples pray together and remain faithful to each other, they become a sign of hope and unity in the world. They show the world that prayer is powerful and that it brings peace and joy to those who practice it.

The Grace of Prayer

Prayer in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to pray together and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through participation in the sacraments, through individual prayer, and through a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can build their marriage on a foundation of prayer.

Conclusion: A Sacred Foundation

Prayer is the sacred foundation upon which every strong Catholic marriage is built. When couples build their marriage on prayer, they are not just asking for God's help but are inviting Him to be the center of their relationship. This foundation of prayer helps couples to grow in love and holiness and to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

As we reflect on the importance of prayer as the foundation of marriage, let us remember that this prayer is not just a human practice but is a divine gift that helps us to grow closer to each other and to God. When we build our marriage on prayer, we are creating a relationship that is strong, lasting, and filled with love.
