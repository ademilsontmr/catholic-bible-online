Wives, Respect Your Husbands (Ephesians 5:33)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Respect

The call for wives to respect their husbands is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Ephesians 5:33: "However, let each one of you love his wife as himself, and let the wife see that she respects her husband." This passage shows that respect in marriage is not just a nice thing to do but is a commandment from God. When wives respect their husbands, they are not just being polite but are honoring God's design for marriage and helping their husbands to fulfill their vocation as leaders of the family.

This biblical foundation shows that respect in marriage is not just about being submissive in a negative sense but is about recognizing and honoring the God-given role that husbands have as leaders of the family. This respect is not about being inferior but is about working together as equal partners in a complementary relationship. When wives respect their husbands, they are helping to create a harmonious and loving marriage.

Respect as a Reflection of Divine Order

Respect in marriage is a reflection of the divine order that God has established for the family. Just as the Church respects and follows Christ as her head, so too are wives called to respect and follow their husbands as the head of the family. This respect is not about being inferior but is about working together in a complementary relationship that reflects the relationship between Christ and the Church.

When wives respect their husbands, they are not just honoring their husbands but are honoring God's plan for marriage and family. This respect helps to create a harmonious and loving environment where both husband and wife can flourish and where children can grow up in a stable and loving home. This respect is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage.

The Daily Practice of Respect

Respect in marriage is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes speaking respectfully to one's husband, listening to his opinions and ideas, supporting his decisions, being grateful for his efforts, and showing appreciation for his leadership. These daily practices of respect build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice of respect includes being willing to defer to one's husband's judgment in important matters, being willing to support his goals and dreams, being willing to help him in his role as leader of the family, and being willing to show him appreciation and gratitude. When wives practice respect in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Respect and Communication

Respect in marriage is closely connected to communication. When wives communicate respectfully with their husbands, they are better able to express their thoughts and feelings in a way that honors their husbands and builds up the relationship. This respectful communication helps couples to understand each other better and to work together more effectively.

Respectful communication includes speaking kindly and gently, avoiding criticism and sarcasm, being willing to listen to one's husband's point of view, being willing to compromise when appropriate, and being willing to express disagreement in a respectful way. When wives communicate respectfully, they are not just being polite but are building a strong and lasting marriage.

Respect and Support

Respect in marriage includes being supportive of one's husband. When wives respect their husbands, they are willing to support them in their role as leader of the family, in their work and career, in their goals and dreams, and in their spiritual growth. This support is not about being a doormat but is about working together as partners in building a strong and loving family.

This support includes being willing to help one's husband in practical ways, being willing to encourage him when he is discouraged, being willing to celebrate his successes, being willing to be patient when he makes mistakes, and being willing to pray for him and with him. When wives support their husbands, they are not just helping them but are building a strong and lasting marriage.

Respect and Trust

Respect in marriage is closely connected to trust. When wives respect their husbands, they are more likely to trust them and to have confidence in their leadership. This trust is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When wives trust their husbands, they are able to work together more effectively and to build a harmonious and loving family.

This trust includes being willing to trust one's husband's judgment in important matters, being willing to trust his commitment to the family, being willing to trust his love and fidelity, and being willing to trust his ability to provide for and protect the family. When wives trust their husbands, they are not just being naive but are building a strong and lasting marriage.

Respect and Growth

Respect in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When wives respect their husbands, they are growing in virtues like humility, patience, and love. They are also learning to see their husbands as God sees them and to love them with the same unconditional love that God has for them.

This growth in virtue is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When wives grow in virtue, they are better able to love their husbands, to communicate effectively, to resolve conflicts, and to fulfill their vocation. This growth also helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

The Grace of Respect

Respect in marriage is not something that wives can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, wives receive the grace they need to respect their husbands and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Wives can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any wife can learn to respect her husband and to build a strong and lasting marriage based on respect and love.

Conclusion: A Sacred Duty

Respecting one's husband is a sacred duty that helps wives to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting marriage. When wives respect their husbands, they are honoring God's plan for marriage and family and becoming better witnesses to His love in the world.

As we reflect on the call for wives to respect their husbands, let us remember that this respect is not just a human virtue but is a divine commandment that requires God's grace. When wives respect their husbands, they are building a marriage that is based on love, respect, and the grace of God.
