What is the Divine Mercy Chaplet?

The Divine Mercy Chaplet is a powerful prayer devotion that focuses on God's infinite mercy and love. The Catholic Church teaches that this devotion was given to St. Faustina Kowalska by Jesus Himself in the 1930s, and it has become one of the most popular and powerful devotions in the Church. The Divine Mercy Chaplet is not just a series of prayers, but a way of meditating on God's mercy and asking for His grace and forgiveness for ourselves and for the whole world. This devotion is particularly powerful for those who are seeking God's mercy, for those who want to pray for others, and for those who want to grow in their understanding of God's love and compassion.

The Bible provides the foundation for the Divine Mercy devotion, with many references to God's mercy and love throughout Scripture. From the Old Testament stories of God's mercy toward His people, to the New Testament teachings of Jesus about God's love and forgiveness, Scripture shows us that God's mercy is central to His character and is available to all who seek it. The Divine Mercy Chaplet helps us focus on this aspect of God's character and to ask for His mercy for ourselves and for others.

Why Pray the Divine Mercy Chaplet?

Praying the Divine Mercy Chaplet is important because it helps us focus on God's mercy and to ask for His grace and forgiveness. When we pray this devotion, we're not just reciting prayers - we're meditating on God's infinite mercy and asking Him to pour out His grace and forgiveness on us and on the whole world. This prayer helps us develop a deeper understanding of God's love and compassion, and it can bring great peace and comfort to those who are struggling with sin, guilt, or other difficulties.

The Divine Mercy Chaplet is also particularly powerful for praying for others, especially those who are in need of God's mercy or who are suffering. When we pray this devotion, we can offer it for specific intentions, such as the conversion of sinners, the souls in purgatory, or for those who are facing difficult situations. This makes it a powerful tool for intercessory prayer and for helping others through our prayers. Most importantly, praying the Divine Mercy Chaplet helps us grow in our relationship with God and in our understanding of His love and mercy.

How to Pray the Divine Mercy Chaplet

To pray the Divine Mercy Chaplet, begin by finding a quiet place where you can pray without distractions. You might want to have an image of the Divine Mercy or a crucifix nearby to help you focus. Take a few moments to quiet your mind and to ask Jesus to help you pray this devotion with sincerity and love. You can pray the Divine Mercy Chaplet using regular rosary beads, or you can count the prayers on your fingers if you don't have beads available.

Start by making the Sign of the Cross and saying the opening prayer: 'You expired, Jesus, but the source of life gushed forth for souls, and the ocean of mercy opened up for the whole world. O Fount of Life, unfathomable Divine Mercy, cover the whole world and empty Yourself out upon us.' Then, on the three beads of the rosary (before the main decades), say the Our Father, the Hail Mary, and the Apostles' Creed. After this, on the large bead before each decade, say: 'Eternal Father, I offer you the Body and Blood, Soul and Divinity of Your Dearly Beloved Son, Our Lord, Jesus Christ, in atonement for our sins and those of the whole world.'

What Prayers to Say

On the ten small beads of each decade, say: 'For the sake of His sorrowful Passion, have mercy on us and on the whole world.' Repeat this prayer ten times for each decade. After completing five decades, say three times: 'Holy God, Holy Mighty One, Holy Immortal One, have mercy on us and on the whole world.' Then, say the closing prayer: 'Eternal God, in whom mercy is endless and the treasury of compassion inexhaustible, look kindly upon us and increase Your mercy in us, that in difficult moments we might not despair nor become despondent, but with great confidence submit ourselves to Your holy will, which is Love and Mercy itself.'

You can also add specific intentions to your Divine Mercy Chaplet by offering each decade for a particular person or intention. For example, you might offer one decade for the conversion of sinners, another for the souls in purgatory, another for those who are suffering, and so on. This makes the devotion even more powerful and meaningful.
