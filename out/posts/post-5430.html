Time Management with Faith — "Teach us to number our days…" (Psalm 90:12)

2025-01-27

Introduction: Time as a Gift from God

Time management with faith represents one of the most important skills that students need to develop in their academic and spiritual life. Psalm 90:12 tells us, "Teach us to number our days, that we may get a heart of wisdom." This biblical prayer provides the foundation for understanding how to manage our time effectively while keeping God at the center of our daily schedule. This approach involves recognizing that time is a precious gift from God that we are called to use wisely and purposefully.

When we understand time management with faith, we recognize that effective time management is not just about productivity but about stewardship of the gift of time that God has given us. This understanding helps us to see that our daily schedule should reflect our priorities and values, with God at the center of all our activities.

Understanding the Biblical Foundation of Time

Time management with faith begins with understanding the biblical foundation of time and stewardship. The Bible teaches us that time is a gift from God and that we are called to use it wisely and purposefully. Ephesians 5:15-16 tells us to "look carefully then how you walk, not as unwise but as wise, making the best use of the time." This biblical wisdom reminds us that our time is valuable and should be used for God's glory.

This biblical foundation shows that time management with faith is about recognizing that our time belongs to God and should be used in ways that honor Him. When we understand this, we can approach time management as a form of stewardship and worship.

Prioritizing God in Your Schedule

Time management with faith includes making God a priority in your daily schedule. This involves setting aside time for prayer, Bible study, and spiritual reflection, and making these activities non-negotiable parts of your daily routine. The key is to recognize that time with God is not just another activity but the foundation that makes all other activities meaningful.

This prioritization shows that time management with faith is about putting God first in your schedule. When we prioritize time with God, we create a strong spiritual foundation that supports all our other activities and helps us to maintain perspective and peace.

Balancing Academic and Spiritual Commitments

Time management with faith involves learning how to balance academic responsibilities with spiritual commitments. This might involve integrating prayer into your study routine, using breaks between classes for spiritual reflection, or finding ways to connect your academic work with your faith. The key is to see your academic and spiritual life as integrated rather than separate.

This balance shows that time management with faith is about finding harmony between all aspects of your life. When we learn to balance academic and spiritual commitments, we can find fulfillment and purpose in all our activities.

Using Time Management Tools with Purpose

Time management with faith includes using time management tools and techniques with a clear sense of purpose and values. This might involve using planners, calendars, or apps to organize your schedule, but always with the understanding that these tools should serve your values and priorities. The key is to use these tools to help you live according to your faith and values.

This purposeful approach shows that time management with faith is about using tools and techniques to support your spiritual and academic goals. When we use time management tools with purpose, we can create schedules that reflect our values and help us to live according to God's will.

Conclusion: Time Management as Stewardship

Time management with faith reveals that effective time management is not just about productivity but about stewardship of the gift of time that God has given us. When we manage our time with faith and purpose, we honor God and create the space we need to grow both academically and spiritually.

As we work to develop time management with faith, let us remember that our time is a gift from God that we are called to use wisely. When we put God at the center of our schedule and manage our time with purpose, we can find the balance and peace we need to succeed in all areas of our lives.
