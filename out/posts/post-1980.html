What is Disability Ministry?

Disability ministry is the practice of serving and supporting people with disabilities through prayer, pastoral care, advocacy, and inclusion, recognizing that all people are created in the image and likeness of God and deserve to be treated with dignity and respect. The Catholic Church teaches that disability ministry is an important form of service that helps ensure that people with disabilities are fully included in the life of the Church and have access to the sacraments, religious education, and other spiritual opportunities. Disability ministry involves not just providing accommodations or special services, but building authentic relationships with people with disabilities, recognizing their gifts and contributions, and working to create a more inclusive and welcoming Church community. It is about recognizing that people with disabilities have the same spiritual needs and desires as everyone else, and that they have important gifts and contributions to offer to the Church and to society. This ministry helps people with disabilities understand that they are valued members of the Church community and that their presence and participation enrich the entire community.

The Bible provides many examples of God's care for those who are marginalized or have special needs, from Jesus' healing of people with various disabilities, to the many references to God's love for all people regardless of their abilities or circumstances, to the call to welcome and include all people in the community of faith. Scripture shows us that God has a special concern for those who are vulnerable or marginalized, and that we are called to serve them with love and compassion. The Bible also teaches that all people are created in God's image and deserve to be treated with dignity and respect, regardless of their abilities or circumstances.
