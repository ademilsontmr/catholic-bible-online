Introduction: Citizenship as Better Foundation

Faith and responsibility as Catholic citizenship for a better America is about recognizing that Catholic citizenship is not just a political status or a collection of civic duties, but a powerful combination of faith and responsibility that can help to build a better American society through its commitment to human dignity, social responsibility, and the common good. When American Catholics practice this citizenship effectively, they're not just fulfilling civic obligations or creating religious atmospheres, but actively participating in God's work of building a better and more just American society where all citizens can work together for the common good, regardless of their differences in background, beliefs, or political perspectives. When American Catholics practice this citizenship effectively, they can help to transform American society and to create a better and more just nation.

Fourth, it provides spiritual resources and practices that can help people to find strength and guidance in their citizenship, and to persevere even when faced with various challenges and difficulties. Fifth, it bears witness to the truth that better America building is not just a civic goal but a moral and spiritual imperative that flows from our relationship with God.

Faith and responsibility as Catholic citizenship for a better America is ultimately about recognizing our calling to practice this citizenship effectively and to use our citizenship to help build a better and more just American society, and about helping American Catholics to understand that true better America building comes not from pursuing partisan interests but from working together to build a society that reflects the values of the Gospel and serves the common good.
