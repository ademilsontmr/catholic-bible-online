Who is St. Michael the Archangel?

St. Michael the Archangel is one of the most powerful and important angels in Catholic tradition, known as the prince of the heavenly host and the defender of God's people. The Catholic Church teaches that St. Michael is an archangel, which means he is one of the highest-ranking angels in heaven, and that he has a special role in protecting God's people and fighting against evil. St. Michael's name means 'Who is like God?' and he is often depicted as a warrior angel, armed with a sword and shield, ready to defend the faithful against the forces of evil. He is the patron saint of police officers, soldiers, and all those who work to protect others, and his feast day is celebrated on September 29th.

The Bible provides several references to St. Michael, including his role in the Book of Daniel where he is described as the great prince who protects God's people, and in the Book of Revelation where he leads the heavenly armies in battle against the dragon. Scripture shows us that St. Michael is a powerful defender and that he has a special concern for protecting God's people from spiritual and physical harm. The Bible also teaches that angels are real spiritual beings that God has created to serve Him and to help His people.
