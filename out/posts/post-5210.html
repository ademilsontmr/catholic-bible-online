Why Prayer Together Strengthens Catholic Marriage (Matthew 18:19)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Prayer Together

The power of prayer together in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Matthew 18:19: "Again I say to you, if two of you agree on earth about anything they ask, it will be done for them by my Father in heaven." This passage shows that when couples pray together, they are not just praying individually but are united in their prayer, and this unity brings special graces and blessings to their marriage.

This biblical foundation shows that prayer together is not just a nice thing to do but is a powerful means of strengthening the marriage bond. When couples pray together, they are acknowledging that their marriage is a partnership with God, and they are inviting Him to be the center of their relationship. This prayer together becomes a source of strength and unity that helps couples to face any challenge.

Prayer Together as a Source of Unity

Prayer together in Catholic marriage is a powerful source of unity. When couples pray together, they are not just sharing a spiritual practice but are uniting their hearts and minds in a common purpose. This unity in prayer helps couples to grow closer to each other and to God, creating a bond that is stronger than any earthly relationship.

This unity through prayer includes praying for each other's needs, praying for their marriage, praying for their children, and praying for the world. When couples pray together, they are not just asking for things but are growing in love and understanding. This prayer together becomes a foundation for their relationship that helps them to weather any storm.

The Grace of Prayer Together

Prayer together in Catholic marriage is a source of special grace. When couples pray together, they are not just praying individually but are united in their prayer, and this unity brings special graces and blessings. These graces help couples to grow in love, to remain faithful to each other, and to fulfill their vocation.

This grace is not just given once but is available throughout married life. When couples pray together regularly, they are constantly drawing on God's grace and strengthening their relationship. These graces help couples to face challenges with courage and to grow in love and holiness.

Prayer Together and Communication

Prayer together in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples pray together, they are communicating with each other on a deep spiritual level. This communication through prayer helps couples to understand each other better and to grow in intimacy.

This communication through prayer includes sharing their hopes and fears, their joys and sorrows, and their spiritual experiences. When couples pray together, they are not just talking to God but are sharing their inner lives with each other. This sharing helps couples to grow closer and to build a relationship that is deep and meaningful.

Prayer Together and Growth

Prayer together in marriage is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples pray together, they are growing in virtues like humility, gratitude, and love. They are also learning to see each other as God sees them and to love each other with the same unconditional love that God has for them.

This growth through prayer includes growing in faith, growing in hope, and growing in love. When couples pray together, they are not just growing individually but are growing together as a couple. This growth together helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

Prayer Together and the Mission of Marriage

Prayer together in marriage is essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples pray together, they are not just strengthening their own relationship but are becoming a source of blessing for others. They are also becoming witnesses to God's love and power in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often experiences brokenness and division. When couples pray together and remain faithful to each other, they become a sign of hope and unity in the world. They show the world that prayer is powerful and that it brings peace and joy to those who practice it.

Practical Ways to Pray Together

Prayer together in marriage can take many forms. This includes praying the rosary together, reading Scripture together, saying grace before meals, praying before bed, and praying for specific needs. The key is to make prayer together a regular part of married life.

This practice of prayer together includes being patient with each other, being respectful of each other's prayer styles, and being willing to learn from each other. When couples practice prayer together regularly, they are building a relationship that is strong and lasting.

The Grace of Prayer Together

Prayer together in marriage is not something that couples can practice on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to pray together and to grow in love and holiness. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through participation in the sacraments, through individual prayer, and through a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can practice prayer together and build a strong and lasting marriage.

Conclusion: A Sacred Practice

Prayer together in Catholic marriage is a sacred practice that helps couples to grow in love and holiness. When couples pray together, they are not just sharing a spiritual practice but are uniting their hearts and minds in a common purpose. This prayer together becomes a source of strength and unity that helps couples to face any challenge.

As we reflect on the power of prayer together in marriage, let us remember that this prayer is not just a human practice but is a divine gift that helps us to grow closer to each other and to God. When we pray together, we are building marriages that are strong, lasting, and filled with love.
