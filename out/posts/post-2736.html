What is Catholic Bioethics?

Catholic bioethics is the application of Catholic moral principles and teaching to issues involving human life, health, medicine, and biotechnology. This field addresses complex ethical questions that arise from advances in medical technology, scientific research, and healthcare practices. Catholic bioethics is grounded in the fundamental belief that every human person is created in the image and likeness of God, possessing inherent dignity and worth from conception to natural death.

The Foundation of Catholic Bioethics

Catholic bioethics is built on several key principles:

  • Human dignity and the sanctity of life
  • Natural law and moral reasoning
  • Scriptural and traditional teaching
  • Church authority and guidance
  • Compassion and care for the vulnerable

Key Principles of Catholic Bioethics

Human Dignity

Central to Catholic bioethics is:

  • Every person has inherent worth
  • Dignity is not earned or lost
  • Respect for all stages of life
  • Protection of the vulnerable
  • Recognition of God's image

Sanctity of Life

Life principles include:

  • Life is sacred from conception
  • Natural death is part of life
  • Suicide and euthanasia are wrong
  • Care for the dying
  • Respect for the body
