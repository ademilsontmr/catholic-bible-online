Biblical Foundation of the Eucharist

The biblical foundation of the Eucharist is found in the Gospels and the writings of St. Paul. At the Last Supper, Jesus took bread, blessed it, broke it, and gave it to His disciples, saying 'This is my body.' He also took the cup of wine and said 'This is my blood of the covenant.' These words instituted the Eucharist as the memorial of His sacrifice.

The Real Presence of Christ

The Catholic Church teaches that in the Eucharist, the bread and wine are truly transformed into the Body and Blood of Christ. This is not a symbol or representation, but the real presence of Christ under the appearances of bread and wine. This mystery is called transubstantiation.

The Eucharist as Sacrifice

The Eucharist is not only a meal but also a sacrifice. It makes present the sacrifice of Christ on the Cross. When we participate in the Mass, we are present at Calvary, participating in Christ's offering of Himself to the Father for our salvation.

The Eucharist as Communion

When we receive the Eucharist, we are united with Christ and with all the members of His Body, the Church. The Eucharist strengthens our bond with Christ and with one another, making us one in Him.

Celebrating Holy Thursday Today

Today, Holy Thursday is celebrated with the Mass of the Lord's Supper, which includes the washing of feet, recalling Christ's example of service. The Eucharist is processed to a place of repose, and the altar is stripped bare, symbolizing Christ's abandonment by His disciples.
