The Joy of Shared Mission (Matthew 28:19-20)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Shared Mission

The joy of shared mission in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Matthew 28:19-20: "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you. And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age." This passage shows that the Great Commission is not just for individuals but is a mission that married couples can share together. When couples embrace a shared mission in their marriage, they are not just living together but are actively participating in Christ's work of making disciples and building His kingdom.

This biblical foundation shows that shared mission in marriage is not just about having common goals but is about actively participating in Christ's mission together. When couples embrace a shared mission, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This mission is not about being perfect but is about being willing to work together to serve God and others.

Shared Mission as a Source of Joy

The joy of shared mission in Catholic marriage comes from working together to serve God and others. Just as the early Christians found joy in working together to spread the Gospel, so too do married couples find joy in working together to fulfill Christ's mission. This joy is not just about being happy but is about experiencing the deep satisfaction that comes from serving God together and making a difference in the world.

When couples embrace a shared mission, they are not just being busy but are experiencing the joy that comes from working together for a higher purpose. They are able to support each other in their mission work, to encourage each other when challenges arise, to celebrate their successes together, to grow in faith together, and to experience the fulfillment that comes from serving God as a team. This shared mission becomes a powerful source of joy and purpose in their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Shared Mission

Embracing a shared mission is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying together for their mission, supporting each other's efforts to serve God, encouraging each other when challenges arise, celebrating their successes together, and growing in faith together. These daily practices of shared mission build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to pray together for their shared mission and for God's guidance, being willing to support each other's efforts to serve God and others, being willing to encourage each other when challenges and difficulties arise, being willing to celebrate their successes and achievements together, and being willing to grow in faith together as they serve God. When couples practice shared mission in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and purposeful.

Shared Mission and Communication

Embracing a shared mission is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively about their shared mission, they are better able to work together effectively and to support each other in their mission work. Good communication helps couples to discuss their mission goals, to share their vision for how they want to serve God, and to work together to fulfill their mission.

This communication for shared mission includes being willing to discuss their shared mission goals and vision, being willing to share their ideas for how to serve God together, being willing to support each other's mission efforts, being willing to discuss challenges and difficulties in their mission work, and being willing to celebrate their successes and achievements together. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a partnership in mission.

Shared Mission and Growth

Embracing a shared mission is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples work together in a shared mission, they are growing in virtues like cooperation, perseverance, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through shared mission includes growing in cooperation as they learn to work together effectively, growing in perseverance as they learn to overcome challenges together, growing in love as they learn to love each other more deeply through their shared work, growing in faith as they learn to trust in God's guidance, and growing in holiness as they learn to serve God together. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Joyful Mission

The joy of shared mission in Catholic marriage is a joyful mission that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples embrace a shared mission, they are not just living together but are actively participating in Christ's work of making disciples and building His kingdom.

As we reflect on the joy of shared mission, let us remember that this mission is not just a human effort but is a divine calling that requires God's grace. When we embrace a shared mission, we are building a marriage that is based on joy, purpose, and the grace of God.
