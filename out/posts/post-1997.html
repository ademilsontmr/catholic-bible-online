Who is St. Thomas Aquinas?

St. Thomas Aquinas was a Dominican friar and theologian who lived in the 13th century and is one of the most important and influential saints in the Catholic Church. He is known as the 'Angelic Doctor' and is considered one of the greatest theologians and philosophers in the history of the Church. St. Thomas wrote extensively on theology, philosophy, and the relationship between faith and reason, and his works continue to be studied and respected today. He is particularly famous for his 'Summa Theologica,' a comprehensive work on theology that has influenced Catholic thought for centuries. St. Thomas is the patron saint of students, teachers, and universities, and his feast day is celebrated on January 28th. He is also known for his deep devotion to the Eucharist and his commitment to using reason to understand and explain the truths of the faith.

The Catholic Church recognizes St. Thomas as a Doctor of the Church and as someone who understood the importance of using reason to explore and understand the truths of faith. The Church teaches that St. Thomas' work shows us that faith and reason are not opposed to each other, but are complementary ways of understanding God's truth. St. Thomas' life and work demonstrate that we can use our God-given reason to explore the mysteries of faith and to grow in our understanding of God and His creation.

Why Pray to St. Thomas Aquinas for Wisdom and Knowledge?

Praying to St. Thomas Aquinas for wisdom and knowledge is particularly powerful for several reasons. First, St. Thomas was one of the greatest minds in the history of the Church, and his intercession can help us develop the intellectual gifts and understanding we need to grow in wisdom and knowledge. When we pray to him for wisdom and knowledge, we're asking for the help of someone who truly understood the relationship between faith and reason and who used his intellectual gifts to serve God and the Church. Second, St. Thomas' intercession has been associated with many miracles and blessings related to learning and understanding, giving hope to those who are seeking wisdom and knowledge.

Third, St. Thomas is the patron saint of students and teachers, making him an ideal intercessor for anyone who is pursuing education or who wants to grow in understanding. Fourth, praying to St. Thomas helps us remember that wisdom and knowledge are gifts from God that should be used to serve Him and others. Most importantly, praying to St. Thomas helps us develop a deeper understanding of the relationship between faith and reason and grow in our appreciation for the intellectual gifts that God has given us.

How to Pray This Novena

To pray this novena to St. Thomas Aquinas for wisdom and knowledge effectively, begin by creating a sacred space where you can pray without distractions. You might want to have an image or statue of St. Thomas nearby, or spend time in a library or study area as you pray. Take a few moments to quiet your mind and focus on St. Thomas' presence and his desire to help you grow in wisdom and knowledge.

Start each day of the novena with the opening prayer, then pray the specific prayer for that day. Take time to reflect on the Scripture passage and how it relates to wisdom and knowledge. Be specific about what you're praying for, whether it's help with your studies, understanding of a particular subject, or general growth in wisdom and knowledge. Ask St. Thomas to intercede for you and to help you develop the intellectual gifts and understanding you need. End each prayer session with a few moments of silence, allowing St. Thomas to speak to your heart.

What to Pray For

During this novena, pray for all aspects of wisdom and knowledge. Pray for help with your studies, asking St. Thomas to help you understand difficult concepts and to develop good study habits. Pray for the ability to use your knowledge wisely, asking him to help you apply what you learn to serve God and others. Pray for the grace to see the relationship between faith and reason, asking St. Thomas to help you understand how your studies can deepen your faith and your faith can guide your studies.

Pray for the humility to recognize that all knowledge comes from God, asking St. Thomas to help you remain grateful for the intellectual gifts you have been given. Pray for the wisdom to use your knowledge for the common good, asking St. Thomas to help you see how your learning can benefit others and contribute to the building of God's kingdom. Remember to also pray for other students and teachers, asking St. Thomas to help them grow in wisdom and knowledge as well.

Frequency of Prayer

This novena is designed to be prayed for nine consecutive days, as the word 'novena' comes from the Latin word for 'nine.' The nine-day format allows for a focused period of prayer that can bring about significant spiritual breakthroughs for wisdom and knowledge. Many people find it helpful to pray the novena at the same time each day, creating a consistent routine that can help maintain focus and commitment to learning and growth.

It's important to be consistent with the novena, but don't be discouraged if you miss a day. Simply continue where you left off, or start over if you feel called to do so. The key is to maintain a spirit of prayer and openness to St. Thomas' intercession for wisdom and knowledge. You may also want to journal your experiences during the novena, noting any insights or improvements in your understanding.

Overcoming Learning Challenges

Many people face challenges in their pursuit of wisdom and knowledge, such as difficulty understanding certain subjects, lack of motivation, or feeling overwhelmed by the amount of information to learn. These challenges are normal and don't mean that you can't grow in wisdom and knowledge or that you're not intelligent. St. Thomas' intercession can help you overcome these obstacles and develop the understanding and skills you need.

When you're facing challenges in your learning, bring these challenges to St. Thomas in prayer. Ask him to help you understand what might be preventing your growth in wisdom and to give you the patience and perseverance you need to overcome these obstacles. Remember that St. Thomas faced many challenges in his own studies and that he can help you navigate the difficulties you're experiencing.

The Power of Thomistic Wisdom

One of the most important aspects of this novena is that it introduces you to Thomistic wisdom, which emphasizes the harmony between faith and reason and the importance of using our intellectual gifts to serve God and others. When you pray to St. Thomas for wisdom and knowledge, you're not just asking for help with your studies - you're learning a way of thinking that can transform your understanding of faith, reason, and the world around you.

Thomistic wisdom is particularly powerful because it helps us see that faith and reason are not opposed to each other, but are complementary ways of understanding God's truth. When you pray to St. Thomas for wisdom and knowledge, you're opening yourself to a tradition of thought that can bring greater clarity and understanding to your studies and your faith. Trust that St. Thomas wants to help you grow in wisdom and knowledge and that he will guide you in developing your intellectual gifts if you ask him.
