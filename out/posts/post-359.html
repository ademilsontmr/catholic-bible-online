Biblical Foundation of the Presentation

The biblical foundation of the Presentation is found in the Gospel of Luke, which tells us about Mary and Joseph bringing the infant Jesus to the Temple in Jerusalem. According to Jewish law, every firstborn male was to be consecrated to the Lord. This presentation fulfilled the requirements of the Law and symbolized Christ's complete dedication to God's will.

The Prophecy of Simeon

At the Presentation, the elderly Simeon, who had been promised by the Holy Spirit that he would see the Messiah before his death, recognized Jesus as the Christ. His prophecy about Jesus being a light for the Gentiles and the glory of Israel, and about Mary's heart being pierced by a sword, reveals the universal mission of Christ and the suffering that would accompany His mission.

The Prophecy of Anna

The prophetess Anna, who was also present in the Temple, recognized Jesus and spoke about Him to all who were looking for the redemption of Jerusalem. Her witness shows that the Holy Spirit was at work revealing Christ to those who were open to His guidance.

The Presentation and Our Consecration

The Presentation of the Lord invites us to reflect on our own consecration to God. Just as Christ was presented to God in the Temple, we are called to offer ourselves to God in our daily lives. The Presentation reminds us that we belong to God and are called to serve Him with our whole being.

Celebrating the Presentation Today

Today, the Presentation is celebrated on February 2nd, also known as Candlemas. This celebration includes the blessing of candles, which symbolize Christ as the Light of the World. The blessing of candles reminds us that we are called to carry Christ's light into the world through our words and actions.
