The Humility of Saint Joseph

2024-01-20

Introduction

Saint Joseph's humility is perhaps his most striking virtue. In a world that often celebrates self-promotion and recognition, Joseph's quiet acceptance of his role as foster father of Jesus stands as a powerful example of true humility.

The Humility of Acceptance

When the angel appeared to Joseph in a dream, revealing that Mary was with child by the Holy Spirit, Joseph's response was not one of pride or self-importance, but of humble acceptance. He recognized that he was being called to serve God's plan, not to be the center of attention.

Silent Service

Throughout the Gospels, Joseph is often described as acting rather than speaking. His humility is expressed through his actions - protecting his family, providing for their needs, and guiding them in faith. He never sought recognition or praise for his role.

The Humility of Obedience

Joseph's humility was most evident in his obedience to God's will. When called to flee to Egypt, he went without question. When told to return to Nazareth, he obeyed immediately. His humility allowed him to trust completely in God's plan.

Teaching Us Humility

Saint Joseph teaches us that true humility is not about thinking less of ourselves, but about thinking of ourselves less. It's about recognizing our role in God's plan and serving others with love and dedication.

Prayer

Saint Joseph, model of humility, teach us to accept our role in God's plan with quiet dignity and selfless service. Help us to find joy in serving others rather than seeking recognition for ourselves. Amen.
