The Liturgy of the Hours as the Church's Prayer

The Liturgy of the Hours is the official prayer of the Catholic Church, designed to sanctify the different hours of the day. Catholic tradition sees the Liturgy of the Hours as essential for understanding that prayer should be a regular part of our daily rhythm. The Liturgy of the Hours is not just for priests and religious but for all Catholics who want to pray with the Church. This understanding shows that the Liturgy of the Hours is not just a clerical practice but a way for all believers to participate in the Church's prayer. The Liturgy of the Hours helps Catholics sanctify their day and grow in their relationship with God.

The Structure of the Hours

The Liturgy of the Hours includes different prayer times throughout the day, each with its own character and purpose. Catholic tradition sees this structure as essential for understanding how the Liturgy of the Hours sanctifies the day. The main hours include Morning Prayer (Lauds), Evening Prayer (Vespers), and Night Prayer (Compline), with additional hours for different times of day. This structure shows that the Liturgy of the Hours is not just about praying but about structuring our day around prayer. The structure of the hours helps Catholics understand how to sanctify their time.
