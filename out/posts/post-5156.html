How Baptism Calls Us to Live as Disciples (Luke 9:23)

2025-01-27

The Call to Discipleship

Baptism is not just a sacrament of initiation but a call to live as disciples of Jesus Christ. This call to discipleship is clearly expressed in Luke 9:23, where Jesus tells His followers: "If any want to become my followers, let them deny themselves and take up their cross daily and follow me." Through Baptism, we are called to embrace this way of life and to follow Christ with our whole heart.

This call to discipleship is not optional or conditional but is the natural consequence of our baptismal identity. When we are baptized, we become followers of Christ, and this identity calls us to live according to His teachings and example. Discipleship is not just about believing in Christ but about following Him in our daily lives.

Denying Ourselves

The first aspect of discipleship that Baptism calls us to is self-denial. This does not mean hating ourselves or neglecting our legitimate needs, but rather putting God and others before our own desires and preferences. Self-denial involves recognizing that we are not the center of the universe and that our lives are meant to be lived for God and for others.

This self-denial is particularly important in today's world, which often emphasizes self-fulfillment and personal satisfaction above all else. As baptized Christians, we are called to a different way of life, one that prioritizes God's will and the good of others. This self-denial is not a burden but a path to true freedom and happiness.

Taking Up Our Cross

The second aspect of discipleship is taking up our cross daily. This involves accepting the difficulties and challenges that come with following Christ. These challenges might include persecution, misunderstanding, or simply the daily struggle to live according to Christian values in a secular world.

Taking up our cross also involves accepting the sufferings that come our way and offering them to God for the good of others. This does not mean seeking out suffering but rather accepting it when it comes and using it as an opportunity to grow in holiness and to help others. The cross is not just a symbol of suffering but also a sign of love and sacrifice.

Following Christ

The third aspect of discipleship is following Christ. This involves imitating His example and living according to His teachings. Jesus showed us how to love God and neighbor, how to serve others, and how to live with humility and compassion. As baptized Christians, we are called to follow His example in our daily lives.

Following Christ also involves being open to His guidance and direction. This means praying regularly, reading Scripture, and being attentive to the promptings of the Holy Spirit. It means being willing to change and grow as we learn more about Christ and His way of life.

The Daily Nature of Discipleship

Discipleship is not a one-time decision but a daily commitment. Each day presents us with new opportunities to follow Christ and to grow in our relationship with Him. This daily commitment involves making conscious choices to live according to our baptismal identity and to follow Christ's example.

This daily nature of discipleship reminds us that following Christ is not always easy and requires ongoing effort and commitment. It also reminds us that we are not alone in this journey but have the support of the Church community and the grace of God to help us persevere.

Discipleship and Community

Discipleship is not meant to be lived in isolation but in community with other believers. Through Baptism, we become members of the Church, the Body of Christ, and we are called to support and encourage one another in our journey of discipleship. This communal aspect of discipleship helps us to grow in faith and to remain faithful to our baptismal call.

This community provides us with the sacraments, teaching, and fellowship we need to grow as disciples. It also provides us with opportunities to serve others and to put our faith into practice. The Church community is essential for our growth as disciples of Christ.

Discipleship and Mission

As disciples of Christ, we are also called to be missionaries, sharing the Gospel with others and helping them to come to know Christ. This mission is not just for priests and religious but for all baptized Christians. We are called to be witnesses to Christ in our families, workplaces, and communities.

This mission involves both words and actions. We are called to proclaim the Gospel through our words, but also to live it through our actions. Our lives should be a witness to the love and mercy of Christ, showing others what it means to be a follower of Christ.

Discipleship and Perseverance

Discipleship requires perseverance and commitment. There will be times when following Christ is difficult, when we are tempted to give up or to compromise our values. However, as baptized Christians, we have the grace and strength we need to persevere in our commitment to follow Christ.

This perseverance is not just about avoiding sin but about continuing to grow in holiness and to deepen our relationship with Christ. It involves being willing to change and grow, to learn from our mistakes, and to continue striving to be more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Lifelong Journey

Baptism calls us to live as disciples of Jesus Christ, following Him in our daily lives and growing in our relationship with Him. This discipleship involves self-denial, taking up our cross, and following Christ's example. It is a daily commitment that requires perseverance and the support of the Christian community.

As we reflect on our call to discipleship through Baptism, let us recommit ourselves to following Christ and to living according to our baptismal identity. Let us remember that we are not alone in this journey but have the support of the Church and the grace of God to help us persevere. May our discipleship be a witness to Christ's love and a source of blessing for others.
