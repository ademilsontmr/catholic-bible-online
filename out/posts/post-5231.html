Building Your House on the Rock (Matthew 7:24–25)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Building on the Rock

The concept of building one's house on the rock in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Matthew 7:24–25: "Everyone then who hears these words of mine and does them will be like a wise man who built his house on the rock. And the rain fell, and the floods came, and the winds blew and beat on that house, but it did not fall, because it had been founded on the rock." This passage shows that when couples build their marriage on Christ's teachings, they create a foundation that can withstand any storm or difficulty.

This biblical foundation shows that building on the rock is not just about being religious but is about living according to God's will and following Christ's teachings in every aspect of married life. When couples build their marriage on the rock, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ. This foundation is not about being perfect but is about being faithful to God's plan for marriage.

Christ as the Foundation

In Catholic marriage, Christ serves as the rock upon which the relationship is built. Just as a house built on a solid rock foundation can withstand storms and floods, so too can a marriage built on Christ withstand the challenges and difficulties of life. This foundation is not just about believing in Christ but is about living according to His teachings and following His example.

When couples build their marriage on Christ, they are not just adding religion to their relationship but are creating a foundation that is strong and lasting. This foundation includes being faithful to God's plan for marriage, being willing to follow Christ's teachings, being willing to put God first in their relationship, and being willing to rely on God's strength and guidance. When couples build on this foundation, they are able to face any challenge with confidence and hope.

The Daily Practice of Building on the Rock

Building one's marriage on the rock is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying together as a couple, reading Scripture together, participating in the sacraments together, making decisions together in prayer, and living according to God's will. These daily practices help couples to keep Christ at the center of their relationship and to build a strong foundation.

This daily practice of building on the rock includes being willing to seek God's guidance in important decisions, being willing to pray for each other's needs, being willing to attend Mass together, being willing to participate in the sacrament of reconciliation together, and being willing to grow in faith together. When couples practice building on the rock in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Building on the Rock and Communication

Building one's marriage on the rock is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively with each other and with God, they are better able to understand each other's needs and to work together to meet those needs. Good communication helps couples to keep Christ at the center of their relationship and to rely on His guidance and strength.

Communication in building on the rock includes being willing to pray together about important decisions, being willing to share one's spiritual journey with one's spouse, being willing to discuss how God is working in one's life, and being willing to seek God's guidance together. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are strengthening their foundation on the rock.

Building on the Rock and Unity

Building one's marriage on the rock creates a unity that is stronger than the sum of its parts. When husband, wife, and Christ work together, they create a bond that is unbreakable. This unity is not just about being together but is about working together for a common purpose and goal.

This unity through building on the rock includes being united in faith, being united in prayer, being united in service to God and others, and being united in the mission of marriage. When couples are united in these ways, they are not just living together but are working together to build a strong and lasting marriage.

Building on the Rock and Strength

Building one's marriage on the rock provides strength and stability in times of difficulty. When couples face challenges, they are not alone but have Christ's strength and guidance to help them through. This strength is not just about being able to endure difficulties but is about being able to grow and learn from them.

This strength through building on the rock includes being able to face financial difficulties together, being able to face health problems together, being able to face family problems together, and being able to face spiritual difficulties together. When couples rely on the rock, they are able to face any challenge with confidence and hope.

Building on the Rock and Witness

Building one's marriage on the rock is a powerful witness to the world of God's love and strength. When couples live their marriage with Christ at the center, they show the world what a strong and lasting marriage looks like. This witness is particularly important in a world that often values temporary relationships over lasting commitments.

This witness through building on the rock includes showing the world that marriage is not just a human institution but is a divine institution that is protected and strengthened by God. When couples live their marriage with Christ at the center, they become a sign of hope and inspiration to others.

The Grace of Building on the Rock

Building one's marriage on the rock is not something that couples can do on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to build their marriage on Christ and to rely on His strength and guidance. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can build their marriage on the rock and create a foundation that is strong and lasting.

Conclusion: An Unshakeable Foundation

Building one's marriage on the rock is an unshakeable foundation that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples build their marriage on Christ, they create a foundation that is protected and strengthened by God's grace.

As we reflect on building one's marriage on the rock, let us remember that this foundation is not just a human creation but is a divine gift that requires God's grace. When we build our marriage on the rock, we are building a relationship that is strong, lasting, and filled with God's love.
