The Parable of the Sheep and the Goats – Matthew 25:31–46

Jesus' parable of the sheep and the goats, found in Matthew 25:31-46, provides profound insights into the final judgment and the importance of serving Christ through serving others. This parable reveals that when Christ returns in glory, he will separate people as a shepherd separates sheep from goats, based on how they have treated the least of his brothers and sisters. Understanding the parable of the sheep and the goats helps you recognize the importance of serving others and the connection between serving others and serving Christ. This parable serves as both a warning about the consequences of neglecting others and an encouragement to serve Christ through serving those in need.

The Son of Man in Glory

The parable begins with Jesus describing how the Son of Man will come in his glory with all the angels and sit on his glorious throne. This glorious return represents Christ's second coming and the establishment of his kingdom. Understanding the Son of Man in glory helps you recognize that Christ will return in power and glory to judge all people.

The Separation of the Nations

The parable describes how all the nations will be gathered before the Son of Man, and he will separate them as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats. This separation represents the final judgment when Christ will distinguish between those who have served him and those who have not. Understanding the separation of the nations helps you recognize that there will be a final judgment and separation of people.

The Sheep on the Right

The parable describes how the sheep will be placed on the right hand of the Son of Man. These sheep represent those who have served Christ by serving others, particularly the least of his brothers and sisters. Understanding the sheep on the right helps you recognize that serving others is a way of serving Christ.

The Goats on the Left

The parable describes how the goats will be placed on the left hand of the Son of Man. These goats represent those who have neglected to serve Christ by neglecting to serve others. Understanding the goats on the left helps you recognize the consequences of neglecting to serve others.

The King's Invitation to the Sheep

The parable describes how the King will invite the sheep to inherit the kingdom prepared for them since the creation of the world. This invitation represents the reward for those who have served Christ through serving others. Understanding the King's invitation to the sheep helps you recognize the reward for serving others.

The Sheep's Confusion

The parable describes how the sheep are confused and ask when they served the King. This confusion represents the fact that many people serve others without realizing they are serving Christ. Understanding the sheep's confusion helps you recognize that serving others is serving Christ, even when we don't realize it.

The King's Explanation

The parable describes how the King explains that whatever the sheep did for the least of his brothers and sisters, they did for him. This explanation reveals the connection between serving others and serving Christ. Understanding the King's explanation helps you recognize that serving others is a way of serving Christ.

The Goats' Condemnation

The parable describes how the goats are condemned to eternal punishment because they did not serve Christ by serving others. This condemnation represents the consequences of neglecting to serve others. Understanding the goats' condemnation helps you recognize the serious consequences of neglecting to serve others.

Lessons About Serving Christ

The parable teaches that we serve Christ by serving others, particularly those who are in need. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of serving others as a way of serving Christ.

Lessons About the Final Judgment

The parable reveals that the final judgment will be based on how we have treated others, particularly the least of Christ's brothers and sisters. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of how we treat others.

Conclusion: Serving Christ Through Others

The parable of the sheep and the goats reveals that we serve Christ by serving others, and that the final judgment will be based on how we have treated others. This parable challenges us to serve others as a way of serving Christ.
