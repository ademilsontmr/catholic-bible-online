Holiness Through Promoting Unity in the Church

2024-12-20

Introduction: Unity as Christ's Prayer

Holiness through promoting unity in the Church is about recognizing that unity is not just a nice ideal or a practical necessity, but the very prayer of Christ Himself and a fundamental characteristic of the Church that He established. Jesus prayed that His followers would be one, just as He and the Father are one, and this unity is not just about organizational structure or doctrinal agreement, but about the deep, spiritual unity that comes from our shared faith in Christ and our participation in His Body. When we work for unity in the Church, we're not just being good church members, but participating in Christ's own prayer and allowing Him to work through us to build up His Church.

In today's world, the Church faces many challenges to unity: disagreements over liturgy, differences in theological emphasis, cultural and generational divides, and various forms of division and conflict. However, these challenges are not insurmountable, and they provide opportunities for us to grow in holiness as we work to overcome them and to build greater unity. When we promote unity in the Church, we're not just avoiding conflict or pretending that differences don't exist, but working to find common ground and to build relationships based on our shared love for Christ and His Church.

How to Promote Unity

Promoting unity in the Church involves several important practices. First, it means focusing on what unites us rather than what divides us, recognizing that our shared faith in Christ is stronger than any differences we may have. Second, it means being respectful and charitable toward those with whom we disagree, avoiding personal attacks or harsh language. Third, it means being willing to compromise on non-essential matters while holding fast to the essential truths of our faith. Fourth, it means praying for unity and asking God to help us overcome our divisions. Fifth, it means being willing to admit when we're wrong and to seek reconciliation when we've caused division.

Conclusion: Unity as Witness

Promoting unity in the Church leads to holiness because it helps us to become more like Christ, who came to unite all people in Himself. When we work for unity, we're not just helping the Church, but allowing God to work in us and to make us more patient, more loving, more humble, and more holy. This unity becomes a powerful witness to the world, showing others that Christ's love is stronger than any human division and that His Church is truly one, holy, catholic, and apostolic.

Prayer for Church Unity

'Lord Jesus, help us to promote unity in Your Church, recognizing that You prayed that we would be one as You and the Father are one. Give us the grace to focus on what unites us and to work to overcome our divisions. May our work for unity lead us to greater holiness and help us to be a witness to Your love in the world. In Your name, Amen.'
