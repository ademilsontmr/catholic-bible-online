The Parable of the Shrewd Manager – Luke 16:1–9

Jesus' parable of the shrewd manager, found in Luke 16:1-9, provides profound insights into the importance of using worldly resources wisely for eternal purposes. This parable reveals that we should be as shrewd and strategic in our use of worldly resources for God's kingdom as worldly people are in their pursuit of earthly gain. Understanding the parable of the shrewd manager helps you recognize the importance of using your resources strategically for God's purposes and the value of being wise in your stewardship. This parable challenges us to examine how we are using our worldly resources and to be more strategic in our service to God.

The Manager's Crisis

The parable begins with a rich man who had a manager accused of wasting his possessions. The rich man tells the manager that he is going to fire him and asks for an accounting of his management. This crisis represents the reality that we will all be called to give an account of how we have used the resources that God has entrusted to us. Understanding the manager's crisis helps you recognize that you will be held accountable for how you use God's gifts and resources.

The Manager's Strategic Response

The parable describes how the manager, realizing he is about to lose his job, acts strategically to secure his future. He calls in his master's debtors and reduces their debts, making friends who will help him when he is unemployed. This strategic response represents the importance of being wise and strategic in our use of resources for God's purposes. Understanding the manager's strategic response helps you recognize the value of being shrewd and strategic in your service to God.

The Master's Commendation

The parable describes how the master commended the dishonest manager because he had acted shrewdly. This commendation represents the fact that even worldly people recognize the value of being strategic and shrewd in their dealings. Understanding the master's commendation helps you recognize that there is value in being wise and strategic in our approach to serving God.

Jesus' Application

Jesus concludes the parable by saying, 'For the people of this world are more shrewd in dealing with their own kind than are the people of the light. I tell you, use worldly wealth to gain friends for yourselves, so that when it is gone, you will be welcomed into eternal dwellings.' This application emphasizes that we should use worldly resources to build relationships and serve others, which will have eternal value. Understanding Jesus' application helps you recognize the importance of using your resources for eternal purposes.

Lessons About Strategic Stewardship

The parable teaches that we should be strategic and shrewd in our stewardship of God's resources. Just as the manager acted strategically to secure his future, we should be strategic in our use of resources for God's kingdom. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being wise and strategic in your stewardship.

Lessons About Using Resources for Relationships

The parable emphasizes the importance of using worldly resources to build relationships and serve others. The manager used his position to make friends who would help him in the future. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the value of using your resources to build relationships and serve others.

Lessons About Eternal Perspective

The parable teaches that we should use worldly resources with an eternal perspective. Jesus encourages us to use worldly wealth to gain friends who will welcome us into eternal dwellings. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of using your resources for eternal purposes rather than just earthly gain.

Lessons About Accountability

The parable emphasizes that we will be held accountable for how we use the resources that God has entrusted to us. The manager was called to give an account of his management, just as we will be called to give an account of our stewardship. Understanding these lessons helps you recognize the importance of being faithful stewards of God's gifts.

Conclusion: Strategic Stewardship for Eternal Purposes

The parable of the shrewd manager reveals the importance of being strategic and shrewd in our use of worldly resources for God's purposes. This parable challenges us to examine how we are using our resources and to be more strategic in our service to God. Understanding this parable helps you recognize the importance of using your resources wisely for eternal purposes and the value of being strategic in your stewardship. The parable serves as both a challenge to be more strategic in your stewardship and an encouragement to use your resources for eternal purposes.
