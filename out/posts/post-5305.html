Encouraging Each Other Toward Holiness (Hebrews 10:24)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Encouraging Toward Holiness

Encouraging each other toward holiness in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Hebrews 10:24: "And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works." This passage shows that encouraging each other toward holiness is not just a nice thing to do but is an essential part of the Christian life. When couples encourage each other toward holiness, they are not just being supportive but are actively participating in God's work of sanctification and helping each other grow in holiness.

This biblical foundation shows that encouraging each other toward holiness is not just about giving compliments but is about actively stirring up one another to love and good works. When couples encourage each other toward holiness, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This encouragement is not about being perfect but is about being willing to help each other grow in holiness.

Encouragement as a Path to Holiness

Encouraging each other toward holiness recognizes that encouragement is a powerful path toward holiness and sanctification. Just as the early Christians were called to stir up one another to love and good works, so too are married couples called to encourage each other toward holiness and good works. This encouragement is not about being critical but is about being supportive and encouraging.

When couples encourage each other toward holiness, they are not just being supportive but are actively helping each other grow in holiness and good works. They are able to support each other in their spiritual growth, to encourage each other to love and good works, to help each other overcome sin and temptation, to build each other up in faith and virtue, and to work together toward holiness and sanctification. This encouragement becomes a powerful path toward holiness.

The Daily Practice of Encouraging Toward Holiness

Practicing encouraging each other toward holiness is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes supporting each other in spiritual growth, encouraging each other to love and good works, helping each other overcome sin and temptation, building each other up in faith and virtue, and working together toward holiness and sanctification. These daily practices of encouraging toward holiness build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to support one's spouse in their spiritual growth and development, being willing to encourage one's spouse to love and good works, being willing to help one's spouse overcome sin and temptation, being willing to build one's spouse up in faith and virtue, and being willing to work together toward holiness and sanctification. When couples practice encouraging toward holiness in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and holy.

Encouraging Toward Holiness and Communication

Practicing encouraging each other toward holiness is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their desire to help each other grow in holiness, they are better able to encourage each other effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their spiritual growth, to share their struggles and victories, and to support each other in their journey toward holiness.

This communication for encouraging toward holiness includes being willing to discuss one's spiritual growth and development with one's spouse, being willing to share one's struggles and victories in the spiritual life, being willing to encourage one's spouse to love and good works, being willing to help one's spouse overcome sin and temptation, and being willing to work together toward holiness and sanctification. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on shared holiness.

Encouraging Toward Holiness and Growth

Practicing encouraging each other toward holiness is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples encourage each other toward holiness, they are growing in virtues like love, good works, and holiness. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through encouraging toward holiness includes growing in love as they learn to love each other more deeply, growing in good works as they learn to do good works together, growing in holiness as they learn to grow in holiness together, growing in encouragement as they learn to encourage each other effectively, and growing in sanctification as they learn to work toward sanctification together. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Holy Encouragement

Encouraging each other toward holiness in Catholic marriage is a holy encouragement that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples encourage each other toward holiness, they are not just being supportive but are actively participating in God's work of sanctification and helping each other grow in holiness.

As we reflect on encouraging each other toward holiness, let us remember that this encouragement is not just a human effort but is a divine work that requires God's grace. When we encourage each other toward holiness, we are building a marriage that is based on holiness, sanctification, and the grace of God.
