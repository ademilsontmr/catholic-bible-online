Introduction: Citizenship as Justice Foundation

When American Catholics respond to this call effectively, they're not just fulfilling civic obligations or creating religious atmospheres, but actively participating in God's work of building a more just and compassionate American society where all citizens can work together for the common good, regardless of their differences in background, beliefs, or political perspectives. Throughout American history, Catholic citizenship has played a vital role in American society, helping to guide various forms of social action and community building that have contributed to the moral and spiritual character of the nation. When American Catholics respond to this call effectively, they can help to transform American society and to create a more just and compassionate nation.

Fourth, it provides spiritual resources and practices that can help people to find strength and courage in their work for justice, and to persevere even when faced with various challenges and difficulties. Fifth, it bears witness to the truth that justice work is not just a political goal but a moral and spiritual calling that flows from our relationship with God.
