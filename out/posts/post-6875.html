How Young People Can Strive for Holiness Today

2024-12-19

Introduction: Holiness for a New Generation

Young people today face unique challenges and opportunities in their pursuit of holiness that previous generations never experienced. The digital age, changing social norms, and the pressures of modern life can make the path to sanctity seem overwhelming or even impossible. However, holiness is not only possible for young people today, but it's also desperately needed. The world needs young saints who can witness to Christ's love in the midst of modern challenges and who can show their peers that a life of faith is not only possible but deeply fulfilling.

The key for young people striving for holiness is to understand that holiness is not about being perfect or living in isolation from the modern world. It's about being authentically Catholic while fully engaging with the culture and technology of their time. Young saints like Carlo Acutis have shown that it's possible to be deeply holy while being fully present in the modern world, using technology and modern tools to serve God and others.

Building a Strong Prayer Life

The foundation of holiness for young people, as for everyone, is a strong and consistent prayer life. This doesn't mean spending hours in prayer each day, but rather developing a habit of regular communication with God. Young people can start with simple practices like morning and evening prayer, grace before meals, and brief moments of prayer throughout the day. The key is consistency rather than length.

Technology can actually help young people develop their prayer life. Prayer apps, digital Bibles, and online retreats can provide structure and guidance for prayer. Social media can be used to connect with other young Catholics and to find inspiration for prayer and spiritual growth. The important thing is to use technology intentionally for spiritual purposes rather than allowing it to become a distraction from prayer.

Living Virtue in Daily Life

Holiness for young people is built through the daily practice of virtue in ordinary situations. This means choosing to be kind when others are unkind, being honest even when it's difficult, practicing patience in frustrating situations, and showing courage in standing up for what's right. These daily choices may seem small, but they're the building blocks of holiness.

Young people can practice virtue in many everyday situations: being respectful to teachers and parents, treating classmates with kindness, being honest in their academic work, showing patience with siblings, and being generous with their time and resources. Each of these choices, made consistently over time, forms character and draws them closer to God.

Engaging with the Modern World

Young people striving for holiness should not withdraw from the modern world but engage with it thoughtfully and critically. This means being aware of current events, understanding the culture they live in, and being able to articulate Catholic teaching on important issues. It also means being creative in finding ways to share their faith with peers who may not share their beliefs.

This engagement requires both knowledge and wisdom. Young people need to understand their faith well enough to explain it to others, and they need the wisdom to know when and how to share it appropriately. They also need to be able to listen to others' perspectives with respect and charity, even when they disagree with them.

Conclusion: Saints for Our Time

Young people who strive for holiness today are not just preparing for heaven; they're making a difference in the world right now. Their example of faith, virtue, and love can inspire their peers and transform their families, schools, and communities. The world needs young saints who can show that holiness is not outdated or irrelevant, but is the path to true happiness and fulfillment.

This pursuit of holiness requires courage, perseverance, and the support of other believers. Young people should not try to go it alone but should seek out mentors, join youth groups, and build relationships with other young Catholics who share their desire for holiness. Together, they can support each other in their spiritual journey and become a powerful force for good in the world.

Prayer for Young People

'Lord Jesus, help young people to strive for holiness in the modern world. Give them the courage to live their faith authentically, the wisdom to navigate modern challenges, and the grace to become saints for our time. May their example inspire others to follow You. In Your name, Amen.'
