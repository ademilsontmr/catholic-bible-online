The Universal Call to Holiness in Vatican II and Beyond

2024-12-19

Introduction: A Revolutionary Teaching

The Second Vatican Council's teaching on the universal call to holiness was one of its most revolutionary and inspiring contributions to Catholic theology. For centuries, many Catholics had believed that holiness was reserved for priests, religious, or extraordinary individuals, while ordinary lay people were expected to be good but not necessarily holy. Vatican II changed this understanding by emphasizing that every baptized Christian, regardless of their state in life, is called to be a saint.

This teaching was not new to the Church - it had been present in Scripture and tradition from the beginning - but Vatican II brought it to the forefront and made it a central theme of its documents. The Council emphasized that holiness is not about escaping the world or living extraordinary lives, but about living ordinary lives with extraordinary love and dedication to God. This teaching has inspired countless Catholics to take their spiritual lives more seriously and to see holiness as an achievable goal.

The Biblical Foundation

The universal call to holiness is firmly rooted in Scripture, particularly in the New Testament. Jesus tells His disciples to 'be perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect' (Matthew 5:48), and St. Paul addresses his letters to 'the saints' in various cities, indicating that all Christians are called to sanctity. The First Letter of Peter reminds us that we are 'a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation' (1 Peter 2:9), emphasizing our call to holiness.

Conclusion: Holiness for Everyone

The universal call to holiness is one of the most inspiring and empowering teachings of the Catholic Church. It reminds us that holiness is not reserved for a select few but is the birthright and calling of every baptized Christian. This teaching gives us hope and confidence that we can become the saints God created us to be, regardless of our circumstances or limitations.

Prayer for Universal Holiness
