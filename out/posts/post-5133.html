Baptism as the Beginning of Christian Mission (Matthew 28:19)

2025-02-02

Introduction: The Beginning of Mission

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? This question addresses one of the most important aspects of the sacrament of baptism. Matthew 28:19 provides the biblical foundation for this understanding: 'Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.' This verse reveals that baptism is not just a personal sacrament but is intimately connected to the mission of the Church to make disciples of all nations.

The Catholic Church teaches that baptism is the sacrament of mission that initiates us into the Church's mission to proclaim the Gospel and to make disciples. Through baptism, we are not just saved individually but are incorporated into the missionary Church and given a share in Christ's mission to the world. This mission is not optional but is essential to our identity as baptized Christians.

The Context of Matthew 28:19

To understand how baptism is the beginning of Christian mission, we must examine the context of Matthew 28:19. This verse is part of the Great Commission, which is Jesus' final command to His disciples before ascending into heaven. The verse reads: 'Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.'

This verse shows that baptism is connected to the mission of making disciples. The command to 'make disciples' includes the command to baptize, indicating that baptism is an essential part of the missionary activity of the Church. This connection shows that baptism is not just about individual salvation but about participation in the Church's mission.

This context shows that baptism is essential for the mission of the Church and that all baptized Christians share in this mission.

The Missionary Church

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we become part of the missionary Church, which is the Church that Christ established to continue His mission in the world. This Church is not just a human organization but is the living Body of Christ, called to proclaim the Gospel and to make disciples.

Through baptism, we are incorporated into this missionary Church and given a share in its mission. This mission is not just for priests and religious but for all baptized Christians, who are called to be witnesses to Christ and to participate in the Church's missionary activity.

The missionary Church shows that baptism is not just about individual salvation but about participation in the Church's mission to the world.

The Call to Witness

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we are called to be witnesses to Christ. This witness is not just about words but about living our lives in such a way that others can see Christ in us.

Through baptism, we are given the grace to live as witnesses to Christ and to share the Good News of salvation with others. This witness involves both proclamation and service, as we are called to proclaim the Gospel and to serve others in love.

The call to witness shows that baptism is not just about personal salvation but about being a witness to Christ in the world.

The Mission of Evangelization

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we are called to participate in the mission of evangelization. This mission involves proclaiming the Gospel and inviting others to come to know Christ and to be baptized.

Through baptism, we are given the grace and the calling to share the Good News of salvation with others. This mission is not just for a select few but for all baptized Christians, who are called to be evangelizers in their own way, according to their state in life.

The mission of evangelization shows that baptism is not just about personal salvation but about sharing the Gospel with others.

The Mission of Service

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we are called to participate in the mission of service. This mission involves serving others in love and working for the transformation of the world according to God's will.

Through baptism, we are given the grace to serve others and to work for justice, peace, and the common good. This service is not just optional but is essential to our identity as baptized Christians, who are called to love and serve others as Christ did.

The mission of service shows that baptism is not just about personal salvation but about serving others in love.

The Mission of Unity

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we are called to participate in the mission of unity. This mission involves working for the unity of all Christians and for the unity of all people in Christ.

Through baptism, we are given the grace to work for unity and to overcome the divisions that exist in the world. This mission involves both prayer and action, as we pray for unity and work to overcome the barriers that separate people.

The mission of unity shows that baptism is not just about personal salvation but about working for the unity of all people in Christ.

The Mission of Transformation

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through baptism, we are called to participate in the mission of transformation. This mission involves working for the transformation of the world according to God's will and for the coming of His kingdom.

Through baptism, we are given the grace to work for the transformation of society and to bring about the values of the kingdom of God in the world. This mission involves both personal transformation and social transformation, as we work to become more like Christ and to transform the world around us.

The mission of transformation shows that baptism is not just about personal salvation but about working for the transformation of the world.

The Ongoing Mission

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? While baptism is the beginning of Christian mission, it is not the end. The mission that begins at baptism continues throughout our lives as we grow in our relationship with Christ and become more effective witnesses to Him.

This ongoing mission involves growing in our understanding of the faith, deepening our relationship with Christ, and becoming more effective witnesses to Him. Through prayer, the sacraments, and good works, we continue to grow in our missionary zeal and to become more effective witnesses to Christ.

The ongoing mission shows that baptism is not just about beginning the mission but about establishing the foundation for a lifelong mission.

Conclusion: The Missionary Call

How is baptism the beginning of Christian mission? Through the sacrament of baptism, we are incorporated into the missionary Church, called to be witnesses to Christ, and given a share in the Church's mission to proclaim the Gospel and to make disciples. This mission is not optional but is essential to our identity as baptized Christians.

As we reflect on Matthew 28:19 and the beginning of Christian mission, let us be grateful for the gift of baptism and the way it calls us to participate in the Church's mission. May we always remember that through baptism, we have been called to be witnesses to Christ and to participate in the Church's mission to the world.
