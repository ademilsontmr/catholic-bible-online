The Importance of Blessing Each Other (Numbers 6:24-26)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Blessing Each Other

The importance of blessing each other in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Numbers 6:24-26: "The Lord bless you and keep you; the Lord make his face to shine upon you and be gracious to you; the Lord lift up his countenance upon you and give you peace." This passage shows that blessing each other is not just a nice thing to do but is a powerful way to invoke God's grace and protection upon each other. When couples bless each other in their marriage, they are not just being kind but are actively participating in God's work of bestowing His grace and favor upon each other.

This biblical foundation shows that blessing each other in marriage is not just about giving compliments but is about actively invoking God's blessing, grace, and peace upon each other. When couples bless each other, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This blessing is not about being perfect but is about being willing to be channels of God's grace and love to each other.

Blessing as a Channel of Grace

Blessing each other in Catholic marriage serves as a channel through which God's grace and favor flow to each other. Just as the priestly blessing in Numbers was a way for God's grace to flow to His people, so too is blessing each other a way for God's grace to flow between husband and wife. This blessing is not about having special powers but is about being willing to be channels of God's love and grace.

When couples bless each other in their marriage, they are not just being kind but are actively being channels of God's grace and favor. They are willing to pray for God's blessing upon each other, to speak words of blessing and encouragement, to ask for God's protection and guidance for each other, to invoke God's peace and grace upon each other, and to be sources of God's love and favor to each other. This blessing becomes a powerful channel of grace in their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Blessing Each Other

Blessing each other is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes praying for God's blessing upon each other, speaking words of blessing and encouragement, asking for God's protection and guidance, invoking God's peace and grace, and being sources of God's love and favor. These daily practices of blessing each other build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to pray for God's blessing upon one's spouse, being willing to speak words of blessing and encouragement to one's spouse, being willing to ask for God's protection and guidance for one's spouse, being willing to invoke God's peace and grace upon one's spouse, and being willing to be a source of God's love and favor to one's spouse. When couples practice blessing each other in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and lasting.

Blessing Each Other and Communication

Blessing each other is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate blessings to each other, they are better able to be channels of God's grace effectively. Good communication helps couples to express their blessings, to pray for each other, and to be sources of encouragement and support.

This communication for blessing each other includes being willing to express blessings and words of encouragement to one's spouse, being willing to pray for God's blessing upon one's spouse, being willing to ask for God's protection and guidance for one's spouse, being willing to invoke God's peace and grace upon one's spouse, and being willing to be a source of God's love and favor to one's spouse. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are being channels of God's grace and blessing.

Blessing Each Other and Growth

Blessing each other is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples bless each other, they are growing in virtues like love, generosity, and grace. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through blessing each other includes growing in love as they learn to be channels of God's love, growing in generosity as they learn to give God's blessings freely, growing in grace as they learn to be gracious to each other, growing in faith as they learn to trust in God's blessing, and growing in holiness as they learn to be channels of God's grace. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Blessed Love

The importance of blessing each other in Catholic marriage is a blessed love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples bless each other, they are not just being kind but are actively being channels of God's grace and favor to each other.

As we reflect on the importance of blessing each other, let us remember that this blessing is not just a human kindness but is a divine grace that requires God's favor. When we bless each other, we are building a marriage that is based on love, grace, and the favor of God.
