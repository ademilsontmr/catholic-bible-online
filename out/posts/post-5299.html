The Call to Be Holy Together (1 Peter 1:15-16)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Being Holy Together

The call to be holy together in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in 1 Peter 1:15-16: "But as he who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, since it is written, 'You shall be holy, for I am holy.'" This passage shows that holiness is not just an individual calling but is a calling that married couples share together. When couples answer the call to be holy together, they are not just being religious but are actively participating in God's work of sanctification and growing in holiness as a couple.

This biblical foundation shows that being holy together is not just about individual holiness but is about growing in holiness as a couple and supporting each other in the journey toward sanctification. When couples answer the call to be holy together, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This call is not about being perfect but is about being willing to grow in holiness together.

Holiness as a Shared Journey

The call to be holy together in Catholic marriage recognizes that holiness is a shared journey that couples undertake together. Just as God calls each individual to be holy, so too does He call married couples to be holy together and to support each other in their journey toward sanctification. This holiness is not about being perfect but is about being willing to grow in holiness together.

When couples answer the call to be holy together, they are not just being religious but are actively participating in a shared journey toward holiness. They are able to support each other in their spiritual growth, to pray together for holiness, to encourage each other in virtuous living, to help each other overcome sin and temptation, and to grow in holiness together as a couple. This holiness becomes a powerful shared journey that strengthens their marriage.

The Daily Practice of Being Holy Together

Answering the call to be holy together is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes supporting each other in spiritual growth, praying together for holiness, encouraging each other in virtuous living, helping each other overcome sin and temptation, and growing in holiness together. These daily practices of being holy together build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to support one's spouse in their spiritual growth and development, being willing to pray together for holiness and sanctification, being willing to encourage one's spouse in virtuous living and good works, being willing to help one's spouse overcome sin and temptation, and being willing to grow in holiness together as a couple. When couples practice being holy together in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and holy.

Being Holy Together and Communication

Answering the call to be holy together is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their spiritual growth and their desire for holiness, they are better able to be holy together effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their spiritual needs, to share their struggles and victories, and to support each other in their journey toward holiness.

This communication for being holy together includes being willing to discuss one's spiritual growth and development with one's spouse, being willing to share one's struggles and victories in the spiritual life, being willing to pray together for holiness and sanctification, being willing to encourage each other in virtuous living, and being willing to support each other in overcoming sin and temptation. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on shared holiness.

Being Holy Together and Growth

Answering the call to be holy together is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples answer this call, they are growing in virtues like holiness, virtue, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through being holy together includes growing in holiness as they learn to be holy together, growing in virtue as they learn to live virtuous lives together, growing in love as they learn to love each other more holy, growing in sanctification as they learn to grow in sanctification together, and growing in witness as they learn to be witnesses to God's holiness. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Holy Love

The call to be holy together in Catholic marriage is a holy love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples answer the call to be holy together, they are not just being religious but are actively participating in God's work of sanctification and growing in holiness as a couple.

As we reflect on the call to be holy together, let us remember that this holiness is not just a human effort but is a divine calling that requires God's grace. When we answer the call to be holy together, we are building a marriage that is based on holiness, sanctification, and the grace of God.
