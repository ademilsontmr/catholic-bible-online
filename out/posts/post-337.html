The Feast of the Visitation

2025-02-02

Introduction: The Visitation

The Feast of the Visitation celebrates the meeting between Mary and Elizabeth (Lk 1:39–56). Mary, carrying Christ, goes “in haste”; Elizabeth, filled with the Spirit, blesses her; John leaps for joy; and Mary sings the Magnificat.

Grace in Motion

Mary shows that true contemplation overflows into mission: grace received becomes grace given. She is the first tabernacle and the first evangelist of the New Covenant.

Biblical Echoes

Like the ark of the covenant traveling to the hill country, Mary—the Ark of the New Covenant—brings the Lord to another home. Elizabeth proclaims Mary blessed for believing God’s promise.

The Magnificat

Mary’s hymn magnifies God’s mercy and reverses worldly pride. It teaches us to praise first, remember God’s deeds, and live for the poor and lowly.

Living the Feast

  • Go “in haste” toward those who need help.
  • Let your words carry Christ’s presence.
  • Pray the Magnificat daily.

Prayer

Lord Jesus, whom Mary carried to Elizabeth, visit our homes today. Make our words vessels of the Spirit and our steps swift in love. Amen.
