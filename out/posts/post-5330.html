Mutual Submission in Love (Ephesians 5:21)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Mutual Submission

Mutual submission in love in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Ephesians 5:21: "Submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ." This passage shows that mutual submission in love is not just a nice concept but is a fundamental principle of Christian marriage where both spouses submit to each other out of reverence for Christ. When couples understand mutual submission in love, they are not just being nice but are actively practicing a Christ-centered approach to marriage and creating an environment of mutual respect and harmony.

This biblical foundation shows that mutual submission in love is not just about one spouse submitting to the other but is about both spouses submitting to each other out of reverence for Christ. When couples understand this submission, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to submit to each other in love.

Mutual Submission as Christ-Centered Love

Mutual submission in love recognizes that submission is not just a human concept but is a Christ-centered approach to love and marriage. Just as both spouses are called to submit to each other out of reverence for Christ, so too is mutual submission a way of honoring Christ and His teachings about love. This submission is not about being dominated but is about being loving and respectful.

When couples understand mutual submission in love, they are not just being nice but are actively practicing a Christ-centered approach to marriage. They are able to submit to each other's needs and preferences out of love, to respect each other's opinions and decisions, to work together in making important decisions, to support each other's goals and aspirations, and to grow together in love and harmony. This submission becomes a powerful expression of Christ-centered love.

The Daily Practice of Mutual Submission

Understanding mutual submission in love is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes submitting to each other's needs and preferences out of love, respecting each other's opinions and decisions, working together in making important decisions, supporting each other's goals and aspirations, and growing together in love and harmony. These daily practices of mutual submission build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to submit to one's spouse's needs and preferences out of love and to put their needs before one's own, being willing to respect one's spouse's opinions and decisions and to value their input, being willing to work together in making important decisions and to seek consensus, being willing to support one's spouse's goals and aspirations and to encourage their dreams, and being willing to grow together in love and harmony and to work toward unity. When couples practice mutual submission in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and harmonious.

Mutual Submission and Communication

Understanding mutual submission in love is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their mutual submission and their respect for each other, they are better able to practice mutual submission effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their needs, to respect each other's opinions, and to work together in harmony.

This communication for mutual submission includes being willing to discuss one's needs and preferences with one's spouse and to listen to their needs, being willing to respect and value one's spouse's opinions and decisions, being willing to work together in making important decisions and to seek consensus, being willing to support and encourage one's spouse's goals and aspirations, and being willing to discuss how to grow together in love and harmony. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on mutual respect and harmony.

Mutual Submission and Growth

Understanding mutual submission in love is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples practice mutual submission, they are growing in virtues like humility, respect, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through mutual submission includes growing in humility as they learn to submit to each other out of love, growing in respect as they learn to respect each other's opinions and decisions, growing in love as they learn to love each other more deeply through mutual submission, growing in harmony as they learn to work together in harmony, and growing in holiness as they learn to practice Christ-centered love. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Submissive Love

Mutual submission in love in Catholic marriage is a submissive love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand mutual submission in love, they are not just being nice but are actively practicing a Christ-centered approach to marriage and creating an environment of mutual respect and harmony.

As we reflect on mutual submission in love, let us remember that this submission is not just a human concept but is a divine principle that requires God's grace. When we practice mutual submission, we are building a marriage that is based on respect, harmony, and the grace of God.
