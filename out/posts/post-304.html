The Sanctification of Ordinary Work

2024-01-24

Introduction: Finding Holiness in Daily Work

Saint Joseph's life as a carpenter demonstrates how ordinary work can become a path to holiness and sanctification. His example teaches us that holiness is not found only in extraordinary circumstances or special religious activities, but in the everyday tasks of daily life. When we approach our work with love, dedication, and a desire to serve God and others, even the most routine tasks become opportunities for spiritual growth.

The sanctification of ordinary work is a central theme in Catholic spirituality, particularly in the teachings of Saint Josemaría Escrivá and the spirituality of Opus Dei. This understanding recognizes that work is not a barrier to holiness, but a means of achieving it. When we offer our work to God and do it with love, we participate in His creative and redemptive work in the world.

Joseph's work as a carpenter was ordinary and unassuming, but it was essential for the well-being of his family and community. Through his skilled hands, he created objects that served practical needs and brought beauty and order to people's lives. This ordinary work became extraordinary when offered to God with love and dedication.

Understanding Work as a Divine Calling

The sanctification of ordinary work begins with understanding that our work is a divine calling. In Genesis, we see that work was part of God's original plan for human beings. Before the Fall, Adam was called to 'till and keep' the garden (Genesis 2:15), indicating that work was part of God's good creation, not a consequence of sin.

This understanding of work as a divine calling means that our daily labor is not just a means of earning money, but a way of participating in God's creative work. When we work with this understanding, even the most ordinary tasks take on new meaning and dignity. We are not just earning a living, but cooperating with God in the building of His Kingdom.

Saint Joseph's work as a carpenter exemplifies this understanding. His work was not just about providing for his family, but about serving God by serving others. Through his skilled hands, he created objects that made life easier and more comfortable for people in his community. This service to others was service to God.

The Virtues of Sanctified Work

Saint Joseph's example teaches us several virtues that characterize sanctified work. First, there is the virtue of diligence - the commitment to doing our work well, with attention to detail and care for quality. Joseph would have taken pride in his craftsmanship, ensuring that each piece he created was well-made and durable.

Second, there is the virtue of patience. Good work often requires patience - taking the time to do things right, waiting for materials to be ready, and persevering through difficulties and setbacks. This patience in work can help us develop patience in other areas of our lives, including our relationships and our spiritual journey.

Third, there is the virtue of humility. Joseph's work was humble and unassuming, but it was essential and valuable. He did not seek recognition or praise for his work, but simply did what was needed with quiet dignity and dedication. This humility reminds us that the value of work is not determined by its prestige or recognition, but by its contribution to the common good.

Work as Prayer and Worship

Saint Joseph's work as a carpenter demonstrates how work can become a form of prayer and worship. As he worked with wood, he would have been praying for his family, offering his work as a sacrifice to God, and asking for the grace to be a good husband and father. This integration of work and prayer is a model for all workers.

When we approach our work with this mindset, even the most routine tasks become opportunities for prayer. We can offer our work to God, ask for His guidance and grace, and use our work as a means of growing closer to Him. This understanding transforms ordinary labor into extraordinary worship.

This integration of work and prayer also means that our work should reflect our Christian values. We should be honest, fair, and compassionate in our dealings with others. We should treat our colleagues, customers, and clients with dignity and respect. And we should use our work as an opportunity to witness to our faith through our actions and attitudes.

The Sanctification of Daily Tasks

Saint Joseph's example teaches us that even the most ordinary daily tasks can be sanctified when offered to God. Whether we are washing dishes, filing papers, or building furniture, our work becomes holy when we do it with love and dedication. The key is not what we do, but how we do it and why we do it.

This understanding of the sanctification of daily tasks is particularly important in today's world, where many people feel that their work is meaningless or unimportant. Joseph's example reminds us that no honest work is beneath our dignity and that every task, when offered to God, can become a means of growing in holiness.

The sanctification of daily tasks also means that we should approach our work with gratitude. We should be thankful for the opportunity to work, for the skills and abilities God has given us, and for the people we serve through our work. This gratitude helps us see our work as a gift rather than a burden.

Work and the Common Good

Saint Joseph's work as a carpenter was oriented toward the common good. The objects he created served the needs of his community and contributed to the well-being of society. This orientation toward the common good is an essential aspect of sanctified work.

When we approach our work with the goal of serving the common good, even the most ordinary tasks take on new meaning and dignity. We are not just earning money, but contributing to the building of a better world. This understanding helps us find meaning and purpose in our work, regardless of its prestige or recognition.

This orientation toward the common good also means that we should be willing to make sacrifices for the sake of others. We should be willing to work hard, to take on difficult tasks, and to put the needs of others before our own comfort and convenience. This selflessness is a hallmark of sanctified work.

Practical Steps for Sanctifying Work

Saint Joseph's example provides us with practical steps for sanctifying our work. First, we can begin each day by offering our work to God. We can pray for the grace to do our work well and to serve others through it. This simple act of offering transforms our work into a form of prayer.

Second, we can approach our work with attention to detail and care for quality. We should take pride in doing our work well, not for our own glory, but for the glory of God and the service of others. This commitment to excellence reflects our understanding that our work is a gift from God.

Third, we can use our work as an opportunity to practice virtue. We can practice patience when things don't go as planned, humility when we make mistakes, and generosity when we help others. These virtues help us grow in holiness through our daily work.

Conclusion: Transforming Ordinary into Extraordinary

Saint Joseph's example teaches us that ordinary work can become extraordinary when offered to God with love and dedication. His life as a carpenter shows us that holiness is not found only in prayer and religious activities, but in the everyday tasks of daily life.

As we go about our daily work, we can remember Joseph's example and strive to approach our work with the same dignity and dedication that he showed. We can offer our work to God, see it as service to others, and use it as a means of growing in virtue and holiness.

Ultimately, the sanctification of ordinary work points us toward the deeper truth that our work is not separate from our spiritual life, but an integral part of it. When we understand this, our daily labor takes on new meaning and becomes a source of joy and fulfillment.

Prayer for the Sanctification of Work

'Saint Joseph, model of sanctified work, teach me to approach my daily tasks with the same dignity and dedication that you showed as a carpenter. Help me to see my work as a form of prayer and worship, and give me the grace to offer it to God with love and dedication. May my ordinary work become extraordinary service to You and to others. Help me to find holiness in the everyday tasks of daily life and to use my work as a means of growing closer to You. In Jesus' name, Amen.'
