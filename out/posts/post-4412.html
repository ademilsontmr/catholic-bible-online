The History of the Prayer to Saint Michael

The Prayer to Saint Michael was composed by Pope Leo XIII in 1886, after he had a vision of the devil attacking the Church. In this vision, Pope Leo saw Satan gathering his forces to destroy the Church, and he heard Saint Michael's voice saying that the Church would be protected.

In response to this vision, Pope Leo composed the Prayer to Saint Michael and ordered that it be prayed at the end of every Mass. This practice continued until 1964, when it was discontinued, but the prayer remains popular among Catholics today.

The Meaning of the Prayer

The prayer also asks for Saint Michael's help in casting Satan and the evil spirits into hell, demonstrating the power and authority that Saint Michael has over the forces of evil.

How to Pray the Prayer to Saint Michael

The Prayer to Saint Michael can be prayed at any time, but it is particularly effective when prayed in times of spiritual difficulty or when facing temptation. The prayer can be prayed alone or with others, and it can be repeated as often as needed.
