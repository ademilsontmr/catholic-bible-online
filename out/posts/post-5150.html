Baptism as a Daily Reminder of Our Christian Vows (Romans 6:11)

2025-01-27

The Vows of Baptism

Baptism is not just a one-time event but a lifelong commitment that we are called to remember and renew every day. Through Baptism, we make solemn vows to renounce sin and to follow Christ. These vows are not just words spoken at a ceremony but are promises that shape our entire lives. Saint Paul reminds us of this in Romans 6:11: "So you also must consider yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus."

Our baptismal vows include renouncing Satan and all his works, professing faith in the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, and committing to live as faithful disciples of Christ. These vows are not optional or conditional but are the foundation of our Christian life. Every day, we are called to remember these vows and to live according to them.

Daily Renewal of Our Vows

While we make our baptismal vows only once, we are called to renew them every day through our thoughts, words, and actions. This daily renewal is not about repeating the baptismal ceremony but about living according to the promises we made. Each day presents us with new opportunities to say "yes" to God and "no" to sin.

This daily renewal can take many forms. It might involve making the sign of the cross when we wake up, offering our day to God, or making a conscious decision to avoid sin and to do good. It might involve praying for the grace to live according to our baptismal promises or asking for forgiveness when we fail to do so.

Remembering Our Identity

Baptism serves as a daily reminder of who we are - children of God and followers of Christ. In a world that often tries to define us by our achievements, possessions, or social status, Baptism reminds us that our true identity is found in our relationship with God. We are not what we do or what we have, but who we are in Christ.

This reminder is particularly important when we face challenges or difficulties. When we are tempted to doubt our worth or to give up hope, our baptismal identity reminds us that we are loved and valued by God. When we are tempted to sin or to compromise our values, our baptismal vows remind us of our commitment to follow Christ.

The Call to Holiness

Our baptismal vows call us to a life of holiness, which means striving to be like Christ in our thoughts, words, and actions. This call to holiness is not about being perfect but about growing in love for God and neighbor. It involves developing virtues, avoiding sin, and serving others with love and compassion.

This call to holiness is ongoing and requires daily effort. Each day, we are called to make choices that reflect our baptismal commitment. We are called to be patient when we are frustrated, to be kind when we are tempted to be harsh, to be forgiving when we are hurt, and to be generous when we are tempted to be selfish.

The Power of Grace

While our baptismal vows call us to a high standard of living, we are not expected to fulfill them on our own. God gives us the grace we need to live according to our vows. This grace is available to us through prayer, the sacraments, and the Holy Spirit who dwells within us.

When we fail to live according to our baptismal vows, we can turn to God for forgiveness and strength. The sacrament of Reconciliation is particularly important in this regard, as it helps us to acknowledge our failures and to receive the grace to start fresh. God's mercy is always available to us, no matter how many times we fall.

The Witness of Our Lives

Our baptismal vows are not just personal commitments but are also meant to be witnessed to others. Through our lives, we are called to show others what it means to be a follower of Christ. Our actions, words, and attitudes should reflect our baptismal identity and commitment.

This witness is particularly important in today's world, where many people are searching for meaning and purpose. Through our lives, we can show others that following Christ brings joy, peace, and fulfillment. We can show them that being a Christian is not about following rules but about living in relationship with a loving God.

The Support of the Community

Living according to our baptismal vows is not meant to be a solitary endeavor. We are part of a community of believers who support and encourage one another. The Church provides us with the sacraments, teaching, and fellowship we need to remain faithful to our vows.

This communal support is evident in the baptismal rite, where the community commits to supporting the newly baptized in their faith journey. This support continues throughout our lives as we participate in the life of the Church and receive the encouragement and guidance of our fellow believers.

The Hope of Eternal Life

Our baptismal vows are not just about this life but also about eternal life. Through Baptism, we are given the hope of sharing in Christ's resurrection and living with God forever. This hope gives us the strength to persevere in our commitment to follow Christ, even when it's difficult.

This hope is not just a future reality but also affects how we live in the present. When we remember that we are called to eternal life, we are less likely to be consumed by the concerns of this world. We are more likely to focus on what is truly important - our relationship with God and our service to others.

Conclusion: A Lifelong Commitment

Our baptismal vows are a lifelong commitment that we are called to remember and renew every day. These vows remind us of who we are, call us to holiness, and give us the hope of eternal life. While living according to these vows is not always easy, we are not alone in this journey. God gives us the grace we need, and the Church provides us with the support and encouragement we need.

As we reflect on our baptismal vows, let us recommit ourselves to living according to them. Let us remember that these vows are not just words but are promises that shape our entire lives. May our daily remembrance of our baptismal vows help us to grow in holiness and to be faithful witnesses to Christ in the world.
