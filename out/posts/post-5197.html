The Call to Serve One Another in Marriage (Mark 10:45)

2025-01-27

Christ as the Model of Service

In Catholic marriage, couples are called to serve each other as Christ served the Church. As Jesus tells us in Mark 10:45: "For the Son of man also came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." This passage shows that service is not just a nice thing to do but is at the heart of what it means to be a disciple of Christ and to live a Christian marriage.

When couples serve each other in marriage, they are imitating Christ's self-sacrificing love for the Church. They are putting the needs of their spouse before their own needs and are willing to make sacrifices for the good of their relationship. This service is not about being a doormat but is about loving their spouse as Christ loves the Church.

Service as an Expression of Love

Service in marriage is not just about doing things for each other but is an expression of love. When we love someone, we naturally want to serve them and to put their needs before our own. This is the kind of love that Christ showed for the Church when He sacrificed Himself on the cross. Married couples are called to love each other with the same self-sacrificing love.

This service is expressed in many ways in daily married life - in the way we care for each other when we are sick, in the way we support each other's dreams and goals, in the way we help each other with daily tasks, and in the way we listen to each other and provide emotional support. When we serve each other, we are showing our spouse that we love them and that we are committed to their happiness and well-being.

The Daily Acts of Service

Most of the service that couples provide for each other in marriage consists of small, everyday acts that may seem insignificant but are actually very important. These include cooking meals, doing laundry, cleaning the house, running errands, caring for children, and providing emotional support. These acts of service may not seem glamorous, but they are essential for building a strong and lasting marriage.

These daily acts of service are not just about getting things done but are about showing love and care for each other. When couples serve each other in these small ways, they are building a foundation of love and trust that helps their marriage to flourish. These acts of service also help couples to grow in virtue and to become more like Christ.

Service and Communication

Service in marriage is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively about their needs, their desires, and their concerns, they are better able to serve each other in meaningful ways. Good communication helps couples to understand what their spouse needs and to provide the service that is most helpful and appreciated.

Communication also helps couples to express their gratitude for the service that they receive from each other. When couples communicate well, they are better able to appreciate each other's efforts and to express their love and gratitude. This communication helps to create an environment of love and appreciation where service can flourish.

Service and Sacrifice

Service in marriage often involves sacrifice. When we serve our spouse, we are often putting their needs before our own needs and making sacrifices for their good. This sacrifice is not always easy, but it is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When couples are willing to sacrifice for each other, they are showing each other that they love them and that they are committed to their happiness.

This sacrifice is not about being a martyr but is about loving our spouse as Christ loves the Church. When we sacrifice for our spouse, we are growing in virtue and becoming more like Christ. This sacrifice also helps us to understand better the love that God has for us and the sacrifice that Christ made for us on the cross.

Service and Growth

Service in marriage is also essential for personal and spiritual growth. When we serve our spouse, we are growing in virtues like patience, kindness, generosity, and self-control. These virtues help us to become better people and better spouses. They also help us to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth in virtue is essential for building a strong and lasting marriage. When couples grow in virtue, they are better able to love each other, to communicate effectively, to resolve conflicts, and to fulfill their vocation. This growth also helps them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

Service and the Mission of Marriage

Service in marriage is also essential for fulfilling the mission of marriage. When couples serve each other, they are better able to serve God and others through their relationship. They are also better able to be witnesses to God's love and service in the world.

This witness is particularly important in a world that often values self-interest over service to others. When couples serve each other with love and generosity, they become a sign of hope and inspiration to others. They show the world that service is not just about duty but is about love and that it brings joy and fulfillment to those who practice it.

The Grace of Service

Service in marriage is not something that couples can do on their own but requires God's grace. Through the sacrament of marriage, couples receive the grace they need to serve each other with love and generosity. This grace is not just given once at the wedding but is available throughout their married life.

Couples can access this grace through prayer, participation in the sacraments, and a commitment to living according to God's will. With God's help, any couple can learn to serve each other with love and generosity and to build a strong and lasting marriage based on service and love.

Conclusion: A Life of Service

Service in Catholic marriage is a way of life that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples serve each other with love and generosity, they are imitating Christ's self-sacrificing love and becoming better witnesses to His love in the world.

As we reflect on the call to serve one another in marriage, let us remember that service is not just about doing things for each other but is about loving each other as Christ loves the Church. When we serve each other, we are building a marriage that is based on love, sacrifice, and the grace of God.
