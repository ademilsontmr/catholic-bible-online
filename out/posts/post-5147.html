How Baptism Prepares Us for Confirmation (Acts 8:14–17)

2025-01-27

The Connection Between Baptism and Confirmation

Baptism and Confirmation are closely related sacraments that work together to bring us into full communion with Christ and His Church. Baptism is the foundation that prepares us for Confirmation, which strengthens and completes the grace we received in Baptism. This connection is evident in Scripture and in the Church's understanding of these sacraments.

In Acts 8:14-17, we see this connection clearly: "Now when the apostles at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had accepted the word of God, they sent Peter and John to them. The two went down and prayed for them that they might receive the Holy Spirit (for as yet the Spirit had not come upon any of them; they had only been baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus). Then Peter and John laid their hands on them, and they received the Holy Spirit." This passage shows that Baptism and the reception of the Holy Spirit (Confirmation) are distinct but related events.

Baptism as the Foundation

Baptism establishes the foundation for our Christian life by cleansing us of sin, making us members of the Church, and giving us the initial grace of the Holy Spirit. This foundation is essential for Confirmation, which builds upon and strengthens what was begun in Baptism. Without the foundation of Baptism, Confirmation would have nothing to build upon.

The grace we receive in Baptism includes the forgiveness of sins, new life in Christ, and the indwelling of the Holy Spirit. However, this initial gift of the Holy Spirit is meant to be strengthened and deepened through Confirmation. Baptism gives us the Holy Spirit as a seed, and Confirmation helps that seed to grow and flourish.

Confirmation as Strengthening

Confirmation is often called the sacrament of strengthening because it strengthens the grace we received in Baptism and gives us the courage and strength to live as faithful witnesses to Christ. While Baptism gives us the Holy Spirit, Confirmation gives us a special outpouring of the Holy Spirit's gifts and fruits.

This strengthening is particularly important for living the Christian life in a world that often opposes or ignores the Gospel. Confirmation gives us the courage to stand up for our faith, to share the Gospel with others, and to live according to Christian values even when it's difficult.

The Gifts of the Holy Spirit

Confirmation brings a special outpouring of the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit: wisdom, understanding, counsel, fortitude, knowledge, piety, and fear of the Lord. These gifts help us to live virtuously and to respond to the Holy Spirit's guidance in our daily lives.

These gifts build upon the foundation laid in Baptism and help us to grow in holiness and wisdom. They enable us to make good decisions, to understand God's will, and to live as faithful disciples of Christ. The gifts of the Holy Spirit are essential for the mission of the Church and for our personal growth in faith.

The Missionary Dimension

Confirmation also has a strong missionary dimension, preparing us to be witnesses to Christ in the world. While Baptism makes us members of the Church, Confirmation empowers us to be active participants in the Church's mission of evangelization. We are called to be witnesses to Christ not just in our words but also in our actions and way of life.

This missionary dimension is evident in the rite of Confirmation, which includes the anointing with chrism and the words "Be sealed with the Gift of the Holy Spirit." This sealing marks us as Christ's own and empowers us to bear witness to Him in the world.

Preparation for Confirmation

The preparation for Confirmation builds upon the foundation laid in Baptism. This preparation typically involves catechesis, prayer, and participation in the life of the Church. The candidate for Confirmation should have a good understanding of the faith and be living according to Christian values.

This preparation is particularly important for those who were baptized as infants, as it gives them the opportunity to make their own commitment to the faith and to take ownership of their baptismal promises. For those baptized as adults, the preparation for Confirmation may be more immediate but still requires proper formation and discernment.

The Communal Aspect

Both Baptism and Confirmation have a strong communal aspect. We are baptized into the Church community, and Confirmation strengthens our bonds with that community and empowers us to serve it. The Church community supports us in our preparation for Confirmation and celebrates with us when we receive the sacrament.

This communal aspect is evident in the rite of Confirmation, which is typically celebrated in the context of the Mass and involves the participation of the entire community. The community prays for the candidates and welcomes them as fully initiated members of the Church.

The Ongoing Journey

While Confirmation completes the sacraments of initiation, it is not the end of our spiritual journey but the beginning of a new phase. We are called to continue growing in faith, to use the gifts of the Holy Spirit for the good of others, and to be active participants in the Church's mission.

This ongoing journey involves regular participation in the sacraments, especially the Eucharist, ongoing formation in the faith, and active service in the Church and the world. The grace of Confirmation helps us to persevere in this journey and to remain faithful to our baptismal promises.

Conclusion: A Complete Initiation

Baptism and Confirmation work together to bring us into full communion with Christ and His Church. Baptism provides the foundation, and Confirmation builds upon and strengthens that foundation. Together, these sacraments prepare us for a life of faith, service, and witness to Christ.

As we reflect on the connection between Baptism and Confirmation, let us be grateful for both sacraments and strive to live according to the grace we have received. Let us also remember that these sacraments are not just for our own benefit but also empower us to serve others and to be witnesses to Christ in the world. May the grace of both Baptism and Confirmation continue to work in our lives, helping us to grow in holiness and to fulfill our mission as disciples of Christ.
