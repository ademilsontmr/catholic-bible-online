Processions as Public Witness of Faith

Processions on feast days are a beautiful tradition in Catholicism that serve as a public witness of our faith and a way of honoring God and the saints. Catholic tradition sees processions as essential for understanding that these public displays are not just about walking but about witnessing to our faith and honoring those we celebrate. Processions are not just about movement but about public witness and honor. This understanding shows that processions are not just about following traditions but about understanding public witness. Processions help believers understand public witness of faith.
