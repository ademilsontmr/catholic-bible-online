The Origin of the Prayer

The Prayer to Saint Michael the Archangel was composed by Pope Leo XIII in 1886, after he had a vision of the devil's attack on the Church. The pope was so moved by this vision that he immediately composed this prayer and ordered it to be recited after every Low Mass throughout the Church. This prayer has been used by Catholics worldwide for over a century and has proven to be a powerful spiritual weapon. Understanding the origin of this prayer helps you appreciate its significance and power.

This understanding helps you approach the prayer with reverence and confidence.

The Text of the Prayer

The traditional Prayer to Saint Michael reads: 'Saint Michael the Archangel, defend us in battle. Be our protection against the wickedness and snares of the devil. May God rebuke him, we humbly pray, and do thou, O Prince of the heavenly hosts, by the power of God, cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits who prowl about the world seeking the ruin of souls. Amen.' This prayer is simple but powerful, calling upon Saint Michael's protection and authority.
