Raising Children in the Faith (Deuteronomy 6:6-7)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Raising Children in the Faith

The practice of raising children in the faith in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Deuteronomy 6:6-7: "And these words that I command you today shall be on your heart. You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise." This passage shows that raising children in the faith is not just a nice thing to do but is a sacred duty that God has entrusted to parents. When couples raise their children in the faith, they are not just being good parents but are actively participating in God's work of passing on the faith to the next generation.

This biblical foundation shows that raising children in the faith is not just about taking children to church but is about diligently teaching them God's word and talking about it in all aspects of daily life. When couples raise their children in the faith, they are not just improving their family but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation as parents. This raising is not about being perfect parents but is about being willing to diligently teach and model the faith to their children.

Raising Children in the Faith as a Sacred Duty

Raising children in the faith in Catholic marriage recognizes that this is a sacred duty that God has entrusted to parents. Just as God commanded the Israelites to teach His words diligently to their children, so too are Catholic parents called to teach the faith diligently to their children. This raising is not about forcing religion on children but is about lovingly and diligently teaching them about God's love and His ways.

When couples raise their children in the faith, they are not just being good parents but are actively fulfilling the sacred duty that God has entrusted to them. They are willing to teach their children about God's love and His ways, to model the faith in their daily lives, to pray with their children, to read Scripture with their children, and to help their children grow in their relationship with God. This raising becomes a powerful ministry that they share together.

The Daily Practice of Raising Children in the Faith

Raising children in the faith is practiced in the small, everyday moments of family life. This includes teaching children about God's love and His ways, modeling the faith in daily life, praying with children, reading Scripture with children, and helping children grow in their relationship with God. These daily practices of raising children in the faith build a strong foundation for the family.

This daily practice includes being willing to teach children about God's love and His ways in age-appropriate ways, being willing to model the faith in daily life through words and actions, being willing to pray with children regularly, being willing to read Scripture with children, and being willing to help children grow in their relationship with God. When couples practice raising children in the faith in these small ways, they are building a family that is strong in faith.

Raising Children in the Faith and Communication

Raising children in the faith is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate effectively about how to raise their children in the faith, they are better able to fulfill this sacred duty effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their approach to faith formation, to share their spiritual goals for their children, and to work together to teach and model the faith.

This communication for raising children in the faith includes being willing to discuss their approach to faith formation for their children, being willing to share their spiritual goals and aspirations for their children, being willing to work together to teach and model the faith, being willing to discuss how to handle spiritual questions and challenges, and being willing to support each other in their role as faith educators. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a partnership in faith formation.

Raising Children in the Faith and Growth

Raising children in the faith is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples raise their children in the faith, they are growing in virtues like patience, wisdom, and love. These virtues help them to become better people and better parents. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through raising children in the faith includes growing in patience as they learn to teach and guide their children, growing in wisdom as they learn to answer spiritual questions, growing in love as they learn to love their children with God's love, growing in faith as they learn to trust in God's plan for their family, and growing in holiness as they learn to model the faith to their children. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their family but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Faithful Family

Raising children in the faith in Catholic marriage is a faithful family that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting family. When couples raise their children in the faith, they are not just being good parents but are actively fulfilling the sacred duty that God has entrusted to them.

As we reflect on raising children in the faith, let us remember that this raising is not just a human responsibility but is a divine duty that requires God's grace. When we raise our children in the faith, we are building a family that is based on love, faith, and the grace of God.
