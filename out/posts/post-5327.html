The Call to Be Fruitful in Love (Genesis 1:28)

2025-01-27

The Biblical Foundation of Being Fruitful in Love

The call to be fruitful in love in Catholic marriage is deeply rooted in Scripture, particularly in Genesis 1:28: "And God blessed them. And God said to them, 'Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth and subdue it.'" This passage shows that being fruitful in love is not just a nice concept but is God's original blessing and call for married couples. When couples understand the call to be fruitful in love, they are not just being romantic but are actively responding to God's call to be fruitful and to multiply His love in the world.

This biblical foundation shows that the call to be fruitful in love is not just about having children but is about actively being fruitful in all aspects of love and multiplying God's love in the world. When couples understand this call, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more faithful to their vocation. This understanding is not about being perfect but is about being willing to be fruitful in love.

Fruitfulness as God's Original Blessing

The call to be fruitful in love recognizes that fruitfulness is not just a human desire but is God's original blessing and call for married couples. Just as God blessed the first couple and called them to be fruitful and multiply, so too are married couples called to be fruitful in love and to multiply God's love in the world. This call is not about being pressured but is about being blessed and called.

When couples understand the call to be fruitful in love, they are not just being romantic but are actively responding to God's call to be fruitful. They are able to be fruitful in their love for each other, to be fruitful in their love for their children, to be fruitful in their love for others, to be fruitful in their service to God and His kingdom, and to be fruitful in their witness to God's love. This fruitfulness becomes a powerful response to God's call.

The Daily Practice of Being Fruitful in Love

Understanding the call to be fruitful in love is practiced in the small, everyday moments of married life. This includes being fruitful in love for each other, being fruitful in love for children, being fruitful in love for others, being fruitful in service to God and His kingdom, and being fruitful in witness to God's love. These daily practices of being fruitful in love build a strong foundation for the relationship.

This daily practice includes being willing to be fruitful in one's love for one's spouse and to grow deeper in love each day, being willing to be fruitful in one's love for one's children and to nurture them with love, being willing to be fruitful in one's love for others and to show God's love to those around one, being willing to be fruitful in one's service to God and His kingdom and to use one's gifts for His glory, and being willing to be fruitful in one's witness to God's love and to be an example of His love to others. When couples practice being fruitful in love in these small ways, they are building a marriage that is strong and purposeful.

Being Fruitful in Love and Communication

Understanding the call to be fruitful in love is closely connected to communication. When couples are able to communicate about their call to be fruitful in love, they are better able to respond to this call effectively. Good communication helps couples to discuss their fruitfulness, to plan ways to be fruitful, and to work together in being fruitful.

This communication for being fruitful in love includes being willing to discuss one's call to be fruitful in love with one's spouse, being willing to plan ways to be fruitful in love for each other and for others, being willing to discuss how to be fruitful in service to God and His kingdom, being willing to share one's desire to be fruitful in witness to God's love, and being willing to encourage each other in being fruitful in love. When couples communicate in these ways, they are not just sharing information but are building a relationship based on fruitfulness and purpose.

Being Fruitful in Love and Growth

Understanding the call to be fruitful in love is essential for personal and spiritual growth. When couples respond to the call to be fruitful in love, they are growing in virtues like love, service, and witness. These virtues help them to become better people and better spouses. They also help them to become better witnesses to God's love in the world.

This growth through being fruitful in love includes growing in love as they learn to be fruitful in their love for each other and others, growing in service as they learn to be fruitful in their service to God and His kingdom, growing in witness as they learn to be fruitful in their witness to God's love, growing in purpose as they learn to understand their call to be fruitful, and growing in holiness as they learn to respond to God's call to be fruitful. When couples grow in these virtues, they are not just improving their relationship but are growing in holiness and becoming more like Christ.

Conclusion: A Fruitful Love

The call to be fruitful in love in Catholic marriage is a fruitful love that helps couples to grow in holiness and to build a strong and lasting relationship. When couples understand the call to be fruitful in love, they are not just being romantic but are actively responding to God's call to be fruitful and to multiply His love in the world.

As we reflect on the call to be fruitful in love, let us remember that this fruitfulness is not just a human desire but is a divine blessing that requires God's grace. When we respond to this call, we are building a marriage that is based on fruitfulness, purpose, and the grace of God.
