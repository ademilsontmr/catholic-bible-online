The Meaning of the Baptismal Promises (Joshua 24:15 + Romans 6:11)

2025-02-02

Introduction: The Baptismal Promises

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? This question addresses one of the most important aspects of the sacrament of baptism. Joshua 24:15 and Romans 6:11 provide the biblical foundation for this understanding. Joshua 24:15 states: 'Now if you are unwilling to serve the Lord, choose this day whom you will serve, whether the gods your ancestors served in the region beyond the River or the gods of the Amorites in whose land you are living; but as for me and my household, we will serve the Lord.' Romans 6:11 teaches: 'So you also must consider yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus.' These verses reveal the profound meaning of the promises we make at baptism.

The Catholic Church teaches that the baptismal promises are not just ritual words but are serious commitments that we make to God and to the Church. Through these promises, we commit ourselves to serve the Lord, to reject sin and the devil, and to live as faithful disciples of Christ. These promises are the foundation of our Christian life and guide us in our journey of faith.

The Context of Joshua 24:15

To understand the meaning of the baptismal promises, we must examine the context of Joshua 24:15. Joshua is addressing the people of Israel and calling them to make a choice about whom they will serve. The verse reads: 'Now if you are unwilling to serve the Lord, choose this day whom you will serve, whether the gods your ancestors served in the region beyond the River or the gods of the Amorites in whose land you are living; but as for me and my household, we will serve the Lord.'

This verse shows that serving the Lord is a choice that we must make, and that this choice involves rejecting other gods and committing ourselves to serve the Lord alone. This choice is not just a one-time decision but is a commitment that affects every aspect of our lives. The baptismal promises are our way of making this choice and committing ourselves to serve the Lord.

This context shows that the baptismal promises involve a choice to serve the Lord and to reject all other gods.

The Context of Romans 6:11

To understand the meaning of the baptismal promises, we must also examine Romans 6:11. Saint Paul is writing about the way baptism changes our relationship with sin. The verse reads: 'So you also must consider yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus.'

This verse shows that through baptism, we are called to consider ourselves dead to sin and alive to God. This change in our relationship with sin is not just symbolic but is a real spiritual reality that affects how we live our lives. The baptismal promises are our way of committing ourselves to this new way of life.

This context shows that the baptismal promises involve a commitment to live as people who are dead to sin and alive to God.

The Choice to Serve the Lord

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? First, the baptismal promises involve a choice to serve the Lord. Through these promises, we choose to serve the Lord and to reject all other gods. This choice is not just a one-time decision but is a commitment that affects every aspect of our lives.

This choice involves putting God first in our lives and making Him the center of everything we do. Through the baptismal promises, we commit ourselves to serve the Lord with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength. This choice is essential for the Christian life and is expressed through the baptismal promises.

The choice to serve the Lord shows that the baptismal promises involve a fundamental commitment to God.

The Rejection of Sin and the Devil

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? Through the baptismal promises, we reject sin and the devil. This rejection is not just symbolic but is a real commitment to turn away from evil and to live according to God's will.

This rejection involves saying 'no' to sin and 'yes' to God. Through the baptismal promises, we commit ourselves to resist temptation and to live as people who are dead to sin and alive to God. This rejection is essential for the Christian life and is expressed through the baptismal promises.

The rejection of sin and the devil shows that the baptismal promises involve a commitment to holiness.

The Commitment to the Christian Way of Life

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? Through the baptismal promises, we commit ourselves to live the Christian way of life. This commitment involves following Christ's teachings and living according to the values of the Gospel.

This commitment involves love, service, sacrifice, and holiness. Through the baptismal promises, we commit ourselves to love God and our neighbor, to serve others, to make sacrifices for the sake of the Gospel, and to grow in holiness. This commitment is essential for the Christian life and is expressed through the baptismal promises.

The commitment to the Christian way of life shows that the baptismal promises involve a commitment to follow Christ.

The Ongoing Commitment

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? While the baptismal promises are made at baptism, they are not just a one-time commitment but are promises that must be renewed throughout our lives. Through prayer, the sacraments, and good works, we continue to live according to these promises and to grow in our commitment to Christ.

This ongoing commitment involves constantly choosing to serve the Lord, to reject sin, and to live according to the Gospel. Through this ongoing commitment, we grow in our relationship with Christ and become more faithful disciples. This ongoing commitment is essential for the Christian life and flows from our baptismal promises.

The ongoing commitment shows that the baptismal promises are not just about the moment of baptism but about a lifelong commitment to Christ.

The Witness to Others

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? Through the baptismal promises, we commit ourselves to be witnesses to Christ in the world. This witness involves living according to our promises and showing others the truth of the Gospel through our lives.

This witness is not just for a select few but for all baptized Christians, who are called to be witnesses to Christ in their own way, according to their state in life. Through our witness, we help others to come to know Christ and to make their own commitment to serve the Lord.

The witness to others shows that the baptismal promises involve a commitment to evangelization.

Conclusion: The Meaning of the Promises

What is the meaning of the baptismal promises? Through the baptismal promises, we choose to serve the Lord, reject sin and the devil, commit ourselves to the Christian way of life, and become witnesses to Christ in the world. These promises are not just ritual words but are serious commitments that guide our entire Christian life.

As we reflect on Joshua 24:15 and Romans 6:11, let us be grateful for the gift of the baptismal promises and the way they guide our Christian life. May we always remember that through our baptismal promises, we have committed ourselves to serve the Lord and to live as faithful disciples of Christ.

Prayer for the Baptismal Promises

'Lord Jesus, thank You for the gift of the baptismal promises and the way they guide our Christian life. Help us to understand the profound meaning of these promises and to live according to them. May we always remember that through our baptismal promises, we have committed ourselves to serve You and to live as faithful disciples. Give us the grace to renew these promises daily and to be faithful witnesses to You in the world. In Your name we pray, Amen.'
